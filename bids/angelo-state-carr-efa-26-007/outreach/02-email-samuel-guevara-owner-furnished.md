Following up on the proposal preparation for the Carr EFA Dressing Room Renovation (Sol 26-007), I want to make sure we have the owner-furnished scope demarcation confirmed in writing before we close subcontractor quotes. The RFCSP narrative identifies several owner-furnished items, and we want to avoid both **double-pricing** items that aren't in our scope and **under-pricing** by missing items that ARE in our scope (like the door-side rough-ins for CBORD).

Could you confirm in writing that the following items are owner-furnished and **not** to be included in our proposal price:

1. **Fire suppression system.** Including sprinkler-head relocation per the new ceiling layout, FP main and branch modifications, FP shutdown coordination, and hydrostatic testing. We assume our scope is "provide access only" — please confirm.

3. **CBORD access-control devices themselves.** Readers, controllers, and electric strikes are owner-furnished and owner-installed by ASU's CBORD vendor. Our scope is the door hardware preparation (electric strike mortise in HM frame, low-voltage conduit + ring & string to plenum, 120V or PoE pathway at the reader location). Please confirm.

4. **Furniture, FF&E, lockers, costume storage.** Owner-furnished. Our scope is wall blocking where the owner needs to mount any heavy items. Please confirm — and let me know if any specific blocking locations should be included in the bid (vs left to a post-award field decision).

5. **Equipment install (any equipment installed in the dressing rooms post-construction).** Owner-furnished. Our scope is "provide finished, punched-out spaces ready for owner-furnished equipment install." Please confirm.

A bonus question that affects pricing materially:

6. **Sargent cylindrical locksets.** These are specified by name in the RFCSP — we read this as IN our scope (we supply and install the mechanical lockset; CBORD furnishes and installs the electric strike + reader). Please confirm we have read this correctly.
