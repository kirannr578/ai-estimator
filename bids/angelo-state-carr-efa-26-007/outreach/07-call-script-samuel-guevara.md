## Substance — the four questions in priority order

> "I sent you an email yesterday with these items but wanted to walk through them by phone in case it's faster. Four things in priority order:

### Question 1 — Pre-response meeting eligibility (the showstopper)

> "First — the pre-response meeting on May 20. We were unable to attend. Was that meeting **mandatory** for proposers, or was it highly recommended? And if firms that did not attend are still eligible, could you share the sign-in sheet, minutes, or any Q&A from the meeting so we can incorporate the clarifications that were issued at the conference?"

**If Samuel says MANDATORY:**

> "Understood — that's important for us to know before we sink more estimator hours. Is there any path to waiver? We saw the conference was published in the RFCSP and we apologize for missing it. If a waiver isn't available, we'll respect the rule and step out of this one — but if there's any procedure for asking, we'd want to follow it through."

**If Samuel says NOT mandatory:**

> "That's great to hear. Could you confirm that in a follow-up email so we have it on the record? And could you also send the minutes / Q&A / sign-in sheet from the meeting? We don't want to be at a disadvantage relative to the firms that did attend."

### Question 2 — Site walk

> "Second — given Carr EFA is an older performing-arts building, would it be possible to arrange a site walk of the dressing-room area before the June 5 proposal deadline? Even 30 minutes would help us finalize abatement and demolition assumptions. We can come any day, any time, including evenings or weekends if that helps."

**If yes:**

> "Excellent — what day and time works for you? I can have our estimator and superintendent on site on short notice."

**If no:**

> "Understood. Could we ask about the alternative — is there any existing-conditions documentation, photographs, or recent inspection reports for the dressing-room area that we could review remotely? Anything that lets us reduce the existing-conditions contingency would let us put a tighter price on the bid."

### Question 3 — Drawings + project manual

> "Third — could you point us to the portal location for the construction drawings and the full project manual / specifications book? > "Fourth — the RFCSP narrative lists technology systems, network cabling, equipment install, furniture, and fire suppression as owner-furnished. Could you confirm in writing — even just a reply to my email — that we should NOT price (a) sprinkler-head relocation or FP system modifications, (b) AV and data cabling pulls and terminations, (c) the CBORD access-control devices themselves, and (d) furniture and FF&E?
