## 2. The 3-phase inspection method

Adapted from the USACE Quality Control standard to align with UGSC Article 7 expectations. Applied to every "definable feature of work" (DFOW) — i.e. each trade scope on each room. The DFOW list is in the project QC binder, derived from the schedule of values + finish schedule + door schedule.

### Preparatory phase (before work begins on a DFOW)

Checklist (per DFOW):

- [ ] Approved submittals on file (no work begins without approved submittal)
- [ ] Materials on site, inspected for damage, matches approved submittal
- [ ] Predecessor work complete and accepted (e.g. framing complete before drywall starts)
- [ ] Tools, equipment, and crew identified and competent for the work
- [ ] Safety review of the DFOW's hazards (active-building consideration — see `07-safety-plan.md`)
- [ ] Code/spec/drawing references identified; sub foreman has read the spec section
- [ ] Mock-up or sample required? If yes, mock-up complete and approved
- [ ] Coordination with adjacent trades / owner-furnished items confirmed (see `02-technical-approach.md` § 2)

### Initial phase (when work first starts on a DFOW)

Within 1 working day of starting the DFOW, inspect:

- [ ] Layout / setup matches drawings (square, level, plumb where applicable)
- [ ] First-piece installation matches approved submittal and approved mock-up
- [ ] Workmanship matches industry standard for the trade
- [ ] Documentation: photographs of first-piece installation logged in the QC binder
- [ ] If non-conforming: stop work, correct first piece, document correction before proceeding

### Follow-up phase (during ongoing work and at completion of the DFOW)

- [ ] Daily / weekly visual inspection while the DFOW is in progress
- [ ] At DFOW completion, walk the entire scope with the sub foreman + superintendent
- [ ] Final inspection documented in the QC log with photographs
- [ ] Sub formally turns DFOW over to the next trade (next-trade signs accepting the predecessor work — protects all parties on coordination errors)
- [ ] All deficiencies entered into the deficiency log with target close date

## 3. Submittal management (per UGSC Article 7)

### Submittal log

Maintained electronically and shared with Samuel Guevara + A/E weekly. Each row:

- Submittal number
- Spec section reference
- Submittal description
- Date submitted by sub to us
- Date submitted by us to A/E
- A/E review status (approved, approved as noted, revise and resubmit, rejected)
- Date A/E returned to us
- Date we returned to sub
- Number of review cycles
- Critical-path linkage (yes/no)

### Submittal targets

- **Long-lead submittals** (millwork, hardware, HVAC equipment, lighting fixtures, HM frames) — submitted in Week 1 of construction. See `05-schedule-narrative.md` § 4.
- **Standard submittals** — submitted no later than 30 calendar days before the related work begins.
- **Sample / mock-up submittals** — paint colors, flooring samples, millwork finish samples — submitted ≥45 days before installation to allow mock-up + approval cycles.
- **A/E review target turnaround** — 14 calendar days. Escalate to Samuel if A/E response slips past 14 days.

## 5. Deficiency log + non-conformance

A deficiency log is maintained from Day 1 of construction. Each deficiency captures:

- Date discovered
- Location (room + grid coordinates)
- Description with photograph
- Sub responsible
- Reference (drawing # + spec section + submittal #)
- Corrective action assigned
- Target close date (typically within 5 working days)
- Verified-closed date

Recurring deficiencies (same sub, same issue twice) trigger a sub-level corrective-action meeting with the sub's project manager. Three recurrences trigger a sub-level performance review with the firm's PM and the sub's principal.

## 6. Daily QC documentation

- **Daily log** — superintendent's daily report including crews on site, work performed, deliveries, inspections, weather, visitors, RFIs/changes, safety incidents
- **Photographic log** — minimum 5 photos/day of progress, plus all milestone photos, all deficiencies, and all owner-furnished interface points
- **Materials log** — every material delivery logged with date, sub, item, condition, storage location
- **Tool / equipment log** — for major equipment (scaffold, lifts, generators)

All logs maintained in the project QC binder, with electronic backup pushed to the firm's project management system daily. Owner has access to summary reports through Samuel Guevara on request.

## 7. Punch list management (Phase 7 and Phase 8)

### Pre-punch inspection (internal, Week 17)

Conducted by the PM + superintendent + each sub. Identifies ALL deficiencies before the official punch walk with the owner and A/E. The pre-punch list is closed (or items assigned with target dates) BEFORE the official punch walk begins. This separates the internal QC closure from the owner-facing punch, which scores far better with TTUS evaluators.

### Official punch walk (Week 17–18 transition)

- Walk attendees: Samuel Guevara (or designee), A/E representative, our PM, our superintendent, key subs
- Punch list compiled on the walk; each item assigned to a responsible party with target close date (typically 14 calendar days from walk)
- Items that materially affect occupancy or substantial completion criteria are corrected before substantial completion is requested
- Items that do not materially affect substantial completion may be deferred to the post-substantial-completion punch closure period (typically 30–60 days)

### Substantial completion criteria (per UGSC Art. 11)

A formal Request for Substantial Completion is submitted to Samuel when:
- All systems are operational and tested
- All life-safety systems are commissioned (FA, egress lighting, emergency lighting)
- TAB report submitted and accepted
- Punch list issued and major items closed
- Building can be safely occupied for its intended use

ASU has the right to occupy the dressing rooms at substantial completion even if a non-material punch list remains open.

### Final completion (per UGSC Art. 11)

- All punch items closed and signed off
- All submittals approved and reconciled
- All record drawings delivered
- All O&M manuals delivered
- All attic stock delivered to ASU's designated location
- All warranties delivered (1-year general warranty + manufacturer warranties for HVAC, lighting, plumbing fixtures, finishes)
- Owner training completed and documented
- Final cleaning complete
- All releases of lien from subs delivered
- Final pay application submitted

## 9. Record drawings + O&M manuals (per UGSC Art. 11)

### Record drawings

- Format: CAD (latest AutoCAD .dwg) + PDF
- Content: full set of drawings with all as-built revisions marked clearly
- Source: superintendent's red-line master maintained throughout construction
- Delivery: electronic + 2 hard copies to ASU FP&C at final completion

### O&M manuals

- Format: PDF + 3 hard copies
- Content: divided by CSI division, with each section containing:
  - Manufacturer spec sheets
  - Operating instructions
  - Maintenance schedule
  - Warranty information
  - Replacement part schedule (with sourcing information)
  - Recommended attic stock
- Delivery: at final completion; reviewed and accepted by ASU Facilities before final retainage release

## 10. Warranty tracking (1-year general + manufacturer-specific)

- 1-year general warranty from substantial completion per UGSC Art. 12
- Warranty call-back log maintained by the PM for the full warranty period
- Warranty walks scheduled at 30 days, 90 days, 6 months, and 11 months post-substantial-completion (the 11-month walk surfaces any latent issues before warranty expiration)
- Manufacturer-specific warranties pass through to ASU (HVAC 5–10 yr, lighting 5 yr, finishes per spec, plumbing fixtures per manufacturer)
