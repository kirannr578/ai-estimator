## 1. Project understanding

The Carr Education and Fine Arts Building Dressing Room Renovation requires the complete demolition and replacement of all interior finishes and primary mechanical, plumbing, and electrical systems within the dressing-room footprint, together with the installation of new IT infrastructure pathway and full code-driven ADA compliance under 28 CFR 35.151 (alterations of a primary function area in a performing-arts venue). Specifically:

- **Demolition:** all interior wall and ceiling finishes, flooring, casework, existing HVAC ductwork and equipment serving the dressing rooms, plumbing fixtures and rough-in within the renovation footprint, electrical branch wiring and devices, lighting, and incidental fire-alarm devices in the affected ceilings.
- **New construction:** full HVAC system (air handler / VAV terminals, ductwork, diffusers, controls, dedicated dressing-room exhaust for hair/makeup chemical control), plumbing (ADA lavatories, water closets if scope includes private toilets, possible shower stalls in wet zones, supply/waste/vent rough-in), electrical (branch wiring, devices including dedicated 20A circuits at each makeup station, GFCI at all wet locations, theater-grade dimmable LED lighting, lighting controls, egress and emergency lighting, sub-panel if existing service is insufficient), low-voltage pathway (conduit + boxes + ring & string for the owner-furnished AV/data/CBORD cabling), and door hardware including the specified Sargent cylindrical locksets with CBORD-ready door prep (electric strike mortise + low-voltage conduit and ring & string to plenum + 120V or PoE pathway at each access-controlled door).
- **Finishes:** light-gauge metal-stud framing and Type-X gypsum board at new or modified partitions, restoration of any exposed brick wall finishes (clean, repoint, seal), acoustical ceiling tile, sheet vinyl or LVT flooring with heat-welded seams in dressing rooms and adjacent circulation, ceramic tile in any wet zones, resilient base, 2-coat institutional low-VOC paint, makeup vanities and mirror surrounds (theater-grade with LED-lit mirror frames), costume storage shelving where designated, and ADA-compliant room signage.
- **Code compliance:** full ADA alterations-standard pass on door clear width, hardware reach range, accessible route, mirror heights, lavatory rim and knee clearance, accessible signage, and (if applicable) accessible shower stalls.

## 3. Approach and phased work plan

The 124-day construction window is sequenced into seven phases. Each phase identifies its critical path, the EHS shutdown windows required, the long-lead items that gate the phase, and the owner-vendor handoff points.

### Phase 1 — Mobilization, protection, coordination (Weeks 1–2, 7/1–7/14)

- Site mobilization at the ASU-designated lay-down location; install full negative-air dust containment with HEPA filtration around the dressing-room footprint; install walk-off mats and protection of all adjacent occupied spaces (theater, classrooms, faculty offices).
- Hold kickoff meeting with Samuel Guevara (ASU FP&C), ASU EHS, and the A/E firm to establish the RFI cadence (target 5 business day response), submittal log, and EHS shutdown notification protocol (target 2 weeks lead time for any utility interruption).
- Hold dedicated CBORD coordination call with the ASU CBORD system administrator and our door hardware sub + electrical sub to confirm electric strike specifications, conduit pathway, J-box size, and 120V vs PoE power requirements at each access-controlled door.
- Confirm ASU EHS hazmat survey results (or coordinate immediate survey if not on file). If asbestos or lead is detected in VCT, mastic, pipe insulation, or fireproofing, transition to Phase 2 (Demo) only after abatement is complete or scheduled.
- Order all long-lead items: theater-grade dressing-room millwork (vanities + LED-lit mirror surrounds, 10–14 wk), Sargent cylindrical locksets with the specified cylinder series (8–14 wk), theater-grade dimmable LED light fixtures (6–8 wk), HM frames (8–10 wk), specified HVAC equipment (8–12 wk).

### Phase 2 — Selective demolition (Weeks 3–4, 7/15–7/28)

- Selective demolition of all interior finishes, casework, ceilings, and MEP terminations within the dressing-room footprint. Demo of HVAC ductwork, plumbing rough-ins, and electrical rough-ins is sequenced with the respective MEP subcontractors to avoid orphaned services in the building.
- After-hours premium work scheduled for any noisy demo during Carr EFA active hours; daytime demo only on confirmed dark-house days from the College of Arts and Humanities production calendar.
- Any differing site conditions discovered during demo (additional asbestos, undersized existing electrical service, damaged structural elements, hidden water-damage) are documented with photographs and immediate written notice to Samuel within 5 calendar days, well inside the UGSC Article 4 (differing site conditions) notice window.

### Phase 3 — MEP rough-in (Weeks 5–8, 7/29–8/25)

- HVAC ductwork from existing trunk to new diffusers and grilles; new air handler or VAV terminal install; dedicated dressing-room exhaust ductwork to roof or existing exhaust collector.
- Plumbing supply, waste, and vent rough-in coordinated with the existing risers; ADA-compliant fixture locations laid out per drawings.
- Electrical branch wiring including dedicated 20A circuits at each makeup station, GFCI protection at all wet locations, lighting controls infrastructure, sub-panel install if existing service is undersized, and all low-voltage pathway (conduit + ring & string) for owner-furnished AV, data, and CBORD cabling.
- Door prep at each access-controlled door: electric strike mortise in HM frame, electric hinge or low-voltage conduit pathway from frame to plenum, 120V or PoE pathway at reader location. Owner-furnished CBORD devices themselves are NOT installed in this phase — owner's vendor follows our punch closure.
- All in-wall inspections (rough-electric, rough-plumbing, framing, fire-stopping) requested with the AHJ before any wall closure.

### Phase 4 — In-wall and above-ceiling close-out (Weeks 9–11, 8/26–9/15)

- Light-gauge metal-stud framing for any new or modified partitions, with PT-plywood blocking installed for all wall-mounted accessories (mirrors, costume racks, ADA grab bars, CBORD reader backing).
- Fire-stopping at all through-penetrations per the listed UL system; insulation; air-sealing.
- Type-X GWB hung on both faces of partitions and on furring at exposed brick areas; moisture-resistant GWB in any wet zones; resilient channel below GWB ceilings in wet areas.
- Ceiling grid set per the RCP, with all in-grid coordination complete (light fixtures, diffusers, sprinkler heads — sprinkler heads installed by owner's FP contractor in coordination with us).
- Brick wall restoration (clean, repoint, seal) where exposed brick is the specified finish.

### Phase 5 — Finishes (Weeks 12–14, 9/16–10/06)

- Flooring install: heat-welded sheet vinyl in dressing-room interiors, LVT in adjacent circulation, ceramic tile in any wet zones with the appropriate waterproofing membrane.
- Resilient base around all room perimeters and casework toe-kicks.
- 2-coat institutional low-VOC paint on walls, ceilings (where GWB), and door frames.
- Finish carpentry: paint-grade MDF or wood trim at door openings and wall accents.
- Millwork install: makeup vanities, counters, mirror surrounds with LED-lit mirror frames (theater-grade), costume storage shelving.
- Acoustical ceiling tile drop-in.
- Door + frame + Sargent cylindrical hardware install. Hardware keying coordinated with the CBORD admin's master-key schedule.

### Phase 6 — MEP trim, accessories, fire alarm (Weeks 15–16, 10/07–10/20)

- Light fixture install (theater-grade dimmable LED at general areas, dedicated makeup-mirror lighting in the millwork, egress and emergency lighting per IBC).
- Diffuser and grille install at the ceiling grid.
- Plumbing fixture set (lavatories, water closets where applicable, shower stalls in wet zones).
- Electrical device install (receptacles, switches, GFCI, lighting control switches) and final terminations.
- Lighting controls programming (occupancy + manual override + dimming per ASU/TTUS standard).
- Fire-alarm device install (strobe, smoke detector) coordinated with ASU EHS shutdown windows.
- Specialties install (ADA signage with raised characters + Braille, toilet accessories, fire extinguishers + cabinets).

### Phase 7 — TAB, commissioning, punch, owner training, substantial completion (Weeks 17–18, 10/21–11/02)

- Independent test/adjust/balance agency performs the TAB report on the new HVAC system.
- Commissioning of lighting controls and HVAC controls integration into ASU's BMS.
- Owner training on all installed systems (HVAC controls, lighting controls, plumbing fixtures, hardware keying).
- Walk-through punch list with Samuel Guevara and the A/E firm; address all punch items.
- Substantial completion **internal target: 2026-10-20**, banking 13 days of float against the contract substantial-completion date of **2026-11-02** and the $250/day liquidated damages trigger.

Phases 8 (close-out: record drawings, O&M manuals, attic stock, final cleaning, final pay app) runs in weeks 19–22 (11/03–11/30), nominally outside the 124-day window but completed before the final retainage release per UGSC Article 10.

## 4. Coordination with active performing-arts building

Carr EFA hosts theater, dance, music classes and productions year-round. Our schedule is built around three constraints:

1. **No daytime noisy work during business hours during fall semester** (8 AM – 5 PM weekdays beginning late August through November). Demolition, MEP cutover, and any percussive or high-decibel work is scheduled either before the fall semester begins (Phases 1–3 fall in the early-summer window when Carr EFA is comparatively dark) or after-hours/weekends during the semester.
2. **No work during scheduled productions, dress rehearsals, or recitals** (typically 7 PM – 11 PM, with full dark-house requirements on production days). Our PM coordinates a written access calendar with the College of Arts and Humanities, updated weekly, with at least one week of advance notice for any production-day work.
3. **No interference with adjacent active spaces** at any time. Dust containment is negative-air HEPA-filtered, with daily containment-integrity inspection logged. Walk-off mats at all containment transitions. Sound-mitigating partitions during any non-silent work occurring during occupied hours.

We carry after-hours premium labor (1.3–1.5× day rate) on approximately 25% of demolition and MEP cutover work hours in our base pricing, calibrated to the expected access-window constraints. The exact percentage will be refined as soon as the production calendar is shared by Samuel Guevara.
