## D. Signing / sealing / delivery procedure

### Signing

- Attachment A — **blue ink, original signature**, signed by an officer of the firm with authority to bind (typically President or VP). Initials on every page that has an initial block.
- Bid bond — original, wet-signed by both surety officer (attorney-in-fact) AND firm officer; surety's seal embossed; surety's power of attorney attached.
- Insurance ACORDs — issued by the broker, dated within 30 days of submission, no white-out, no manual corrections.
- W-9 — signed within the current calendar year (not stale).
- CIQ — signed by the officer of the firm.
- HB 1295 — printed copy of the e-filed certificate from the TX Ethics Commission portal (`https://www.ethics.state.tx.us/whatsnew/elf_info_form1295.htm`); the certificate is "certified" only when bearing the EC's certificate number assignment.

### Sealing

- Each package sealed in its own envelope (proposal = one envelope; HSP = separate envelope).
- Exterior labeling per RFCSP § Submission Instructions, typically:
  ```
  PROPOSAL — Carr EFA Dressing Room Renovation
  Solicitation 26-007
  Due: 2026-06-05, 2:00 PM CST
  From: [Firm legal name]
  [Firm address]
  ```
- Tamper-evident seal (signed and dated across the flap).

### Delivery

- **Hand-deliver preferred** for proposal — courier risk on TTUS submissions is real (lost packages = automatically late = automatic rejection).
- Arrive **≥30 minutes before** the 2:00 PM CST deadline on Friday.
- Obtain a **dated and timestamped receipt** from the ASU FP&C clerk.
- If unable to hand-deliver: use **next-day, signature-required courier** (FedEx Priority Overnight or UPS Next Day Air Saver) scheduled for **Thursday 6/4 delivery**, with Saturday delivery backup. NEVER ship for Friday-AM delivery on the day of the deadline — too much weather/route risk.

> If courier service has a delay: by 12:00 PM CST on the deadline day, call Samuel Guevara to notify; this preserves the right to argue "good faith effort to submit timely" but does NOT relieve the deadline. TTUS practice is strict — 2:01 PM = late. There is no grace period.

### Public opening

- TTUS pattern: public opening of base proposal prices **immediately after** the 2:00 PM CST deadline.
- Send a representative to attend if possible. Useful for competitive intelligence (who else bid, at what envelope).
- Public opening reads names + base bid amounts only — qualifications, references, schedules are not read.
