## 1. HSP form structure (TX standard HSP form per 34 TAC §20.285)

The State of Texas HSP form has 4 main sections. Each is filled per the guidance below.

| Section | Title | What it covers | Page count typical |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Respondent information | Firm name, address, contacts, HUB status | 1 |
| 2 | Subcontracting intentions | Per-subcontract: vendor, scope, $ value, HUB status | Multiple (1 per subcontract) |
| 3 | Self-performing justification | What the firm self-performs and why | 1 |
| 4 | Affirmation + GFE documentation | Compliance affirmations + GFE attachments | 1 + GFE binder |

The form is fillable on the TX Comptroller's website at `https://comptroller.texas.gov/purchasing/vendor/hub/forms.php` (the "HSP Form" link); use the most recent version (currently the 06/2024 revision per Comptroller's posting). The PDF in Attachment D is the same form, agency-customized for ASU/TTUS.

## 4. Section 3 — Self-performing justification

The form has a free-text block (typically 1 page) for justifying any work the firm intends to self-perform. Draft text:

```
SELF-PERFORMING JUSTIFICATION

[Firm name] intends to self-perform the following scopes of work on the Carr EFA
Dressing Room Renovation, with the justification stated for each:

1. Project management, supervision, and general conditions (Div 01)
   Estimated value: $60,000 (12% of contract)
   Justification: In-house project management and superintendence are the firm's core
   capability. Direct accountability for schedule, cost control, and quality control to
   ASU FP&C requires our personnel to lead day-to-day field operations. Subcontracting
   project management would dilute accountability and increase coordination overhead
   to the detriment of project outcomes.

2. Field engineering, layout, submittal management, and RFI coordination (Div 01)
   Estimated value: $15,000 (3% of contract)
   Justification: Integral to project management; not commercially available as a
   stand-alone subcontracted service for a project of this size.

3. Project closeout activities — punch list management, record drawings assembly,
   O&M manual compilation, owner training coordination, attic stock distribution
   Estimated value: $10,000 (2% of contract)
   Justification: In-house close-out specialist is faster and more accurate than
   subbing; close-out quality directly affects final retainage release and warranty
   period customer satisfaction.

Total self-performed: ~$85,000 (~17% of contract value)

The remaining ~83% of the contract value, approximately $400,000, is subcontracted
across the trades listed in Section 2 above.

The HUB participation goal of 30% of subcontracted dollars (~$120,000) is calculated
against the subcontracted $400,000 base, not the $485,000 total contract value, in
accordance with 34 TAC §20.285 and TTUS HSP practice.
```

## 5. Section 4 — Affirmation + GFE documentation

### Block A — Affirmation (sign each)

The form has typically 3–5 affirmations the officer signs:

| Affirmation | What it confirms |
|---|---|
| HSP completeness | The HSP is complete and accurate to the best of the respondent's knowledge |
| Good-faith effort | The respondent has made a good-faith effort to subcontract to HUB-certified firms |
| Revised HSP commitment | If subcontracting scope changes post-award, the respondent will submit a revised HSP to TTUS HUB Operations for approval before engaging the new sub |
| Reporting commitment | The respondent will file monthly Progress Assessment Reports (PARs) with each pay application post-award |
| Documentation retention | The respondent will retain all HSP-related documentation (vendor outreach, sub-quotes, GFE binder) for the contract retention period |

### Block B — GFE documentation (the make-or-break)

For every trade where the HUB allocation is light or where no HUB sub was selected, the GFE binder includes:

1. **Dated HUB outreach records** — ≥3 HUB subs solicited per trade, with method (email, fax, certified mail), date, recipient, and outcome (response received / no response). Per `../05-hsp-plan.md` § D, use the CMBL search starting points to identify candidate HUB subs.

2. **Advertisement records** — Ad placement in ≥2 TX HUB publications:
   - TX Comptroller's HUB News (`https://comptroller.texas.gov/purchasing/vendor/hub/news.php`)
   - Texas Construction News OR Hispanic Contractors Association of TX bid list OR NAWIC TX Chapter OR Greater San Antonio Black Chamber of Commerce publication
   Each ad copy + placement confirmation in the binder.

3. **Response log** — One row per HUB outreach with: date sent, sub name, contact, method, response, bid amount (if any), outcome (selected / not selected / no response).

4. **Non-selection reasons** — For each HUB sub that bid but was not selected, a written explanation: price differential >5%, schedule incompatibility, qualifications gap, bonding gap, or other documented reason.

5. **Trade-specific GFE narrative** — For each trade with light HUB allocation (masonry, theater-grade millwork, HVAC, hazmat per `../05-hsp-plan.md` § C), a 1-paragraph narrative explaining why the HUB share is limited. Examples:

   > "**Masonry / brick restoration:** Specialty masonry subcontractors for brick restoration (clean, repoint, seal) in west Texas are limited; HUB-certified specialty masonry firms in the San Angelo / Abilene / Midland radius are even thinner. We solicited 3 HUB-certified masonry firms (`[USER TO FILL: list with cert numbers]`); 1 responded with a bid 18% above the non-HUB market quote and 2 did not respond. We selected the non-HUB sub based on the >5% price differential rule."

6. **HUB outreach event attendance records** — If the firm attended any TTUS HUB Operations or TX Comptroller HUB outreach events, attach attendance records.

7. **HSP outreach packet template** — A copy of the template email/letter used in the HUB outreach (the template is in `../contacts.md` Draft #3).
