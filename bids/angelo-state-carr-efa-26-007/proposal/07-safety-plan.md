## 2. Site-specific hazard analysis (the Carr EFA-unique risks)

Standard 29 CFR 1926 hazards (fall protection, electrical, scaffolding, PPE, hazardous chemicals, hot work) are addressed by the firm's corporate safety program and are not re-litigated here. The project-specific hazards below DO require additional planning.

### Hazard 1 — Working in an active performing-arts building

**Risk:** Carr EFA hosts theater, dance, and music productions and classes during portions of the 7/1–11/2 construction window. Faculty, staff, students, and patrons share the building.

**Controls:**

- Daily safety toolbox includes an "active-building today?" check; high-noise / high-dust work permitted only during confirmed dark-house windows
- Permanent dust containment partitions with negative-air HEPA filtration; daily containment-integrity inspection
- Walk-off mats at every containment transition; HEPA vacuum of debris escape weekly
- Sound-mitigation partitions during any non-silent work that could occur during occupied hours
- Egress routes through and around the dressing-room footprint clearly signed; ASU EHS-approved temporary egress plan posted at each containment door
- Coordination call with the Carr EFA building manager + College of Arts and Humanities calendar contact at kickoff and weekly thereafter

### Hazard 2 — Hazardous-materials abatement (lead, asbestos, PCBs)

**Risk:** Carr EFA is older construction. Asbestos in VCT, floor mastic, pipe insulation, and fireproofing is realistic. Lead in pre-1978 paint layers is realistic.

**Controls:**

- ASU EHS hazmat survey obtained or coordinated pre-NTP (if not on file, an emergency survey is scheduled in Week 1; see R-03 in `../07-risk-register.md`)
- If positive: licensed TX-DSHS abatement sub mobilized; sub's site-specific abatement plan integrated with our overall safety plan; abatement area sealed with critical barriers + decontamination chamber + air monitoring; ASU EHS notified
- Air clearance testing by an independent agency before re-entry
- Worker exposure monitoring per 29 CFR 1926.1101 (asbestos) / 1926.62 (lead) as applicable
- Waste manifested and disposed at a TCEQ-permitted facility; manifest copies to ASU FP&C

### Hazard 3 — Fire-alarm shutdown coordination during demo + MEP work

**Risk:** Carr EFA's fire-alarm system must be active for occupied portions of the building. Demo and MEP work near devices requires planned shutdowns; uncoordinated work near an active FA device can trigger nuisance alarms (with evacuation impact) or worse, deactivate FA protection during a live event.

**Controls:**

- 2-week advance notice to ASU EHS for any FA shutdown per UGSC Art. 8
- Fire watch during any active shutdown — minimum 1 hour after work ends
- No work that could activate an FA device (welding, grinding, dusty work) within 10 feet of any active device without prior owner authorization + temporary device protection (per device manufacturer's bagging procedure)
- Daily start-of-shift verification that all FA devices in the work area are properly bagged or de-energized per the agreed plan

### Hazard 4 — Electrical lockout/tagout in a live institutional building

**Risk:** Carr EFA shares electrical service across multiple uses. Lockout/tagout (LOTO) of dressing-room circuits must NOT inadvertently de-energize adjacent active spaces.

**Controls:**

- LOTO program per 29 CFR 1926.417 + 1910.147; verified breaker IDs against single-line diagram pre-LOTO
- Coordinate any panel-level LOTO with ASU Facilities electrical staff
- All sub-electricians follow our LOTO procedure; we audit weekly
- Sub-panel work scheduled outside business hours where possible
- Temporary power for our work pulled from a dedicated panel; no shared circuits with active loads

### Hazard 5 — Dust and indoor air quality for performers and patrons

**Risk:** Performer health is acutely sensitive to dust and chemical fumes (paints, adhesives, sealers) — they sing and recite in the spaces. Standard institutional IAQ controls are insufficient.

**Controls:**

- Negative-air HEPA filtration on the dust containment, with daily filter inspection
- Low-VOC paints, adhesives, sealers, and finishes (specified in the finish schedule and in our sub-quotes)
- After-hours work for any high-VOC application (or shift to a different product if available)
- Air quality monitoring during paint and adhesive applications
- Notification to the Carr EFA building manager 48 hours before any work that may produce odor migration

### Hazard 6 — Confined-space / above-ceiling work

**Risk:** Above-ceiling MEP work above an occupied building requires fall-protection planning and protection of occupants below.

**Controls:**

- Fall protection per 29 CFR 1926.501 for any work at heights >6 feet
- Hard-barrier protection below all above-ceiling work (no work above active corridors during occupancy)
- All scaffolding and ladder use per OSHA standards; daily inspection
- Confined-space evaluation if above-ceiling space is determined to be a permit-required confined space (typical above-ceiling work is non-permit but evaluation is documented)

### Hazard 7 — After-hours work

**Risk:** Significant portions of demo and MEP cutover are after-hours. Reduced lighting, reduced ASU staffing, and fatigue are real factors.

**Controls:**

- Project-supplied task lighting in all work areas during after-hours operations
- Two-person minimum on site at all times during after-hours work
- Sign-in / sign-out at the campus security office for every after-hours entry
- Maximum 12-hour shifts; fatigue mitigation per the firm's overtime policy
- ASU Security informed of after-hours work and worker identifications
- Emergency contact tree posted at every entrance; ASU EHS on the contact tree

### Hazard 8 — Coordination with owner's vendors (FP, AV/IT, FF&E)

**Risk:** Owner's vendors will be in our work area at various points. Coordination errors lead to safety incidents (one trade's lockout sequence conflicts with another's energization; one trade's hot work conflicts with another's combustible storage).

**Controls:**

- Owner-vendor pre-work coordination meeting before each owner-vendor enters the work area
- Owner-vendor signs in to our project safety brief on first day
- No simultaneous work by owner's vendor and our crews in the same room without a documented dual-trade safety plan
- ASU EHS coordinates with both us and owner's vendors

## 5. PPE policy

Minimum PPE on site for all workers (per 29 CFR 1926.95 and firm policy):

- Hard hat (ANSI Z89.1 Type I Class E)
- Safety glasses (ANSI Z87.1)
- High-visibility vest (Class 2 minimum during after-hours work)
- Steel-toed boots (ASTM F2413)
- Hearing protection in 85 dBA+ areas (continuous monitoring of high-noise work)
- Fall protection harnesses and lanyards when working at heights >6 feet
- Respiratory protection (N95 minimum for dusty work; half-face P100 for abatement-adjacent work; full-face per abatement sub's plan in abatement areas)

Visitor PPE — hard hat, safety glasses, high-vis vest provided at the site entrance.

- TX Right-to-Know training current for all workers handling chemicals
