## 1. Form structure (TTUS Attachment A pattern)

A typical TTUS Attachment A has these sections in order:

1. **Cover sheet** — solicitation identification (project name, project number, owner, due date)
2. **Form of Proposal** — base bid lump sum + allowance line + unit prices + alternates
3. **Execution of Offer** — the binding signature block + acknowledgments
4. **Certifications** — anti-collusion, debarment, conflict of interest, resident bidder
5. **Acknowledgment of addenda** — numbered/signed list

### Section 2 — Form of Proposal (the priced offer)

This is the most-error-prone section. The form will have:

#### 2a. Base proposal line

- **What it asks for:** "Lump-sum base proposal price for the work as described in the RFCSP and the Construction Documents, exclusive of the cash/contingency allowance referenced below."
- **What to enter:** The base lump-sum price from `10-price-proposal.md` § B (the consolidated price). **Do NOT include the $25,000 allowance in this number.** **Do NOT include sales tax** — ASU is sales-tax exempt under Attachment E (TX Form 01-339).
- Format: Words AND figures (typical TX RFCSP requires both — "Three hundred eighty five thousand dollars ($385,000)"). If words and figures differ, the words govern.
- `[USER TO FILL: $___,___ after final takeoff; spell out in words]`

#### 2b. Cash / contingency allowance line (THE $25K LINE)

- **What it asks for:** "Cash/Contingency Allowance for unforeseen scope of work."
- **What to enter:** **$25,000.00** — exactly. This is a fixed dollar value set by the owner; you do NOT bid against it.
- **Critical:** Do NOT roll the allowance into the base price. Do NOT mark up the allowance. Do NOT submit a base bid that is "base + allowance." The form has the allowance as a separate line because it must be a separate line on the executed CSA.

#### 2c. Total proposal

- **What it asks for:** "Total Proposal (Base + Allowance)."
- **What to enter:** Base price + $25,000.00. This is the sum line; the owner reads this for budget reconciliation against the published budget.
- `[USER TO FILL: $___,___ + $25,000 = $___,___]`

#### 2d. Unit prices

- **What it asks for:** Per-unit prices for owner-directed change items typically requested by TTUS (additional doors, additional outlets, additional SF of flooring, additional fixture, etc.).
- The v3 extract only surfaced the $25K allowance as a unit-price entry — meaning the actual unit-price grid on the form is `[VERIFY ON PDF]`.
- **Filling guidance:** Once `10-price-proposal.md` § D (unit prices) is finalized, transfer each unit price to the form's unit-price grid. Unit prices set the ceiling for change-order pricing during construction — bid them at full retail (sub cost + your full GC markup), NOT shaved.

## 3. Critical fill rules

1. **Blue ink for ALL signatures.** Black ink = automatic rejection at TX state-level RFCSP intake.
2. **Initial every page with an initial block.** Missing initials = automatic rejection at clerk review.
3. **No white-out, no manual corrections.** If a field is wrong, reprint the page.
4. **Legal name consistency.** The firm's exact legal name must match across:
   - Attachment A (this form)
   - Bid bond
   - Insurance ACORDs
   - W-9
   - CIQ
   - HB 1295
   - Surety's power of attorney
   - The firm's TX Taxable Entity Search record
   Mismatches in legal name get the proposal rejected at clerk review. Triple-check on 2026-06-04 as part of the assembly QC.
5. **Numbers in words AND figures.** Where the form has both fields, fill both. Words govern if they disagree — make sure they don't.
6. **Don't add unsolicited content.** Don't attach VE proposals, don't add "see attached" notes that go to anything outside what the form requested. Anything beyond what's asked is grounds for rejection as non-responsive.
7. **No leading zeros, no trailing commas.** "$385,000.00" — not "$0,385,000.00" or "$385,000," or "$385K".
8. **Date format consistency.** Use the date format the form uses (typically MM/DD/YYYY for TX state forms).

## 4. Fields that MUST be signed in ink (vs. typed or digital)

| Field | Ink? | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Section 4 — Officer's signature | **Yes — BLUE INK ORIGINAL** | Wet signature only |
| Section 5 — Each certification signature | **Yes — BLUE INK ORIGINAL** | Each separate certification gets a separate signature |
| Section 6 — Addenda initials | **Yes — BLUE INK** | Initials only, not full signature |
| Page initials throughout | **Yes — BLUE INK** | Every page with an initial block |
| Notary stamp (if applicable) | **Yes — original notary stamp + seal** | If notarization is required |
| Corporate seal (if applicable) | **Yes — embossed original** | If firm has a seal |

The **bid bond** is a separate document but follows the same ink rules — original wet signatures from the surety's attorney-in-fact AND the firm officer, surety's seal embossed, original power of attorney attached.

The **insurance ACORDs** can be issued electronically by the broker but must be **dated within 30 days of submission**.

The **W-9** can be typed and signed in any color ink, but the signature must be original (not a photocopy).

The **CIQ form** must be wet-signed by the officer (TX state pattern).

The **HB 1295 certificate** is e-filed with the TX Ethics Commission and printed; the printed certificate has an EC-assigned certificate number — that number is the "certification" (no separate signature required).
