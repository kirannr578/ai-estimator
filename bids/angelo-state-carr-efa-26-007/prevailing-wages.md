## B. Fringe-vs-base split

The TTUS-adopted prevailing wage determination uses the Texas Workforce Commission's survey-based determination, which reports **base wage + fringe benefits separately** (similar to the Davis-Bacon Act federal WD format).

**Typical Tom Green County 2023 fringe percentages** (apply to base wage; final fringe is in the actual PDF — confirm):

| Trade | Typical fringe % of base | Fringe $ at given base |
|---|---|---|
| Carpenter | 15–25% | $3.10–$5.17/hr → loaded $23.79–$25.86/hr |
| Drywall installer | 15–25% | $2.82–$4.70/hr → loaded $21.60–$23.48/hr |
| Electrician — Journeyman | 25–35% | $5.87–$8.22/hr → loaded $29.34–$31.69/hr |
| Plumber / Pipefitter | 25–35% | $6.75–$9.45/hr → loaded $33.75–$36.45/hr |
| Painter | 10–20% | $1.74–$3.49/hr → loaded $19.18–$20.93/hr |
| Roofer | 10–20% | $1.70–$3.40/hr → loaded $18.70–$20.40/hr |

> ⚠️ The fringe percentages above are **rules of thumb** for Texas building-trades WDs of this era. The actual fringe amounts in Attachment F.1 should be used as the floor. Some WDs list fringe as a single dollar amount rather than a percentage; some include health & welfare + pension + apprenticeship + training as separate line items.

**The TX prevailing wage rule (Tex. Gov't Code § 2258.022):** the contractor (and every subcontractor) must pay **at least the base + fringe combined total** for each hour worked, regardless of how the contractor splits compensation between cash wages and fringes. We can pay all-cash (no fringes) as long as the cash exceeds base + fringe combined.

## C. Escalation — 2023 rates → 2026 bid

Attachment F.1 is dated **July 2023**. The bid will be priced and labor will be performed in **mid-to-late 2026**. Texas construction labor cost has escalated meaningfully in the 2023–2026 window:

| Source | 2023–2026 TX construction labor escalation |
|---|---|
| ENR Building Cost Index (Texas region) | ~7% / yr → ~22% cumulative over 3 years |
| AGC Texas Building Branch wage survey | ~4–6% / yr → ~13–19% cumulative |
| RSMeans City Cost Index (TX cities) | ~4% / yr → ~12% cumulative |
| Federal Reserve PCE deflator (construction services) | ~3.5% / yr → ~11% cumulative |

**Working escalation factor: +12–18% on the Attachment F.1 rates** — apply to the loaded (base + fringe) wage. Carry the midpoint (+15%) for the bid; reduce to +12% if our labor cost basis is already at-or-above the escalated rate.

**Example — Plumber:**
- 2023 base + fringe (loaded) = ~$35/hr
- 2026 escalated (+15%) = ~$40/hr
- If our internal cost basis is $42/hr, no adjustment needed (we're already above)
- If our internal cost basis is $36/hr, bump labor cost basis to $40/hr in the estimate

## D. Compliance requirements (per Tex. Gov't Code § 2258 + UGSC Art. 2)

1. **Pay at-or-above the WD rates** for every craft on the site (own forces + subs + sub-subs).
2. **Post the WD at the job site** in a location accessible to workers.
3. **Certified payroll reports** — TTUS UGSC typically requires weekly certified payrolls (TX Form WH-347 equivalent) signed by an officer of the firm, listing each worker, craft, hours, gross + net + deductions.
4. **Penalty for non-compliance** — $60/day per worker per violation (Tex. Gov't Code § 2258.023); plus potential debarment from TX state work.
5. **Sub flow-down** — every subcontract must include a clause requiring the sub to comply with the WD. Audit subs' certified payrolls at the firm level.
6. **Classification** — workers must be paid at the rate of the work they actually perform, not the rate of their job title. If a "laborer" spends an hour doing carpenter's work, that hour is paid at the carpenter rate.
7. **No "split-craft" or "helper" exception** in the Texas WD — every hour is paid at the prevailing rate of the work performed.
8. **Apprentice and trainee rates** — only allowed if the apprentice is registered in a state-certified apprenticeship program; otherwise pay the full journeyman rate.

## E. Putting wages into the bid

For each labor line in `takeoff-template.json`:
- Use **loaded wage (base + fringe + escalation)** × **burden factor** (payroll taxes + workers comp + general liability + admin overhead) for the fully-burdened labor cost
- Typical burden factor for TX commercial work: **1.45–1.65×** loaded wage (i.e. payroll burden is 45–65% of the loaded wage)
- Apply productivity factor for active-building / after-hours work (typical 0.75–0.85× standard productivity → multiply labor hours by 1.18–1.33× to land same output)

**Example — Plumber labor cost in bid:**
- Loaded wage (2023 base + fringe): $35/hr
- Escalated to 2026 (+15%): $40/hr
- Burden (×1.55): $62/hr fully-burdened
- After-hours premium (×1.30 for ~25% of hours): blended ~$67/hr
- Use $65–$70/hr as the plumber labor cost basis in the bid
