## A. Texas HUB Program — rules in force (34 TAC §20.281–.298, as amended through 2024)

A "HUB" is a Texas-certified Historically Underutilized Business — minority-, woman-, or service-disabled-veteran-owned, certified through the TX Comptroller's CMBL/HUB program.

### Statewide HUB participation goals (the regulatory floor)

State agencies and TTUS member institutions are required to make a good-faith effort (GFE) to meet the following **statewide HUB goals** when subcontracting opportunities exist (per 34 TAC §20.284, current goals):

| Procurement category | Statewide goal | Applies when |
|---|---|---|
| Heavy construction (other than building) | **11.2%** | Highway / utility / earthwork / civil |
| Building construction, including general contractors and operative builders | **21.1%** | New construction of buildings, AND major building renovations that don't fall under "special trade" |
| Special trade construction | **32.7%** | Trade-specific subcontract work — e.g. a single-trade reno scope |
| Professional services | **23.7%** | A&E, consultant |
| Other services | **26.0%** | |
| Commodities | **21.1%** | Materials-only buys |

> ⚠️ **Important goal clarification:** The user's task brief lists "special trade construction ~32.7%" and "heavy construction ~21.1%". The 2024-amended 34 TAC §20.284 actually has it the other way around: building construction is 21.1% and special trade construction is 32.7%. Different sources cite different years' numbers — the current published TX Comptroller goals (as of the 2024 amendment) have **special trade construction at 32.7%** and **building construction at 21.1%**. The Carr EFA Dressing Room Renovation is a building renovation that we will subcontract across multiple trades — most often a renovation like this is classified **"building construction" (21.1%)** when scoped as a single GC contract, or "special trade construction" (32.7%) when each trade scope is procured separately by the owner. Since ASU is awarding to a single GC, the **21.1% statewide goal for building construction** is the default floor.
>
> **The sample CSA (Attachment B.2) shows a 30% HUB contracting commitment as the placeholder** the executed CSA carries. The 30% is the TTUS pattern preference (well above the 21.1% statewide goal); the project-specific number ASU sets for Carr EFA may match the 30% pattern or may differ. We should bid **a HSP that commits to ≥30% HUB participation of the subcontracted portion** to align with the TTUS sample CSA pattern and to score / pass on HSP responsiveness with margin.

### Self-perform allowance

The prime is allowed to **self-perform** any portion of the work as long as the self-performed scope is justified and documented in the Self-Performing Justification section (Section 3 of the HSP form). The HUB goal applies only to the **subcontracted** portion — self-performed dollars are excluded from the denominator.

### GFE safety valve

A prime that does NOT meet the project HUB goal can still be responsive if it documents **good faith effort** (GFE). See § E below for what GFE documentation looks like.

### Post-award compliance

After award, **monthly HUB Progress Assessment Reports (PARs)** must be filed with each pay application. TTUS member institutions have their own HUB Operations office (centralized through TTU main campus) that receives PARs.

### Revised HSP

If subcontracting scope changes post-award (sub goes out of business, sub fails to perform, scope grows / shrinks), a **revised HSP** must be submitted to TTUS HUB Operations for approval before any new sub is engaged. The original HSP filing includes an acknowledgement that revised HSPs may be required — sign the acknowledgement at proposal time.

## D. HUB sub outreach plan — solicit ≥3 HUB subs per trade

### Lookup procedure

Texas CMBL HUB search at `https://comptroller.texas.gov/purchasing/vendor/hub/search.php`:
- Filter by HUB-certified
- Filter by NIGP code matching the trade (e.g. `912-XX` for construction trades)
- Filter geographic region: prioritize San Angelo (Tom Green County) and Abilene (Taylor County) for trades where local presence reduces mobilization cost; widen to Austin / San Antonio / Dallas-Fort Worth radius for specialty trades (theater-grade millwork, theater-grade lighting, hazmat abatement)

### Per-trade outreach process

1. Pull the top 5–10 candidate HUB subs from CMBL search per trade
2. Email a standardized solicitation packet to each within **2 business days of drawings arriving** (or, if drawings haven't arrived by 2026-05-25, send the packet with the scope narrative + "drawings to follow" note — this still counts toward GFE):
   - Project name + number (Carr EFA Dressing Room Renovation, Sol 26-007)
   - Scope summary for that trade (lift from `04-scope-of-work.md`)
   - Bid due date (back-timed from June 5 → sub quote needed by **2026-05-29** to allow assembly time)
   - Plans / specs (attached or linked when in hand)
   - Required certifications (TX W-9, HUB certification cert current within 2 years, insurance per UGSC Art. 5)
   - GC contact for questions
3. Log every outreach: date, method (email / fax / certified mail), recipient, response (or non-response)
4. If a HUB sub bids and is selected → include in HSP § 2 with vendor # and HUB cert number
5. If a HUB sub bids and is NOT selected (price, schedule, qualification, bonding gap) → document the **written reason** in the GFE binder
6. If a HUB sub does NOT respond → that's part of GFE evidence; document the attempt date + method

## E. Good-faith-effort documentation (the make-or-break) — § 4 of the HSP form

Texas HSP rules require **at least two** of the following when the HUB goal is not met. **To be safe, do all five**:

1. **Divide the scope into reasonable HUB-sized portions** — the per-trade table in § C is the seed for this; package small enough that a HUB sub can handle each chunk (i.e. do not bundle electrical + low-voltage + fire alarm into a single mega-sub that only large non-HUB firms can quote).
2. **Advertise the subcontracting opportunity** in at least two of:
   - The **TX Comptroller's HUB News** publication
   - **Texas Construction News** (or similar trade publication serving TX construction)
   - The **Hispanic Contractors Association of Texas** bid list
   - **NAWIC Texas Chapter** (National Association of Women in Construction)
   - **Greater San Antonio / Houston / Dallas Black Chamber of Commerce** publication
   - Posting on the firm's own subcontractor-bid portal with an explicit HUB notice
3. **Send written notice to a representative sample of HUB subcontractors** for each subcontracting opportunity (the per-trade outreach in § D satisfies this — target ≥3 HUBs per trade).
4. **Attend HUB outreach events** the agency hosts. TTUS HUB Operations runs periodic HUB events; check `https://www.depts.ttu.edu/procurement/` for their events calendar.
5. **Contact a HUB Discretionary Contracting Forum participant** — request from Samuel Guevara whether ASU/TTUS has a current discretionary contracting forum we should engage.

For each HUB sub that bid but was not selected, the GFE binder must contain:
- The written quote
- The selected non-HUB sub's quote (for comparison)
- A **written explanation** of why the non-HUB sub was selected (price differential >5%, schedule incompatibility, qualifications gap, bonding gap)
- Dated evidence the HUB sub was offered an opportunity to match (TTUS preference, though not a regulatory requirement)

## H. Risk callouts on HSP specifically

- **Project-specific HUB goal may exceed 30%.** TTUS sample CSA shows 30% placeholder; the actual ASU/TTUS project-specific commitment for Carr EFA could be higher. Confirm with Samuel + read the RFCSP body. If the project goal is >30%, our per-trade allocation in § C should be re-balanced upward.
- **Theater-grade millwork has thin HUB representation.** This is the trade most likely to be all-non-HUB; carry written GFE documentation specifically for this trade.
- **HUB certifications expire** every 2 years. Before listing a HUB sub on the final HSP, re-verify their cert number on the CMBL search (`https://mycpa.cpa.state.texas.gov/tpasscmblsearch/`); expired certs get the HSP kicked back.
- **HSP submission location may be different from proposal location.** TTUS sometimes splits these (proposal to FP&C, HSP to HUB Ops). Confirm with Samuel and `timeline.md` reflects the right address.
- **Three-day window between proposal and HSP is a coordination risk.** If the proposal binder is fully assembled but the HSP isn't quite done, the firm faces a Monday-AM scramble. Build assembly cushion into the schedule (target HSP final-print by COB Friday June 5, same day as proposal submission).
