## Scope (verbatim from PDF §§1.1, 1.6, 1.7)

> **§1.1** *"Goal: Replace the Camp Maxey Building 1 roof"*
>
> **§1.6 Lower Roof Section:**
> 1.6.1. Remove all loose gravel & dispose off site
> 1.6.2. Remove all drip edge, gutter, downspouts & counter flashing metal
> 1.6.3. Install 1/2" High Density ISO cover board, mechanically fastened through the
>    Lightweight & into the metal deck, per wind uplift calculations
> 1.6.4. Install 60 Mil TPO membrane over the HD ISO, fully adhered
> 1.6.5. Install 60 Mil TPO flashing on all walls & curbs, fully adhered
> 1.6.6. Install new pipe penetrations & TPO wrap on all vent stacks
> 1.6.7. Install new 1/2" plywood around the perimeter to match the new 1/2" HD cover board thickness
> 1.6.8. Install new 24 ga, TPO coated metal drip edge around perimeter
> 1.6.9. Install new 24 ga, pre-finished metal counterflashing, gutter & downspouts
>
> **§1.7 Upper Roof Section:**
> 1.7.1. Remove all loose gravel & dispose offsite
> 1.7.2. Remove all drip edge, gutter, downspouts & counter flashing metal
> 1.7.3. **Install 1/2" High Density ISO cover board, Fully Adhere with Low Rise Foam** per wind uplift calculations *(differs from lower-roof §1.6.3 mechanical fastening)*
> 1.7.4. Install 60 Mil TPO membrane over the HD ISO, fully adhered
> 1.7.5–1.7.9. (same TPO flashing / vent / plywood / drip edge / CF/gutter/DS as lower roof)

**Key technical distinction:** lower roof gets **mechanically fastened** ISO (through lightweight concrete into metal deck) per uplift calcs; upper roof gets **fully adhered** ISO with Low Rise Foam adhesive per uplift calcs. Different deck assemblies (lightweight concrete vs. presumed structural deck only) drive the attachment-method choice. Implies lower roof has a lightweight-concrete fill over a steel deck.
