## 1. Understanding of the work

The B1710 Office Refurbishment is a **single-building, occupied-condition interior finishes renovation** at Building 1710, NAS JRB Fort Worth, covering approximately 3,500 SF of office and common-area space. The work is a coordinated 4-trade scope: demolition, drywall (limited), carpet tile + base, and paint. There is one specialty item (access panel for valve in Rm 1008) and a minor structural infill (sheetrock-in of Rm 1030 doorway).

We have read the SOW in full and the floor plan; our scope decomposition is in `../02-scope-of-work.md`.

### 1.1 Critical-path activities

In order of duration:
1. **Paint** (Item C in SOW) — longest single-trade duration; requires phased room-by-room access. ~12 working days for ~13,500 SF wall area with a 2-painter crew, 2 coats over primer.
2. **Carpet tile + base install** (Item B) — second-longest; ~10-12 working days for 3,500 SF tile + 1,300 LF base with a 2-person crew. Must follow paint completion in each room to avoid overspray onto new flooring.
3. **Demolition + furniture relocate** (Item A) — must lead all other trades in each room; ~5-7 working days for 16 rooms.
4. **Drywall in-fill at Rm 1030 + Rm 1008 patch + access panel** (Items D/F) — short duration (~3 working days) but must precede paint in those two rooms.
5. **Disposal** (Item E + general) — continuous through PoP; 10 CY roll-off staged on-base per coordinated delivery with `steven.munnell@us.af.mil`.

### 1.2 Sequence — by-room batching

We will divide the 16 C/B/P rooms into **4 batches of 4 rooms each** to minimize disruption to AFRC operations per SOW §3.1.2:

- **Batch 1** (week 1-2): Rooms 1002, 1006, 1008, 1009 — includes Rm 1008 wall patch + access panel
- **Batch 2** (week 3-4): Rooms 1013, 1020, 1022, 1023
- **Batch 3** (week 5-6): Rooms 1025, 1026, 1028, 1029
- **Batch 4** (week 7-8): Room 1030 (includes doorway sheetrock-in) + hallway + remaining + punch-list

This sequence allows the AFRC tenant to vacate rooms in rolling fashion (4 at a time), preserving operational continuity. Floats in weeks 9-12 absorb any furniture-coordination slip and the final close-out.

### 1.3 Working hours + base access

Per SOW §SCHEDULE: 0700–1600 M-F. Material deliveries via the commercial vehicle gate (Alta Mere Drive × Ridgmar Meadows Road) 0600-1400 M-F. Base-access vetting for prime + sub personnel coordinated with `steven.munnell@us.af.mil` post-NTP; lead time 3-5 business days from passname submission. Same procedure as on the Cmd Post + NDI project (separate workspace; same base).

## 4. Quality control plan

We will execute the project under our firm's standard QC plan, summarized:

- **Daily** — Site supervision; punch-walk each completed room before move-on to next batch
- **Per-trade hold-points** — Demolition completion / drywall completion / paint 1st coat dry / paint 2nd coat dry / carpet install acceptance / base install acceptance — internal QC signature each
- **Submittals** — AF IMT 3000 forms for each material before procurement (carpet, base, paint, drywall, access panel)
