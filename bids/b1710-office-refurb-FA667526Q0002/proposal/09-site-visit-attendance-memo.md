## 4. Base-access constraint (why site walk is unlikely in window)

NAS JRB Fort Worth is a Joint Reserve Base under Navy installation command. Base access for contractors who are not previously vetted requires:

- Personnel base-access submission package: name, DOB, SSN (last 4), citizenship, valid REAL ID compliant ID
- Sponsor coordination (typically the project COR — Steve Munnell)
- Background-check lead time: **3-5 business days minimum**, longer if a personnel-security-check is required

With the offer due 29 May at 17:00 and the RFI submitted 28 May AM, the earliest possible base-access vetting completion would be ~02 June — i.e., **after** the offer deadline.

**Conclusion:** A pre-submission site walk is **structurally infeasible** in the available window, absent special CO action.

## 6. Reservation of rights under FAR 52.236-2 and 52.236-3

The offeror acknowledges that:

- FAR 52.236-2 (Differing Site Conditions) applies. If, upon field verification post-NTP, materially different conditions are discovered (e.g., additional floor leveler needed, additional patch areas not visible in the SOW, etc.), the offeror will notify the CO in writing per the clause's requirements and seek an equitable adjustment if appropriate.
- FAR 52.236-3 (Site Investigation and Conditions Affecting the Work) is acknowledged.
- Because no Government-scheduled site visit was offered in the bidding window, **the SOW-stated quantity (3,500 SF) is the basis of the offered price**, subject to field-verification under FAR 52.236-2 if material variance is discovered.

The offeror believes this approach is consistent with the SOW intent and the CO's expectation; the RFI submitted on 28 May is documentation of the offeror's good-faith attempt to coordinate.
