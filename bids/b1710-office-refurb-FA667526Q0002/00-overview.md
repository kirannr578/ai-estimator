## Site-visit posture (important — affects pricing risk)

The SOW §SITE VISITATION states verbatim:

> *"Prior to submitting proposals, potential contractors shall walk the facility to confirm all spaces and measurements are accurate and understood."*

The RFQ package does **not** announce a scheduled site-walk date/time/POC. Two interpretations are tenable:

1. The CO expects offerors to coordinate a site walk individually with `lydia.carlton@us.af.mil` (Lydia Carlton) ahead of the 29 May deadline. **Action: send an RFI today (Wed 27 May)** requesting site-walk coordination — see `proposal/11-rfi-cover-letter.md`. Lead time for base access for visitors is typically 3-5 business days; given the 48-hour window, this likely cannot land before the deadline.
2. The CO is using the site-walk language as a CYA clause — offerors are expected to **field-verify after award**, and the language exists to bar a post-award "we didn't know" Differing Site Conditions claim under FAR 52.236-2.

**Most likely interpretation given the 48-hour window: option 2.** We price field-verified-after-award, build a contingency for unforeseen quantity variance (recommend +5% on the carpet/base/paint takeoff vs SOW-stated 3,500 SF), and submit the RFI on the morning of 28 May to document the request. See `07-risk-register.md` Risk #2 for the scoring + mitigation.

## Evaluation factors

The Section M of this RFQ is **sparse** — only FAR 52.225-10 (Notice of Buy American Requirement) is cited. There is **no FAR 52.212-2** (Evaluation - Commercial Items) clause in the package. Combined with FAR 52.212-1 Deviation 2026-O0038 and the absence of any LPTA / best-value scoring matrix, the evaluation is most plausibly **comparative evaluation per FAR 12.203(c)(2)** (CO compares offers without published objective criteria, picks the best value). See `04-evaluation-strategy.md` for the detailed read.

**Practical implication:** Past performance + price competitiveness + responsiveness to the SOW. Comparative evaluation gives the CO wide latitude; a low-priced but sloppy offer can lose to a slightly-higher offer with a clean technical and past-perf story.

## Decision: pursue

Confirmed **PURSUE** at 2026-05-27 scaffold creation. Rationale:

- Scope is a 100% match for BPC's self-perform sweet spot (carpet + base + paint + drywall patch).
- Project size ($25K-$100K typical for this scope at federal Davis-Bacon rates) is well within bonding/insurance capacity.
- 100% Small Business set-aside → competition pool is BPC's natural peer set, not large GCs.
- Past performance is buildable from existing BPC projects (Hindu Temple of Southlake, Holiday Inn Hall Park, Lavon RV Park — see `proposal/03-past-performance.md`).
- AFRC's 301 LRS/LGC is a repeat federal customer at NAS JRB Fort Worth; a clean execution opens the door to follow-on small-projects work.

The "no-go switch" is now narrow: pursuit reversal only if (a) SAM.gov is found inactive / reps-and-certs lapsed and cannot refresh in 24 hours, OR (b) bonding agent confirms no payment-bond / ILC capacity. Both are very low probability per the firm profile.
