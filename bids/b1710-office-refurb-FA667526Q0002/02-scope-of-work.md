## 1. Headline (verbatim SOW intro)

> *"REPLACE CARPET, VINYL COVE BASE, PAINT INTERIOR SURFACES AND SHEETROCK IN A CURRENT OPEN DOORWAY. APPROXIMATE SQ FOOTAGE FOR THE FACILITY REFRESH IS 3,500. OVERVIEW: This project replaces the carpet and cove base for approximately 3,500 Sq ft of the facility. It also requires new interior paint for each identified area and the sheetrock fill in of an open doorway in Room 1030. The facility is located at Bldg. 1710 at Naval Air Station (NAS) Fort Worth Joint Reserve Base (JRB), Texas. The contractor shall work around or relocate existing furniture and then restore furniture to the original or designated locations. Where required, level flooring before new carpet squares are placed."*

## 2. Work items (SOW §SCOPE OF WORK A–F)

### Item A — Demolish/dispose existing flooring (~3,500 SF)

- Demolish + dispose **all carpeting and flooring** from the identified areas on the floor plan.
- **Contractor to verify actual square footage** at time of proposal (per SOW §A verbatim).
- All removed materials disposed of in a **certified land fill for construction materials**.
- Existing furniture in scope: contractor shall move/relocate furniture affecting the work and **re-position at the completion** of the new flooring effort.

### Item B — Furnish + install carpet tile + 6" rubber base

Materials specified by SOW §B verbatim:

| Spec field | SOW value |
|---|---|
| Carpet type | Fiber pile, **100% Nylon** |
| Carpet style | **Shaw Constellat EW24** (Style # 59326) |
| Carpet description | **24" × 24" carpet tile** |
| Carpet manufacturer | **Shaw or other accepted contractor submission** |
| Ideal color | **26110 Moonlight Grade A, Dye Lot H6958** |
| Base description | **Rubber Base 6"** (vinyl cove base) |
| Base manufacturer | Contractor shall provide **samples for selection** |
| Base product / style | N/A (samples-driven) |
| Base ideal color | **Gray** — contractor shall provide samples for selection |

Coverage area: per floor plan (Attachment 02), labeled "C/B/P" (Carpet/Base/Paint) — Rooms 1002, 1006, 1008, 1009, 1013, 1020, 1022, 1023, 1025, 1026, 1028, 1029, 1030, plus hallway-segment per the label `"paint all hallway"`. The plan also marks "CARPET/BASE/PAINT" zones in several locations on the lower portion of the building. Total SOW-stated coverage: **~3,500 SF**.

> ⚠️ Equal-product substitution rationale (proposal point): SOW §B says "Shaw or other accepted contractor submission." We may bid Shaw **Constellat EW24** at spec, OR offer a quality-equal alternative (e.g. Mohawk SmartStrand, Interface 24×24 tile in equivalent grade) if cost/availability is materially better. **Recommended:** quote Shaw at spec to maximize technical-acceptability score; flag substitution option in cover letter as cost reduction available on request.

### Item C — Paint walls + identified surfaces ("Agreeable Gray")

- **Patch / fill holes / prepare surface and paint** all identified surfaces in the facility.
- Color: **"Agreeable Gray"** — verbatim SOW; this is **Sherwin-Williams SW 7029** (Agreeable Gray) by common usage. *RFI item: confirm SW 7029 specifically vs any brand color-matched.*
- Surfaces: SOW says "all identified surfaces" — read with floor plan annotations "paint all hallway" and the per-room C/B/P tags. Conservatively scope all walls floor-to-ceiling in C/B/P rooms + hallway walls.
- Standard finish system inferred (SOW silent on sheen / coat count): assume one coat primer (where bare drywall or color-change) + two coats finish in eggshell or matte interior latex (SW ProMar 200 or equivalent for federal-spec compatibility).

### Item D — Sheetrock-in open doorway in Room 1030 (full-length wall, texture, paint)

- Verbatim: *"Sheetrock in current door frame opening in Room 1030 to make it a full length wall, texture and paint new surface."*
- Inference: existing wood/metal door frame must be removed (or boxed) before sheetrock. Add metal stud framing in the door opening (16" o.c. typical), 5/8" GWB both sides, tape/bed/texture (orange peel or knockdown to match adjacent walls — field verify), prime + paint to match Item C.
- Estimated single-opening area: ~3' wide × 7' tall = **21 SF each side = 42 SF total GWB**, plus framing ~ 7 LF metal stud.

### Item E — Dispose furniture + material in Room 1008

- Verbatim: *"Dispose of all Furniture and material in Room 1008."*
- Inference: full pack-out + haul-off of one room's furniture (size unknown without site walk). Conservative estimate: 1 day labor for 2-person crew + 1 roll-off CY contribution.

### Item F — Repair wall in Room 1008 + install access panel for exposed valve

- Verbatim: *"Repair damage to wall in Room 1008 and install a panel access to the exposed Valve."*
- Inference: wall-repair scope unknown without site verification. Conservative estimate: ~16 SF GWB patch + tape/bed/texture + prime/paint + 12"×12" or 12"×18" steel-frame plaster-bead access panel (Karp Associates DSB-214M or equal), valve-rated.

## 5. PART III — Execution (key clauses)

### Scheduling + coordination

- Work shall be coordinated with the assigned **Contracting Officer (KO)**.
- **Sequence of work:** SOW §3.1.2 verbatim:
  > *"The work shall be performed in such a manner as to minimize interruption of the normal duty day of Government activity. Most of the facility will be vacated as necessary in order for required work to be performed. Prior to commencement of work, the Contractor shall meet with the Contracting Officer so that the planned sequence of work and/or schedule may be reviewed and approved."*

### Utility interruptions

- "Should not be a factor for this effort" per SOW. If unavoidable, routing through Project Rep + KO.

### Measurements

- **Contractor field-verifies all aspects of the project.** Government not responsible for contractor oversight. This shifts quantity risk to the contractor — see `07-risk-register.md` Risk #2.

### Existing work / facilities protection

- Contractor protects surrounding areas + Government property; pays for any damage caused.
- Electrical/mechanical utilities protected; barricades, temporary covering for exposed areas if removal of utilities/pavement is specified.

### Dust + debris control

- Limit spread of dust/debris to surrounding buildings + outside.
- Non-hazardous debris cleaned up **daily**; on-site dumpsters **covered at all times**.

## 6. PART III — Performance + workmanship

- Contractor responsible for **quality control** + maintains an effective QC system.
- All work in accordance with **OSHA 29 CFR 1910 + 1926**.
- Complies with all current applicable **Federal, State, local regulations/codes/laws** — most stringent applies.
- "Replace" = provide new materials to replace existing/missing.

### Unforeseen major repairs

- Deteriorated material of a major nature → brought to KO's attention; out-of-scope work not undertaken without **written KO direction**. This is the Differing Site Conditions / Changes-Fixed-Price clause hook (FAR 52.236-2 + 52.243-1 Alternate III) per Section I of the RFQ.

## 7. PART IV — Other requirements

### Schedule (hours of operation)

- Construction Schedule submitted for CO approval **prior to work commencement**.
- **Work hours: ~0700–1600, Monday–Friday** (normal Base operating hours).
- **No work weekends / outside weekday hours without prior written CO approval.**
- Federal holidays during PoP: Juneteenth Fri 2026-06-19, Independence Day Fri 2026-07-03 (observed), Labor Day Mon 2026-09-07 (if PoP slips that far). See SF 1449 Section G p.7 holiday list.

- Contractor furnishes ID for each employee on-site (as approved by CO).
- **Hard-hat markings clearly identifying the company** (subs included).
- IDs returned to CO on release of any employee.

### Job site tools + equipment

- Hand tools / equipment in good working order, stored properly.
- Construction attire per OSHA.

### Sanitation

- **Government toilet facilities available** to contractor personnel.
- Contractor maintains cleanliness of restroom areas.

### Occupational safety

### Generated refuse

- Refuse hauled in trucks with **tight-fitting beds and covers** (prevent roadway spillage).
