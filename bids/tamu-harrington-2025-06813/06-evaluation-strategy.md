# 06 — Evaluation strategy

> The Notice does NOT publish evaluation weights (typical for TAMU System CSPs — the weights live in the CSP package's "Instructions to Proposers" section). Until we pull the CSP package, this document is a strategy framework based on the standard TAMU System / Texas Higher Ed CSP scoring pattern.

---

## A. What TAMU System CSPs typically evaluate

Per Tex. Gov't Code Ch. 2269 (Texas's procurement statute for government building construction) and standard TAMU System CSP practice, evaluation factors typically include:

| Factor | Typical weight range | What it measures |
|---|---|---|
| **Price** | 40–60% | Lump-sum proposal price, sometimes weighted with unit prices and alternates |
| **Past performance / qualifications** | 15–25% | Similar institutional projects, references, key-personnel résumés |
| **Technical approach / project understanding** | 10–20% | Narrative covering schedule, sequencing, coordination, quality control, lab-specific considerations |
| **HUB / HSP compliance** | 10–15% | Quality of the HSP submission and demonstrated GFE; sometimes pass/fail rather than scored |
| **Schedule** | 5–10% | Proposed completion duration vs the owner's target |
| **Safety record** | 0–5% | EMR, OSHA record, safety plan |

> Compare to the canvas's Cleburne ISD example (50% price / 25% performance / 10% qualifications / 10% schedule / 5% delivery method) — same general shape; weights vary by agency.

## C. Where we DON'T win (don't waste narrative on these)

| Lever | Why it doesn't move the needle |
|---|---|
| Federal certifications (SAM.gov, SBA 8(a), HUBZone, etc.) | This is state work — federal small-business status doesn't help in TAMU scoring. TX HUB status (the firm's own, if applicable) is what matters. |
| Big-project past performance ($5M+, ground-up institutional) | TAMU wants to see the bidder has done **work like this** — single-room educational renos under $500K. A $20M ground-up academic building reference looks like overkill and may even signal "this contractor will treat us like a nuisance." |
| Long marketing biography of the firm | TAMU's evaluators are TAMU System construction PMs — they read for technical substance, not branding. Keep the narrative tight. |
| Generic safety plan boilerplate | If safety is scored, it's scored on actual EMR and OSHA-incident history, not on the length of the safety plan. Submit a brief, project-specific safety plan that names lab-specific hazards (chemical, electrical, plenum work, after-hours egress). |
| Proposing a value-engineering deduct without owner request | TAMU CSPs accept alternates only when published in the CSP package. Unsolicited VE proposals often get the bid disqualified for non-responsiveness. Park any VE ideas in a separate "Post-award discussion" cover letter, not the priced proposal. |

## D. Pricing strategy

- **Lump-sum number.** Base proposal price; the dominant scoring axis.
- **Unit prices.** TAMU CSPs often request unit prices on common owner-directed change items (sq. ft. of additional flooring, additional door, additional outlet, etc.). When the CSP package arrives, populate these *carefully* — unit prices set the ceiling for change orders. Bid them at full retail (sub cost + your full GC markup), not stripped-down.
- **Alternates.** Bid only those the CSP lists; ignore any temptation to add an unsolicited alternate.
- **Allowances.** If the CSP includes an owner-controlled cash/contingency allowance (the ASU Carr EFA bid form had a `$25,000 allowance` per `exports/calibration_v3/estimate.json`), carry it line-by-line per the CSP instructions — do NOT roll it into the lump-sum, do NOT mark it up.
- **Markup discipline.**
  - Sub cost + standard markup
  - General conditions (PM, super, temp services) priced from the duration in `04-scope-of-work.md`
  - Bond + insurance loaded as a percentage of subtotal
  - Contingency: **5–7% for a single-room reno with reasonable drawings**; bump to 10% if drawings are thin
  - Overhead: 8–12% typical
  - Profit: 5–7% typical for institutional state work; institutional clients expect modest profit %, not the 10–15% sometimes seen in private commercial
- **Don't over-shave.** TAMU rejects bids that are >25% below the median as "non-responsible" risk. Aim for tight, not lowest.

## E. The narrative arc of the technical proposal

A typical TAMU System CSP technical narrative is 8–15 pages. Suggested structure:

1. **Cover letter** — 1 page; references the solicitation, addenda received, key team, summary of price
2. **Project understanding** — 1 page; restate the scope in our words; flag the assumptions in `04-scope-of-work.md`
3. **Approach & sequencing** — 2–3 pages; tied to the schedule; calls out the coordination pain-points from § C above
4. **Lab-specific considerations** — 1 page; dedicated section as above
5. **Team & key personnel** — 2–3 pages; bio + experience + recent similar project for the PM, super, and safety lead
6. **Past performance / references** — 1–2 pages; 3–5 similar projects with owner contact + scope + value + completion date
7. **HSP** — separate document, but referenced in the narrative
8. **Schedule** — separate exhibit (Gantt or bar chart); referenced in the narrative
9. **Assumptions & clarifications** — 1 page; explicit list of anything not in the drawings + specs that we've assumed (e.g. "no hazmat abatement assumed pending TAMU EHS survey")
10. **Safety plan + EMR letter** — separate exhibit
11. **Quality control plan** — short, project-specific; mention TAB report, punch-list close-out, owner training
