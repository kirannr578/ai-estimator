# Scope outline — TPWD Old Tunnel State Park Stair Replacement

> **Source:** §1 + §4 of the IFB cover, with Attachment B (Specifications) + Attachment C (Drawings) controlling the detailed scope. The scaffold reads pages 1–12 of the 48-page bound document; **the actual specs + drawings have not yet been transcribed** — see [`03-missing-documents.md`](./03-missing-documents.md).

## High-level scope (verbatim)

> "Furnish all labor, tools, equipment, materials and incidentals to **replace the lower loop stairs at Old Tunnel State Park (SP) located in Kendall County, Texas** per the specifications provided herein." (§1)

> "All work not specified and/or not shown on the drawings, but which is necessary for the completion and/or functioning and operation of the Project, shall be understood and implied as part of the Contract to be performed by the Contractor for the contract price." (§4)

This "implied scope" clause is standard for TPWD Minor Construction IFBs and means the contractor cannot use scope-gap arguments to add cost later. Pricing must include reasonable allowance for unstated but necessary work.

## Scope **not** likely in scope

- Utility relocations
- Vertical building construction
- Septic / well / restroom work
- Trail-wide replacement (scope is the *lower loop* stair only)
- Asbestos / lead abatement (no existing building)

## Critical questions to resolve at site visit (target: 2026-06-01 or 6-02)

1. **Stair material spec** — pressure-treated SYP, cedar, redwood, composite, or steel?
2. **Foundation type** — concrete piers, helical piles, ground screws, or surface-set timbers?
3. **Number of stairs / total LF / total EA risers** — scope sizing
4. **ADA compliance required?** Trail-system stair accessibility varies by park designation
5. **Handrail material + finish** — wood, steel, picket spacing, height per IBC §1014
6. **Erosion control + re-vegetation** — TPWD preference for Texas native vegetation per Title 34 TAC 20.38
7. **Vehicle access to lower-loop trail head** — staging, material laydown, debris haul-off
8. **Water supply** — §6.4 says Contractor provides; verify on-site source vs. truck-in
9. **Work hours** — outside regular business hours per §4.1.5 (regular = 7:00 AM – 4:30 PM M-F)
10. **Bat-colony / wildlife construction window** — Old Tunnel's bat colony has seasonal emergence (May-Oct); coordinate with TPWD biology team

## TPWD-specific construction conventions to capture

These are the elements that distinguish a TPWD State Parks Minor Construction IFB from a generic state IFB — worth seeding into the playbook learning log:

1. **Best Value evaluation with VPTS pass/fail** (Attachment A §3.3.6) — past performance is gated, not merely scored.
2. **Texas Vegetation Native to the Region preference** under Title 34 TAC 20.38 — applies when landscaping is in scope.
3. **State of Texas + TPWD as both additional insured AND loss payee** (§11.3) — non-standard dual designation; many brokers default to additional-insured only.
4. **10% retainage** (§23) — released only when warranty + O&M documentation is in hand. Includes manufacturer warranties, installation letters, O&M manuals.
5. **$500/week LD rate** (§21.1) — small in absolute terms but significant for a $15K-$75K bid.
6. **30-day POP** for Minor Construction — aggressive even for a simple stair replacement; mobilization must be efficient.
7. **Email-only submission** (§19.1.3) with date+project# in subject line — no portal, no paper.
