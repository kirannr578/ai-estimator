## B. Aggregate benchmarks — total project cost-per-magnitude

| Bid envelope | Likely outcome |
|---|---|
| **< $100K** | Bid will likely be rejected as unreasonably low per FAR 15.404-1(d). Don't bid here. |
| **$100K – $130K** | LPTA-aggressive. Profit margin < 4%; risk of loss on any takeoff error. |
| **$130K – $170K** | **LPTA-thin balanced.** 5–7% net profit if takeoff is accurate. Recommended target. |
| **$170K – $200K** | Cautious. 8–10% net profit. Likely loses to a more aggressive competitor on LPTA. |
| **$200K – $250K** | Fat. Likely loses to a more aggressive competitor unless competitors are absent. |
| **> $250K** | Above magnitude ceiling; the CO will scrutinize as a possible non-responsible offer. |

## C. Federal small-project markup benchmarks (national)

| Markup component | Range for sub-$250K federal renovation |
|---|---|
| General conditions | 6–14% of direct cost |
| Bonds (bid + P&P) | 1.5–3% of contract value |
| Insurance (CGL + WC + Auto + Umbrella amortized) | 2–4% of contract value |
| Davis-Bacon labor burden uplift | 0–5% on labor-heavy CLINs (if firm's labor rates are < prevailing) |
| Contingency | 3–10% (low end for site-visited, mid-tier for partial, high for sight-unseen) |
| Overhead (firm-level G&A) | 7–12% |
| Profit | 4–10% (institutional / federal norm: lower than commercial) |
| Total markup over direct cost | ~22–48% |

For this LPTA bid, **target the LOW end** of these ranges (GCs 8–10%, contingency 3–5%, profit 5–6%) to be competitive.

## D. Comparable federal awards (for sanity-checking)

Sources: USAspending.gov, FPDS-NG searches by funding agency = USFWS, NAICS = 236220, contract value $100K–$300K, completed in last 36 months. (User should verify specific comps; below is general guidance.)

- Small USFWS facility-rehab awards in the $100K–$250K range frequently clear at **$150K–$225K**
- Award rates within ±15% of the agency's independent government estimate (IGE) midpoint are typical
- IGE midpoint for this project (magnitude $100K–$250K) = $175K → expect lowest-priced technically-acceptable offer at $135K–$170K

These numbers are consistent with the bottoms-up benchmark in § A above: **bid in the $150K–$170K range** is the LPTA-thin sweet spot.

## E. Locality factor for San Marcos / Hays County, TX

- **RSMeans City Cost Index (CCI):** San Marcos / Austin metro typically clears at ~94–98% of national average for commercial construction
- **Labor:** Hays County DBA prevailing wages are mid-range for Texas — neither low (RGV) nor high (Austin City / Dallas core)
- **Material:** Standard national pricing; no port-distance premium since most material is domestically manufactured and trucked from Dallas / Houston / San Antonio supply hubs

**Apply ~0.96 locality factor** to national-average benchmarks. For a $160K national-average bid → ~$153K locality-adjusted.
