# 08 — FAR / DIAR clauses worth flagging

> Federal small construction ≠ FAR-zero. Even at a sub-$250K firm-fixed-price job, this RFP triggers a long list of FAR clauses. Most flow down to subs verbatim; some require contractor-level certifications BEFORE submission.
>
> This document highlights the clauses that have a real operational or compliance impact (not the boilerplate). Page references are to `inbox/.../Sol_140FC126R0017.pdf`.

---

## A. Clauses that change WHAT we bid

### FAR 52.225-9 / 52.225-10 — Buy American Construction Materials

- **Where:** RFP pp.42–47 (full text)
- **What it does:** Requires domestic construction material (mined, produced, or manufactured in US) for incorporated material on the project. For iron-and-steel-predominant materials (24-ga roof panels, 20-ga OH door panels, hollow metal doors, sheet-metal gutters), the foreign iron/steel content must be < 5% of all components. For other construction material, ≥ 65% domestic content (rising to 75% in CY 2029).
- **Impact:** Pre-clear all SKUs as domestic. If any item is non-domestic, file a 52.225-9(b)(3) determination request BEFORE submission; CO may grant exception based on unreasonable cost (foreign material more than 20% under domestic), public-interest, or non-availability.
- **Action:** Submit **52.225-4 Buy American Certificate** with the proposal. List any non-domestic material — none expected on this scope if subs are domestic suppliers.

### FAR 52.236-1 — Performance of Work by the Contractor (15% self-perform)

- **Where:** RFP p.49
- **What it does:** Contractor must perform on site, with its own organization, work equivalent to ≥ 15% of total contract amount.
- **Impact:** Affects crew loading + sub mix. Self-performed = supervision (PM/super on site), site logistics, plus probably ceiling demo, personnel-door install, or gutter install.
- **Action:** Design the schedule with at least 15% direct labor on the firm's own payroll. Document in certified payroll.

### FAR 52.211-18 — Variation in Estimated Quantity

- **Where:** RFP p.18
- **What it does:** If actual unit-price quantity varies > 15% above OR below estimated quantity, either party can request equitable adjustment.
- **Impact:** Only CLIN 0006 (gutter at 110 LF est.) is unit-priced with a quantity. JOB and EA-with-fixed-count CLINs (0001, 0002, 0003, 0004, 0005, 0007, 0008) are not subject to this clause.
- **Action:** Site-visit measurement of gutter LF. If outside 93.5–126.5 LF range, raise an RFI to update the estimate.

### FAR 52.222-6 — Construction Wage Rate Requirements (Davis-Bacon Act)

- **Where:** RFP p.32 (reference only); WD attached as Section J Attachment 3
- **What it does:** Labor on the work site (and on hauling between site and dedicated nearby facility per H.16) must be paid not less than the prevailing wage rates in WD TX20260254.
- **Impact:** Labor rates and fringes flow to all subs. Most TX small-construction subs pay at or above prevailing for skilled trades but verify on the WD.
- **Action:** Transcribe WD into `prevailing-wages.md`; share with subs when soliciting quotes; require weekly certified payroll (WH-347) from each sub.

### FAR 52.222-8 — Payrolls and Basic Records

- **Where:** RFP p.33
- **What it does:** Weekly certified payroll (and Statement of Compliance) for prime and every tier of sub, submitted to CO 7 days after each payroll period close.
- **Impact:** Administrative overhead — pulls together prime + 2–4 subs' payrolls every week for ~8–9 weeks. Build PM-hours into GCs.

### FAR 52.236-2 — Differing Site Conditions

- **Where:** RFP p.31 (reference) + flagged in §H + L.2.1.1
- **What it does:** If contractor encounters subsurface or hidden physical conditions materially different from those indicated in the contract, contractor gives prompt written notice; CO investigates; equitable adjustment if conditions warrant.
- **Impact:** Critical on a renovation where the substrate isn't fully documented. Most likely application here: compromised roof joists, hidden gas-line routing, asbestos or lead found during demo.
- **Action:** Train the superintendent on the written-notice procedure — STOP, NOTIFY IN WRITING (within the contract's notice period; check 52.236-2(a) — typically prior to disturbance or as soon as practicable), DO NOT PROCEED until CO acknowledges. Failure to give prompt notice = waiver of the adjustment.

### FAR 52.228-15 — Performance and Payment Bonds (100% each)

- **Where:** RFP pp.47–49
- **What it does:** P&P bonds at 100% of contract price are required when contract value > $150K (per FAR 28.102-1(a)).
- **Impact:** Bonding agent capacity check is the long-pole item. Order bond commitments tomorrow.
- **Action:** SF 25 (Performance), SF 25A (Payment), on Treasury Circular 570 sureties. Furnish before starting work.

### FAR 52.228-1 — Bid Guarantee (bid bond)

- **Where:** RFP p.68
- **What it does:** Bid bond at 20% of bid OR $1M (whichever less), submitted with the proposal.
- **Impact:** Must accompany the proposal; non-conforming if missing.
- **Action:** Order with the P&P commitments (same surety).

## B. Clauses that change HOW we operate post-award

### FAR 52.232-5 — Payments under Fixed-Price Construction Contracts

- **Where:** RFP p.48 (reference); progress-payment mechanics in RFP §G.4 + Step 1–7 procedure
- **What it does:** Monthly progress payments based on percentage of work completed. CO may withhold 10% if quality / progress concerns remain.
- **Impact:** Cash flow: bond + insurance + GC labor + materials all paid by contractor and recovered monthly. Plan working capital accordingly.
- **Action:** Submit FWS Application and Certificate for Payment via IPP per G.4 Steps 1–7. Include certified payroll attachments per H.19 and G.4.

### FAR 52.232-33 — Payment by Electronic Funds Transfer (EFT)

- **Where:** RFP p.39 (reference)
- **What it does:** Payment via EFT using SAM-registered banking info.
- **Impact:** Banking info in SAM must be correct.
- **Action:** Verify EFT banking with controller before submission.

### DOI IPP — Invoice Processing Platform (1452.232-XX local clauses + G.4)

- **Where:** RFP G.4
- **What it does:** All invoices submitted electronically via `https://www.ipp.gov`.
- **Impact:** Enrollment 3–5 days post-award; first invoice can't go until enrollment complete.
- **Action:** Coordinate IPP enrollment immediately upon award. Controller / billing admin owns this.

### FAR 52.246-12 — Inspection of Construction

- **Where:** RFP p.8–11 (full text)
- **What it does:** CO + COTR + Field Inspector right to inspect at all reasonable times.
- **Impact:** Daily site presence by FI (Juan Martinez); daily inspector's log signed by superintendent at end of each workday (RFP G.3).
- **Action:** Schedule super's last-30-minutes-of-day for log signing + daily-progress photo.

### FAR 52.246-21 — Warranty of Construction (MAR 1994)

- **Where:** RFP §E "Pre-Final / Final Inspection" + SOW §3.4
- **What it does:** 1-year general warranty on materials + workmanship from substantial completion.
- **Impact:** Plus longer manufacturer warranties: 20 years on metal roof panels, 3-yr door / 10-yr finish on roll-up doors (SOW §3.4.B.2–.3).
- **Action:** Collect manufacturer warranty paperwork; submit to COR before final inspection (SOW §3.4.B.3 written-out condition).

### FAR 52.236-13 — Accident Prevention

- **Where:** RFP p.31 (reference)
- **What it does:** Contractor responsible for safety; effective safety program required.
- **Impact:** Safety plan + competent-person designation + accident notification within H3.0 procedures.
- **Action:** Submit safety plan with submittals (SOW §1.8.C requires safety plan before starting work). Notify CO of any accident/incident with $2,500+ loss within written-report timeline.

### FAR 52.236-7 — Permits and Responsibilities

- **Where:** RFP p.8 (reference)
- **What it does:** Contractor obtains necessary permits + complies with applicable Federal, state, local law.
- **Impact:** Federal land typically supersedes county building permit, but the gas-utility permit for gas-line abandonment is still required from the local gas utility (Atmos Energy or co-op).
- **Action:** Gas sub coordinates utility permit; contractor verifies sub has permit before work.

## C. Reps & certs / submission-stage compliance clauses

### FAR 52.204-7 — System for Award Management

- **Where:** Referenced via L.1.0 + reference list
- **What it does:** Offeror must be SAM-registered prior to award, during performance, and through final payment.
- **Impact:** GO/NO-GO per R-01 in the risk register.

### FAR 52.204-8 — Annual Representations and Certifications

- **Where:** RFP §K pp.52–57 (full text)
- **What it does:** Incorporates SAM Reps & Certs by reference. Offeror states that SAM-posted reps are current.
- **Impact:** If SAM Reps are > 12 months old, the bid cannot invoke this incorporation — must complete individual reps in the solicitation.
- **Action:** Refresh SAM Reps & Certs to within last 12 months; state invocation per (d).

### FAR 52.204-21 — Basic Safeguarding of Covered Contractor Information Systems

- **Where:** RFP referenced at p.40
- **What it does:** Even at sub-$250K, contractor's information systems holding FCI (Federal Contract Information) must implement 17 basic safeguarding controls per NIST SP 800-171 subset.
- **Impact:** Internal IT compliance — limited-access on contract files, antivirus, patching, etc.
- **Action:** `[USER TO VERIFY]` that firm IT meets the 17 controls. Most small firms with basic IT hygiene clear this without dedicated effort.

### FAR 52.204-24 / 52.204-25 / 52.204-26 — Covered Telecommunications Equipment

- **Where:** RFP pp.58–60 + reference list
- **What it does:** Offeror represents whether it uses or provides "covered telecom" (Section 889 — Huawei, ZTE, Hytera, Hikvision, Dahua, ByteDance, etc.).
- **Impact:** Tick "does not / will not" in SAM (annually) and confirm.
- **Action:** `[USER TO VERIFY]` SAM reps. Boilerplate for most US small businesses.

### FAR 52.204-29 — FASCSA Orders Representation & Disclosures

- **Where:** RFP pp.60–61 (full text)
- **What it does:** Offeror represents that it has conducted reasonable inquiry and does not propose to use covered articles or sources subject to FASCSA orders.
- **Impact:** Tick the rep; search "FASCSA order" on SAM and verify no proposed items hit.
- **Action:** `[USER TO VERIFY]` and tick in SAM.

### FAR 52.204-30 — FASCSA Orders Prohibition

- **Where:** RFP pp.13–17 (full text — long clause)
- **What it does:** Post-award, contractor reviews SAM quarterly for new FASCSA orders + reports if affected.
- **Impact:** Quarterly cadence (CY: Jan / Apr / Jul / Oct) for the duration of the contract. For a 60-day contract, falls within at most one quarterly cycle.

### FAR 52.219-1 — Small Business Program Representations

- **Where:** RFP K(xii) reference
- **What it does:** Self-cert as small business under NAICS 236220 at $45M size standard.
- **Impact:** Required since this is a 100% small business set-aside.
- **Action:** `[USER TO VERIFY]` in SAM. The firm must currently meet the $45M size standard (3-year average annual receipts) to bid.

### FAR 52.209-5 — Certification Regarding Responsibility Matters

- **Where:** RFP K(viii) reference
- **What it does:** Offeror discloses any tax delinquencies, contract terminations, criminal/civil proceedings, etc. in prior 3 years.
- **Impact:** Self-disclose if applicable.
- **Action:** `[USER TO VERIFY]` in SAM (annually) + ensure nothing has changed.

### FAR 52.225-2, -4, -20, -25 — Buy American + Sudan + Iran certifications

- **Where:** Various reference + K
- **What it does:** Certifications around domestic-content + restricted-business operations.
- **Action:** All boilerplate "does not" / "does" reps in SAM. `[USER TO VERIFY]`

### FAR 52.222-22 / 52.222-25 — Previous Contracts & Compliance / Affirmative Action

- **Where:** RFP §I reference (RESERVED in this RFP — but listed)
- **Impact:** If firm has previous federal construction contracts > $10K, EEO compliance reports may be due.
- **Action:** `[USER TO VERIFY]` previous compliance status with OFCCP.

## E. Cyber + supply-chain (Section 889 / FASCSA) — small project, still asserted

The user task brief specifically called out cyber hygiene. The RFP:

- Incorporates **FAR 52.204-21 (Basic Safeguarding)** — even at $250K, contractor must implement the 17 basic controls if it holds Federal Contract Information.
- Incorporates **FAR 52.204-23, -25, -27** — bans Kaspersky, Huawei/ZTE/etc., ByteDance covered apps.
- Incorporates **FAR 52.204-29 + 52.204-30** — FASCSA orders, requiring quarterly SAM check during performance.

**Action:** Firm IT lead confirms the 17 basic safeguarding controls are in place + completes SAM reps. None of this should affect bid price but a "yes" answer in SAM that doesn't match reality is a False Claims Act exposure.
