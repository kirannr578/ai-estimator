# 06 — Evaluation strategy (LPTA edition)

> **This RFP is LPTA. LPTA is NOT a value-based award. Read RFP Sections M.1.0–M.1.10 (pp.70–73) before doing anything in this document.**
>
> The instinct of a bidder coming from TAMU CSP / ASU RFCSP / commercial private work is to lead with technical narrative, past performance prose, and value-add differentiation. **On an LPTA, that is wasted effort and a measurable risk** — every hour spent polishing narrative is an hour not spent tightening price or de-risking takeoff. This document spells out exactly how to allocate effort.

---

## A. What the RFP literally says (verbatim or paraphrased)

From Section M.1.0–M.1.4:

- **M.1.0 BASIS OF AWARD.** "This is a 100% Total Small Business Set-Aside solicitation which will be evaluated using the **Lowest Price Technically Acceptable** source selection process to evaluate proposals and make an award."
- **M.1.2.** "The LPTA process is a simplified source selection strategy that **deems cost as the sole evaluation criterion provided that technical, management, and past performance are deemed acceptable (pass/fail)**. **Tradeoffs between price and performance in reaching the award decision are not permitted.**"
- **M.1.3.** "competing offeror's past performance history, management, and technical capability will be evaluated on a basis approximately equal to, price considerations. ... **All proposals shall be treated equally except for their prices.** Failure to meet a requirement may result in a proposal being determined technically unacceptable."
- **M.1.4. Evaluation Process:**
 - **Step 1** — Evaluate all proposals for technical acceptability (Pass/Fail).
 - **Step 2** — Evaluate price reasonableness of all technically Acceptable or Reasonably Susceptible of Being Made Acceptable proposals; rank by total evaluated price.
 - **Step 3** — Award to the **lowest priced offeror who meets the technical requirements with a "pass" rating**. At that point, evaluation stops.

The note in M.1.3 that "performance ... will be evaluated on a basis approximately equal to price" is the **only** place the RFP softens LPTA. **Do not be fooled by it.** M.1.2 immediately above and M.1.4–M.1.7 below explicitly cement Pass/Fail on the non-price factors and explicitly forbid tradeoffs. The award goes to **lowest price that passes**, full stop. Treat the "approximately equal" line as boilerplate that survived from a tradeoff template; the operative evaluation rules are at M.1.2 and M.1.4.

---

## B. The two things that matter and the two things that don't

### What matters (drive your hours here)

1. **PRICE.** Bid as close to your true cost as you can defend with a thin but non-zero margin. The winner is the lowest qualifying bid, period. Every extra dollar of margin is a dollar of risk that someone else clears.
2. **PASS the technical acceptability gate.** The gate is narrow but explicit:
 - Recent (last 3 years) past-performance — **at least 2 examples** of general construction projects "similar in size, scope, and complexity" to the SOW (L.2.1.3.(a) + M.1.4.B.(1)).
 - **Bondability** at this contract magnitude (L.2.1.3.(b) + M.1.4.B.(2)) — provide a surety letter.
 - **Adequate Reps & Certs** in SAM (L.2.1.4).
 - **Bid Guarantee** in proper form at submission (L.2.1.5).
 - **All 8 CLINs priced** with unit + extended + grand total (M.1.9 + L.2.1.2).
 - **Firm-fixed-price**, no contingencies, no sliding scales (M.1.10 + L.2.1.2.E).
 - **No exceptions to the SOW or specs** raised in the priced proposal (L.2.1.1.C + L.2.1.2.E + M.1.10). Any exception must be raised with the CO **before** submission, and the CO must agree to an amendment.

If all the above are present and conformant, the proposal passes. That's the entirety of the technical-acceptability test. Polishing past-performance write-ups beyond a one-paragraph project summary buys nothing extra.

### What doesn't matter (don't burn hours here)

1. **Past-performance prose beyond the bare minimum.** M.1.4.B.f says past performance is rated **Pass or Fail** ("no relevant past or present performance history shall receive the rating Fail rating"). Once we clear "Pass," more eloquent past-perf write-ups add no points.
2. **Technical-approach narratives, sequencing diagrams, schedule narratives, value-engineering deducts.** None of these are listed in Section L as a required submission item; none are scored in Section M. The QC plan is referenced at Section E but is operational, not evaluation-scoring.
3. **Differentiating qualifications.** LPTA does NOT reward differentiation. Two offerors both "Pass" past-performance with two relevant projects each are evaluated equally — the CO does not say "this one had four references and one was a federal job, so they win." The price decides.
4. **HUB / Small Business preference math.** This is a 100% Small Business set-aside. Within the set-aside pool, there is no further preference. Sub-tier HUB participation does NOT score (unlike TAMU System work).

## C. Pricing strategy — LPTA-thin

Goal: bid **as close to true cost + minimum defensible markup** as your risk tolerance allows.

### Markup envelope to target (recommended)

| Component | % of direct cost | Note |
|---|---|---|
| **Direct cost** (subs + materials + self-performed labor + minor incidentals) | 100% | Tighten this aggressively — site visit, sub quotes from ≥ 2 vendors per CLIN, no anchor padding |
| **General conditions / supervision** | 8–14% | Self-performed; counts toward 15% on-site self-perform |
| **Bonds (bid + P&P)** | 1.5–2.5% | Per surety quote |
| **Insurance** | 2–4% | If carried as a percentage rather than fixed-dollar |
| **DBA labor-burden uplift** | 0–4% | Only if firm's labor rates are sub-prevailing on any trade |
| **Contingency** | **3–5%** (LPTA-thin) | Down from typical 7–10% on a value-based bid. Site visit is the risk-buy-down |
| **Overhead** | 7–10% | Floor-of-range for a competitive LPTA |
| **Profit** | **3–6%** (LPTA-thin) | Up to 8% if you have a defensible past-performance edge that locks in a competitive moat; down to 3% if the local market is crowded |
| **Total markup over direct** | **≈ 25–40%** | A direct-cost-to-bid multiplier of roughly 1.25–1.40 |

### Recommended headline margin

**5–7% net profit at the bid envelope of $130K–$170K** is the LPTA-thin target. That preserves a viable margin (assuming takeoff is right) while staying competitive against other small-business offerors at the same magnitude.

**Don't pad above 7% net profit** unless you have a specific reason (long-lead OH-door market is tight, or your sub-quote came in higher than the cost-DB seeds) — every dollar over true cost gives the next offeror a window to underbid you.

**Don't drop below 3% net profit + 3% contingency** unless you accept the risk of losing money on a bad day. At this magnitude, a single bad takeoff (say, the roof footprint comes in 25% larger than measured) wipes out a 3-3-3 margin instantly.

### The math at three envelopes

| Bid envelope | Direct cost (back-calc) | Approximate net profit | Approximate $ at risk if takeoff is 20% off |
|---|---|---|---|
| **$130K (LPTA-aggressive)** | ~$95K | ~$5K (~4%) | -$19K — you LOSE $14K |
| **$160K (LPTA-thin balanced)** | ~$115K | ~$10K (~6%) | -$23K — you BREAK EVEN ± |
| **$200K (cautious / fat)** | ~$140K | ~$20K (~10%) | -$28K — you make ~$0 net |

→ Recommendation: **bid in the $150K–$170K range** unless the sub quotes come in materially under or over the seed assumptions.

### "Don't go too low" calibration

Per FAR 15.404-1(d), a CO can deem a price "**unreasonably low**" and reject the offer as non-responsible if the price is below the government's independent estimate to a degree that suggests the contractor cannot perform. The magnitude range is $100K–$250K. **Bidding below $100K is asking to be ruled non-responsible.** Stay above the floor by at least a few percent.

## D. Allocation across the 8 CLINs

LPTA scoring is on the **total evaluated price**, not on per-CLIN unit prices, so the allocation across CLINs is internal — but the unit prices must be defensible if the CO requests cost or pricing information (L.2.1.1.B says "if the contracting officer determines that adequate price competition no longer exists offerors may be required to submit information other than cost or pricing data").

**Defensible allocation:**

- Spread OH+P proportionally to direct cost (the "honest" allocation in `05-bid-schedule-mapping.md` § F)
- Avoid extreme front-loading (heavily weighting CLINs that are completed early to maximize cash-flow) — front-loading is detectable and is one of the things a CO flags as "not balanced" under FAR 15.404-1(g)(2). For a 60-day fixed-price project this is rarely a problem, but don't push it.
- Avoid zero or near-zero CLINs (M.1.9 says blanks reject; near-zeros suggest unbalanced bidding)

## E. The technical-acceptability gate (Pass requirements)

Per L.2.1.1–L.2.1.5 and M.1.4.B, here is the EXACT minimum to Pass:

1. **SF 1442 fully signed** (blocks 14–20C)
2. **All amendments acknowledged** (SF 30s if any)
3. **All 8 CLINs priced** with unit price + extended amount + grand total
4. **Past Performance** — minimum **2 examples** of recent (last 3 years) general construction projects similar in size, scope, and complexity to the SOW. Owner contact, dollar value, completion date. **Subcontractor past performance counts equally** (M.1.4.B.f) — include OH-door + metal-roof sub references if your firm's own past projects are thin.
5. **Bondability proof** — surety commitment letter for $250K-magnitude P&P bonds
6. **SAM Reps & Certs** — current per 52.204-8(d). State in proposal that 52.204-8(d) is invoked.
7. **Bid Guarantee** — bond / cert check / cashier's check / letter of credit per 52.228-1
8. **No exceptions** to the SOW or specs in the priced proposal

**That's it.** Nothing else moves the Pass/Fail needle. A 1-page Past Performance Questionnaire, 1-page bondability letter, 1-page SAM reference, and the SF 1442 + bid bond + priced Section B is a fully-conforming Part II.

## F. What to write in the proposal narrative (Part II)

Keep Part II tight. The agent's recommendation: **3 pages, maximum**.

1. **Page 1 — Past Performance** (per L.2.1.3.(a))
 - Brief firm identification (name, UEI, CAGE, NAICS, size status)
 - 2–3 past projects in a table: owner, owner contact email + phone, project title, scope summary (one sentence), $ value, completion date, contract type (federal preferred but commercial OK if comparable)
 - One short paragraph (3–4 sentences) for each project tying the scope to the San Marcos SOW (e.g., "Replaced 4 overhead doors on a similar shop-rehab in Hays Co. for [owner], $X, completed [date]; project included gas-line modifications and 24-ga metal-roof replacement").
 - If subcontractor past performance is being borrowed (M.1.4.B.f), 1 paragraph per critical sub with their similar project.
2. **Page 2 — Bondability** (per L.2.1.3.(b))
 - Insert the surety's bondability letter on surety letterhead, signed
 - One paragraph: surety name, surety's position on Treasury Circular 570, single-project capacity, aggregate capacity headroom against current backlog
3. **Page 3 — SAM Reps & Certs reference** (per L.2.1.4)
 - Statement: "Offeror confirms that the representations and certifications under FAR 52.204-8 are current, accurate, and complete as of the date of this offer, and have been entered electronically in SAM at https://www.sam.gov. Offeror invokes incorporation by reference per FAR 4.1201 and 52.204-8(d). No changes are made for purposes of this solicitation."
 - SAM record dump or CAGE/UEI block

**Resist the urge** to add a 5-page approach narrative, a Gantt chart, a safety plan, a QC plan, or a "Why us" pitch. None of it adds Pass-points; some of it could add unintended exceptions that trip a Fail.

## G. Negative-narrative risks (things that could push the bid into Fail)

Be alert: an LPTA bid can also fail by overcommunication. Watch for:

1. **Exceptions / qualifications / clarifications buried in narrative.** Don't say "We assume X" if X is a deviation from the SOW. If you genuinely cannot bid without an assumption, raise it as an RFI BEFORE submission; if not addressed, document in a SEPARATE cover-letter exception list that the CO can choose to accept (and may not). L.2.1.1.C is explicit on this.
2. **Conditional pricing** ("our price is $X assuming Y") — fails M.1.10.
3. **Optional alternates** — fails LPTA basis (not requested in the bid schedule).
4. **Past-performance project that's too dissimilar.** A $5M ground-up academic building reference signals "this contractor will treat us like a nuisance" on a $200K rehab. Lead with comparable size + scope, not biggest-and-best.
5. **Adverse past performance you didn't address.** M.1.7 explicitly says the CO may ask offerors to "respond to adverse past performance information." If the firm has any unresolved CPARS adverse-performance flags in the federal record, **proactively address them** in the past-performance write-up (one sentence: what happened, what was done, current status) rather than leaving the CO to surface them.
6. **Saying anything in the narrative that the CO could read as "we're not sure if we can meet a SOW requirement."** That's an exception. If you can't meet 24-ga panel + 20-year warranty, raise an RFI; if you can't get it answered before submission, walk away.

## H. Post-submission

- **No discussions are anticipated** (M.1.8). The CO reserves the right to ask clarifications on minor or clerical errors, but **proposal revisions are not expected.** Send the cleanest version possible the first time.
- The CO may award without discussions; the bid is locked at submission. There's no opportunity to drop price after award is initiated.
- If you finish ahead of the lowest bidder and theirs gets a Fail on past performance / bondability, you can advance. M.1.6 says the CO will continue down the price-ranked list until someone Passes. So if you bid second-low at a thin margin and the lowest-priced offeror has a SAM-registration problem or a thin past-performance package, the award could still come to you.

## I. Quick LPTA decision tree

```
Are SAM, bonding, past-performance, and SF 1442 all clean?
 ├─ No → STOP. Fix or walk away.
 └─ Yes →
    Did you measure roof + gutter + heater + fixture qty on site visit?
     ├─ No → STOP. Risk on takeoff is too high for LPTA-thin margin.
     └─ Yes →
        Did you get ≥ 2 sub quotes on CLINs 0001, 0005, 0006, 0007, 0008?
         ├─ No → Get them. Move on only with named sub-quote prices.
         └─ Yes →
            Compute direct cost + ~30% markup → that's your bid.
            Is bid above $100K (avoid "unreasonably low") and below $200K (stay in the magnitude window)?
             ├─ No → revisit subs and contingency
             └─ Yes → submit.
```
