## A. Assumptions baked in (until the site visit proves otherwise)

1. **Building footprint** — ~1,500–2,500 SF working assumption based on a single-story shop + 2-stall garage and the 416-SF ceiling-tile area (SOW §1.1.A.3). Carry **2,000 SF** as the working median. **MEASURE at site visit.**
2. **Existing substrate is sound** — SOW §4.2.A.2 says "Inspect underlayment metal joists to ensure they are not structurally compromised due to water damage. If damage is suspected, inform Refuge Manager before proceeding." Treat joist repair as **out of base scope**; carry as a Differing Site Conditions risk that triggers an equitable adjustment via 52.236-2 if encountered.
3. **No drawings exist** — per SOW Appendix 3 placeholder. The contractor measures on-site; the SOW + photos + amendments are the design basis.
4. **Site is civilian USFWS, normal hours 7 AM – 4:30 PM CT M–F** — no premium-time labor budget. Plan to fit all noisy / dusty work inside the window.
5. **Existing roof has the lean-to / awning on the south side that is EXCLUDED** from re-roofing (SOW §1.1.A.7). Measure only the main shop + 2-stall garage roof.
6. **Gas service is being permanently abandoned** (capped and removed) — implies the gas-fired unit heaters (CLIN 0008) are being removed without replacement. Electric / alternate heat is NOT in scope of this contract.
7. **Building is single-story, no roof-mounted equipment to remove first** — per SOW Appendix 2 photos. Verify at site visit (RTU? Solar? Vent stacks? Roof drains?).
8. **No hazmat budgeted** — pending site visit + RFI #7. If pre-1980 construction + transite panels visible, this flips to a Div 02 hazmat allowance.
9. **No site / yard work** — the contract is confined to the shop/garage building envelope. No paving, fencing, landscaping, or sitework.
10. **All work happens with the building unoccupied or with personnel re-located** — per the work-hours restriction in H.1.0 and the USFWS station-manager coordination requirement.

## C. Coordination notes (the pain-points worth pricing for)

1. **Site is a national fish hatchery.** Live fish-rearing facilities are sensitive to noise, dust, and water-quality disturbance. Plan to minimize roof tear-off debris fall (tarps, ground covers), and coordinate with Katherine Bockrath (Supervisory Biologist) on any work that creates dust or fumes near hatchery operations.
2. **Work hours 7 AM – 4:30 PM CT M–F only** (H.1.0). No premium-time labor in the base price. Plan crew loading + sub scheduling against this.
3. **60 calendar days NTP-to-completion is mandatory** (52.211-10 + SOW §3.3) — not negotiable. With a 7-day submittal review window (H.6.0) on roof panels, OH doors, personnel doors, and LED fixtures, the procurement phase alone is **~21 days** (10 days to submit + 7 days CO review + 4-day lag for shop-drawing turnaround on long-lead OH doors). Construction phase = ~40 days. **Critical path** = OH door lead time + metal roof panel lead time. Place orders the day after submittals are approved.
4. **15% on-site self-perform** (52.236-1). General conditions + supervision + ceiling demo + maybe gutters or personnel-door install — design the crew loading so > 15% of construction-cost is delivered with the firm's own employees on site, documented in certified payroll.
5. **Differing Site Conditions on the roof substrate** (52.236-2). The SOW explicitly cites this risk (§4.2.A.2). If joist damage is encountered, **stop, notify CO in writing, request equitable adjustment**. Do NOT keep going and absorb the cost.
6. **Gas-service permanent abandonment requires coordination with the local gas utility** (likely Atmos Energy or a Hays County co-op). The sub handles permits; the contractor needs to coordinate the cap window so the unit-heater removal can proceed within the same work day.
7. **Buy American** (52.225-9 + 52.225-10) on the roof panels, OH-door panels, gutter material, hollow-metal doors. 20% domestic-content price differential if non-domestic. Submit a 52.225-4 Buy American Certificate with the proposal.
8. **Final inspection notice = 10 days before desired final-inspection date** (RFP §E "Final Inspection"). Plan the closeout: punch-list ~5 days before substantial completion, final inspection on day 60.
9. **Warranties:** 1-year general (FAR 52.246-21), **20-year on metal roof panels** (SOW §3.4.B.2), **3-year door + 10-year finish on roll-up doors** (SOW §3.4.B.3). Manufacturer warranty paperwork to COR before final inspection.
10. **Daily inspector's log** (RFP G.3) — superintendent signs at end of every workday. Build the daily-log time into supervision budget.
11. **Submittals must come from the prime contractor with stamp + signature** — NOT directly from the sub or supplier (SOW §1.4.C). Build a few hours of submittal-stamp time into PM hours.

## D. Site-visit shopping list (May 27 or 28)

Bring:

- Tape measure (100' steel)
- Laser distance meter
- Camera (or phone with cloud-backup)
- IR thermometer (for any thermal anomalies on the existing roof, hints at insulation gaps)
- Moisture meter (especially for the roof deck if accessible)
- Roof-pitch finder / smart-level
- Notebook + sketch pad
- 8' step ladder OR confirm with FWS that an extension ladder will be onsite for roof access

Measure / photograph:

- Building footprint length × width (for roof SF)
- Roof slope / pitch
- Existing roof panel profile (so the replacement matches per §4.2.A.1) — width of pan + rib height + rib spacing
- Eave height + ridge height + ridge length + valleys (if any)
- Each OH-door opening: width × height
- Each personnel-door opening: width × height + frame depth + hinge side
- Ceiling area: confirm 416 SF or measure
- Gutter run: confirm ~110 LF; identify downspout locations + lengths
- Gas-meter location + meter type + pipe diameter into building
- Interior gas-pipe routing + length + fittings
- Each gas-fired unit heater: model #, capacity, mounting (suspended vs floor-mount), gas-supply orientation
- Existing electrical light fixtures: count, type (T8 fluorescent? HID?), mounting height, junction-box layout
- Electrical main + sub panel: photograph directory, breaker count, available capacity
- Any visible asbestos / lead-paint indicators (transite siding panels, painted surfaces with crazing/peeling on pre-1980 substrate)
- South-side lean-to / awning extents (so the excluded roof area can be precisely subtracted)

Ask the site contact (Katherine Bockrath):

- Confirm the Davis-Bacon WD number TX20260254 is the one in force (and look at the printed copy posted at the work site if available)
- Confirm whether facility manager (Refuge Manager) wants any specific staging area or restricted access zones
- Confirm whether any prior surveys exist for asbestos / lead paint / hazardous materials
- Confirm trash dumpster placement permitted location
- Confirm parking + delivery vehicle access route
- Confirm whether the Site Visit / Pre-bid sign-in sheet will be published with an amendment (it usually is)
