## C. How DBA rates flow to crew pricing

For each labor hour on the site:

1. Hourly cost to contractor = BHR + Fringe (paid as cash OR to a bona-fide plan) + Payroll burden (FICA, FUTA, SUTA, WC) + General insurance + Apprentice ratio adjustment + per-diem if applicable
2. Total burdened hourly cost is typically **~150–180% of BHR**, depending on fringe-handling and burden basis
3. Federal certified payroll (WH-347) reports BHR + Fringe separately; contractor must show fringe is properly delivered

Example math for an Electrician at $30.00 BHR + $10.00 Fringe (illustrative — replace with actual TX20260254 rates):

| Component | $/hr |
|---|---|
| BHR | $30.00 |
| Fringe (cash or plan) | $10.00 |
| FICA (7.65%) | $2.30 |
| FUTA + SUTA (1.5%) | $0.45 |
| Workers Comp (5–8% varies by class) | $1.80 |
| General insurance (1%) | $0.40 |
| **Subtotal: certified-payroll-reported wage** | **$44.95** |
| Equipment / vehicle allocation | $4–$8 |
| **Total burdened cost in cost-DB lookup** | **~$50–$53/hr** |

Confirm WC class codes for each trade with your insurance broker before pricing.

## D. Fringe-handling decision

Per 29 CFR 5.31, the contractor has 3 options for the fringe portion:

1. **Pay all of the fringe in cash** in the wage envelope (added to BHR) — simplest payroll, no need for a bona-fide plan. Most TX small contractors use this on small federal jobs.
2. **Pay all of the fringe to a bona-fide fringe-benefit plan** (health insurance, 401k, vacation, apprenticeship) — fringe dollars don't show in W-2 Box 1; effectively cheaper to the contractor on payroll taxes
3. **Split** — part to plan, part to cash

For a 60-day project with new hires unlikely to participate in long-vesting fringe plans, **option 1 (all cash) is the simplest and what most small TX contractors use.** Document this on each weekly certified payroll.

⚠️ **DO NOT** pay BHR + Fringe to the worker as a single rounded number and then back-derive — DOL requires the certified payroll to show BHR and Fringe as separate columns on WH-347.

## E. Apprentice / trainee rules (52.222-9)

- Apprentices may be paid at a percentage of the journeyman rate per their registered apprenticeship agreement (typically 50%–90% based on level/year)
- Apprentice must be enrolled in a registered DOL or Texas Workforce Commission apprenticeship program
- Apprentice-to-journeyman ratio on site must not exceed the ratio in the apprenticeship agreement
- Non-apprentices working as helpers must be paid the full journeyman rate for the trade

If the firm does not have apprentices, ignore — pay all crew at journeyman rates.

## F. Posting requirements at the work site

Per 29 CFR 1.6 + 29 CFR 5.5(a)(1)(ii):

- WD must be posted at the work site in a place accessible to workers (typically the job-site trailer or break area)
- **WH-1321 Notice** ("Notice to All Employees Working on Federal or Federally Financed Construction Projects") must be posted alongside
- DOL Fact Sheet #66 (Davis-Bacon Wage Rates) is a useful adjunct posting but not required

Both posters available at `https://www.dol.gov/agencies/whd/posters` — free download, no compliance-package vendor needed.

## G. Weekly certified payroll mechanics (52.222-8 + 52.222-10 Copeland Act)

- **Form:** WH-347 (or equivalent)
- **Frequency:** Weekly, 7 days after close of each payroll period (RFP H.15)
- **Recipient:** Contracting Officer (typically also COTR for visibility)
- **Required for prime AND every tier of subcontractor**
- **Statement of Compliance (WH-348)** signed by the contractor or sub-prime accompanying each weekly payroll
- **Anti-Kickback / Copeland Act:** any deduction other than statutorily required ones (FICA, federal income tax, state income tax) must have prior written authorization from the worker. NO kickbacks to the contractor on overtime, fringe, or any portion of the certified rate.

Build payroll-admin time into PM hours within general conditions.

## H. Truck driver DBA coverage (per H.16 + 29 CFR 5.2(j))

Per RFP H.16: DBA covers truck drivers ONLY for time spent:

1. Directly on the site of the work, OR
2. Hauling between the site of the work and an adjacent dedicated supplier facility

Long-haul material delivery (e.g., 24-ga roof panels trucked from Dallas) is NOT DBA-covered. Don't accidentally over-burden material freight in the cost basis — those drivers are paid commercial rates per their CDL employer.

If the firm self-performs material hauling with company-owned trucks, only the on-site portion + adjacent-yard portion is DBA. Document this in certified payroll under the "Truck Driver" classification with on-site hours only.
