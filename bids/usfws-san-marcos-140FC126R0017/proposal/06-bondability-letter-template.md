# Bondability letter template

> Source: FAR 52.228-15 (Performance and Payment Bonds — Construction) + RFP §L.2.1.3.(b) (p.65) + Treasury Department Circular 570 (`https://fiscal.treasury.gov/surety-bonds/circular-570.html`).
>
> **What this is:** A letter on **Treasury Circular 570–listed surety company letterhead**, signed by an underwriting officer of the surety, stating that the surety has reviewed the offeror's financial position and is prepared to issue performance and payment bonds at 100% of the contract price upon award. Required as part of Volume II per L.2.1.3.(b) and M.1.4.B.2 (bondability is one of the Pass/Fail technical-acceptability factors).
>
> **What this is NOT:** The bid bond itself (covered in `07-bid-bond-form-template.md`). The bid bond is a separate instrument also issued by the surety, accompanies Volume I, and guarantees that if the offer is accepted, the offeror will execute the contract and furnish the P&P bonds.

---

## A. Hard requirements for the letter

The CO will check each of these. A letter missing any one is not bondability evidence and the bid Fails on M.1.4.B.2:

- [ ] **Surety company letterhead** — not the broker's, not the firm's
- [ ] **Surety company name** explicitly stated
- [ ] **Surety listed on Treasury Circular 570** — confirm at `https://fiscal.treasury.gov/surety-bonds/circular-570.html` before requesting
- [ ] **Surety's NAIC code + state of domicile**
- [ ] **Surety's underwriting limit** (the per-bond authority on Circular 570) ≥ this contract magnitude — the underwriting limit must comfortably exceed $250K (T-570 limits are usually in the millions)
- [ ] **Statement of intent**: "Upon award of the referenced contract, [Surety Name] is prepared to issue Performance Bond and Payment Bond, each in the penal amount of 100% of the original contract price, on Standard Forms 25 and 25A respectively, naming [Offeror Name] as Principal and the United States of America as Obligee."
- [ ] **Solicitation number** referenced: 140FC126R0017
- [ ] **Project description** referenced: USFWS San Marcos ARC, Rehabilitate Shop & 2 Stall Garage Building
- [ ] **Magnitude / contract value** referenced: not to exceed $250,000 (or specific bid amount once locked)
- [ ] **Single-project bonding capacity** stated (must be ≥ contract magnitude — typically a multiple)
- [ ] **Aggregate bonding capacity** stated (must accommodate the firm's current bonded backlog + this project — typically a much larger figure)
- [ ] **Bid bond** also addressed in same letter or accompanying letter: "[Surety Name] has issued or is prepared to issue a Bid Bond on Standard Form 24 in the penal amount of 20% of the bid price or $1,000,000, whichever is less" (per FAR 52.228-1)
- [ ] **Attorney-in-fact signature** (NOT a broker signature) — the surety's authorized representative, with a Power of Attorney attached if the AIF is not a corporate officer
- [ ] **Letter dated** within 60 days of submission (preferably within 30 days)
- [ ] **Notarized** if surety / CO requires (most surety letters are not notarized; the bid bond and P&P bonds ARE; check with surety)

## C. Lead time

- **Order date:** Monday 2026-05-24 (with `../outreach/04-email-bonding-agent.md`)
- **Surety underwriting:** 3–5 business days for known clients with current financials on file; 7–14 business days if surety needs updated financials, in-force bond schedule, or other underwriting items
- **In-hand date target:** **Wednesday 2026-06-17** (5 business days before submission)
- **Hard latest date:** Friday 2026-06-19 (1 business day before submission; 0 buffer)

If the surety can't issue by 6/19, the bid is at risk. Escalate to a second surety relationship if the firm has one.

## D. Companion documents the surety also produces

The bondability letter is one of three deliverables from the surety:

| Document | Purpose | Form | Where it lives in the proposal |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Bondability letter** (this file) | Demonstrates surety's commitment per L.2.1.3.(b) | Surety letterhead | Volume II |
| **Bid Bond** | The bid guarantee per FAR 52.228-1 | SF 24 | Volume I (scanned PDF; original may need to follow physically) |
| **P&P bond commitment letter (optional, often combined with #1)** | Reiterates P&P commitment at 100% | Surety letterhead | Volume II (paragraphs 2 and 3 of the template above cover this) |

Some sureties issue separate letters for items 1 and 3; some combine. The template above combines them. Either format is acceptable as long as the substantive elements are present.

## E. Verification before insertion into Volume II

- [ ] Letterhead is the surety company's, not the broker's
- [ ] Surety is listed at `https://fiscal.treasury.gov/surety-bonds/circular-570.html`
- [ ] Surety's underwriting limit ≥ contract magnitude
- [ ] Solicitation number 140FC126R0017 referenced
- [ ] All four bond commitments (bid + performance + payment + capacity) addressed
- [ ] Signed by attorney-in-fact (not broker)
- [ ] Power of Attorney attached
- [ ] Letter dated within 30 days of submission
- [ ] No exceptions, conditions, or hedges in the letter language
