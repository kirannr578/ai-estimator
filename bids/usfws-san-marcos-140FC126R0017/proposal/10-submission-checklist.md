- [ ] Volume I PDF total size ≤ 10 MB (federal email gateways often reject > 10 MB; if Volume I + scanned bid bond + amendments exceeds 10 MB, split into Volume I-a and Volume I-b with clear filenames and note in body)

- [ ] PDFs open correctly in Acrobat Reader DC (test on a different machine from the one that produced them)
- [ ] No tracked-changes markup, no metadata indicating "draft", no unresolved comments

## D. If the package is delayed / can't be sent

This is a hard deadline (5:00 PM EDT, Monday 2026-06-22). The late-bid exceptions in FAR 52.215-1(c)(3)(ii) are narrow:

| Scenario | Exception applies? |
|---|---|
| Offeror's email server has an outage | ❌ No — offeror responsible |
| Internet outage at offeror's office | ❌ No — offeror responsible |
| Federal government email server has an outage that prevents receipt | ✅ Possibly — document and contact CO |
| Solicitation is amended within 24 hours of deadline and offeror needs time to respond | ✅ Possibly — RFP must be amended to extend the deadline |
| Force majeure (natural disaster, etc.) | ✅ Possibly — but the CO must accept the exception |

**Practical mitigation:**

1. **Send by 4:30 PM EDT** (30-minute buffer)
2. **Have two send paths:** corporate email + offeror's personal/secondary domain email as backup
3. **Have two send devices:** office computer + mobile hotspot + tablet/phone
4. **Print a paper copy of Volume I + Volume II + bid bond** and keep at the office; if all-electronic fails, the CO may accept a paper hand-deliver (very rare, but covers the catastrophic case)

### Alternative submission via SAM.gov upload

`[VERIFY VS RFP SECTION L]` — The RFP does **not** appear to enable SAM.gov direct upload as an accepted alternative submission method. RFP p.3 + L.1.2 both specify email. Do **not** rely on SAM.gov upload as the primary or backup submission method without first confirming with the CO that it is acceptable for this specific solicitation. If in doubt before 6/22, raise as a clarification RFI before 6/8.
