## A. What the RFP literally requires

Per L.2.1.3.(a) (verbatim or close paraphrase from RFP p.65):

> "The Offeror shall provide a minimum of **two (2) examples of recent (last 3 years) general construction projects similar in size, scope, and complexity** to the Statement of Work. For each example, include:
> - Owner name + contact (name, title, email, phone)
> - Project title + brief scope description
> - Dollar value of the project
> - Project completion date
> - Contract type (federal, state, commercial)"

Per M.1.4.B (paraphrased):

> "Past performance evaluation considers the offeror's record of performance on similar contracts. **Subcontractor past performance counts equally** for any sub performing a major or critical aspect of the requirement. The CO may obtain past-performance information from **Government and commercial sources** beyond what the offeror submits (CPARS, FAPIIS, FPDS-NG, prior owner contacts)."

Per M.1.4.B.f:

> "**No relevant past or present performance history shall receive a Fail rating.**"

Translation: any 2 relevant projects → Pass. Zero relevant projects → Fail. Three or five relevant projects → also Pass, same as two.

---

## B. "Similar in size, scope, and complexity" — how to read it

| Dimension | San Marcos benchmark | "Similar" interpretation |
|---|---|---|
| **Size (contract value)** | Magnitude $100K–$250K (this project) | Past projects in **$50K–$500K** range. A $5M ground-up academic building is *not* similar in size and signals "this contractor will treat us like a nuisance" on a $200K rehab |
| **Scope** | Commercial / institutional building rehab — doors, roofing, light electrical, gutters, gas-line modifications, ceiling demo | Past projects with overlap on ≥ 2 of: overhead doors, metal roofing, hollow metal doors, light fixtures, gutters, gas-line cap/remove, ceiling demo |
| **Complexity** | Federal Davis-Bacon, Buy American, certified payroll, IPP invoicing, 60-day fixed schedule, 4–6 specialty subs coordinated | Past **federal or state-funded** projects preferred (DBA exposure, certified-payroll experience). Pure commercial work without prevailing-wage exposure is acceptable but weaker. |

The strongest reference is a **prior federal small-construction renovation in Texas, $100K–$300K, with overlapping trades and Davis-Bacon compliance**. The second-strongest is a state-funded or large-commercial renovation with similar trades. **Avoid leading with brand-name big-dollar projects that are size-mismatched.**

## E. Pre-submission reference-hygiene checklist

- [ ] Each owner contact contacted in advance (target: by **2026-06-15**) and warned that USFWS may call between June 22 and award. Confirm the contact still works at the same org with the same email/phone.
- [ ] Each reference has been asked: "Would you give us a positive recommendation if asked?" If the answer is anything other than an unqualified yes, **swap the reference**.
- [ ] CPARS / FAPIIS / FPDS-NG check by the firm's contracts admin — verify nothing has changed in the past 3 months. `[USER TO VERIFY]`
- [ ] If any reference is at the same federal agency contacting them (USFWS specifically), confirm the contact is not currently in a position of conflict with the CO Tracy Gamble or CS Drew Ferrall.
- [ ] No reference older than 36 months from June 22, 2026 — that's calendar dates June 22, 2023 onward for "recent" per L.2.1.3.(a)
- [ ] For each reference, the contract type matches "general construction" — design-build or pure A/E commissions are weaker fits and should be backed by a stronger second reference

---

## F. What NOT to include

- ❌ More than 3 projects (extra projects don't score; they add CO reading time and edge-case dilution risk)
- ❌ Photos, brochures, or case-study layouts (not requested)
- ❌ Project narratives longer than 1 paragraph each
- ❌ Pricing for past projects beyond the dollar-value field
- ❌ References from > 36 months ago, even if the work was excellent
- ❌ References where the owner contact has left the organization or won't respond
- ❌ References without a working email and phone
- ❌ Subcontractor references if the firm's own 2 prime references already establish similar-scope past performance

---

## G. Reminder: Pass/Fail, not scored

> "No relevant past or present performance history shall receive a Fail rating." — M.1.4.B.f

Two solid references = Pass. Three solid references = Pass (no extra credit). Five solid references with photos and case-study narratives = still Pass (and now you've spent 8 hours on something that buys nothing).
