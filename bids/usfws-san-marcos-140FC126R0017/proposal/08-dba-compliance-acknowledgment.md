# Davis-Bacon Act compliance acknowledgment

> Source: FAR 52.222-6 (Construction Wage Rate Requirements) + 52.222-8 (Payrolls & Basic Records) + 52.222-10 (Copeland Act) + RFP §H.15.0 + §H.16.0 + DBA WD TX20260254 (Hays County, Building Construction).
>
> **Why include this in Volume II?** Strictly, this acknowledgment is not required at proposal stage — DBA compliance attaches automatically when the contract is awarded. However, including a signed 1-page acknowledgment in Volume II:
>
> 1. Demonstrates to the CO that the offeror has read and understood the DBA requirements (a soft-positive on the Responsibility-Matters review under FAR 9.104)
> 2. Documents the offeror's election on fringe-handling (cash vs. plan), removing a routine post-award question
> 3. Pre-commits the offeror's payroll lead to the certified-payroll cadence and form (WH-347), which the CO will be looking for in the first 2 weeks of performance
>
> Total burden: 1 page, 1 signature. Worth including.

## C. What the CO will want to see in the first 2 weeks of performance

If awarded, the CO will look for:

| Item | When | Form |
|---|---|---|
| First weekly certified payroll | 7 days after first work week | WH-347 + WH-348 |
| Posted WD + WH-1321 at job site | First day of performance | n/a |
| Apprenticeship agreements (if applicable) | Before first apprentice on site | DOL or TWC registered agreement |
| Sub-tier compliance flow-down language | At sub-contract execution | Sub contract incorporates 52.222-6 through 52.222-15 |
| Truck-driver classification methodology | First payroll with on-site truck driver hours | Documented in payroll narrative |

Pre-stage these in the firm's project management system as task templates so the first 2 weeks of performance go smoothly. The CO's first DBA touch-point is the field inspector's review of the WH-347 / WH-348 forms; getting this clean early sets the tone for the project.
