## Why this opportunity (BPC fit — refined post-enrichment)

1. **Scope fit — STRONG, confirmed by spec book.** Spec book divisions present are **02 (demo only) / 03 (misc CIP slab patch) / 06 (rough carp + plam casework) / 07 (insulation/firestop/sealants) / 08 (steel frames + wood doors + hardware + fire-rated glazing) / 09 (drywall + finishes incl. fluid-applied chemical-resistant flooring) / 10 (specialties) / 12 (quartz countertops + entrance mats) / 22 (plumbing) / 23 (HVAC) / 26 (electrical)**. No exterior, structural, sitework, fire sprinkler, or low-voltage-systems scope. BPC self-perform lane (GWB, paint, resilient flooring) + manage-via-subs lane (plam casework, plumbing, HVAC, electrical, glazing, doors/hardware, firestopping) maps cleanly. Three quoting strategies remain viable — see [`06-scope-outline.md`](./06-scope-outline.md).
2. **Past-performance fit.** Strongest exemplar is **Hindu Temple of Southlake** (institutional finish-out, Assembly A-3, ~10,700 SF — interior demo + finishes + partitions in an occupied institutional building). Holiday Inn Hall Park (commercial finish) + cumulative SFH portfolio (drywall + paint + flooring + tile) round out a 3-pack.
3. **Magnitude — still unknown directly** (no engineer's-estimate published). 414-page spec book + 37-sheet drawing set + plastic-laminate casework + chemical-resistant fluid floor + Alternate 1 lighting/finish package suggest a **mid-sized interior renovation** in the $300K–$1M total range. BPC's sub package would be a fraction; price the BPC-self-perform + managed-sub portion only.
4. **Geographic stretch.** Waco is ~95 mi south of Frisco — at the edge of BPC's "North Texas + central Texas" radius per `firm/firm-profile.json → trade_capabilities.service_radius`. Drive 1.5 hr each way. Workable for a phase-of-work super assignment, not for daily visits. Note: **selection criterion (e) "office location close to college" is worth 5%** of HCS's MCC scoring — BPC at 95 mi loses points to Waco-domiciled GCs at this criterion, irrespective of price.
5. **Process fit — confirmed.** The Owner's Proposal Form (Section 004213) is now in hand. It is a **single-lump-sum CSP form** to MCC — not a line-item form. HCS's "line item / alternate / unit price" framing is HCS's *internal* sub-template, not the MCC form. BPC's normal quote structure (line-itemized with takeoffs) feeds HCS's roll-up cleanly.
6. **Federal-funding signal.** Spec §1.17 lists CWHSSA + EEO + EPCA + EOAA — implies **Davis-Bacon Act prevailing wages apply** (Section 007343 in spec book ToC). Increases the labor-cost burden meaningfully on self-perform trades; price labor against TX McLennan County DBA wage determination, not BPC's market labor rates.
