## 4. If similar opportunity arises, what to track differently

### Track 1: City of Garland Housing & Community Services Department weekly monitoring

- The City of Garland Housing & Community Services Department issues residential rehabilitation RFBs on a **recurring basis** (typical: multiple per quarter)
- Track at: `https://www.garlandtx.gov/government/departments/housing-community-services` (or equivalent municipal procurement portal)
- Cadence: **weekly check** during active program periods (typically year-round)
- Subscribe to the City of Garland procurement email list if available

### Track 2: DFW-area municipal rehab program monitoring

- Similar residential rehabilitation programs are issued by:
  - **City of McKinney** (BPC already has preferred-vendor status)
  - **City of Dallas Office of Housing and Community Investment**
  - **City of Plano** Department of Housing & Community Services
  - **City of Frisco** Department of Community Development
  - **City of Garland** Housing & Community Services
- Set up bi-weekly check across this set
- Many of these programs are CDBG-funded and have predictable annual budget cycles

### Track 3: HUD CDBG-funded contractor list registration

- Register on local-CDBG contractor lists where applicable (City of Dallas, City of Garland)
- This lets BPC receive direct notifications when new RFBs are released under CDBG programs

### Track 4: Federal Federal Funding Opportunity (FFO) keyword search

- Many local-municipal residential rehab programs are HUD-funded; keyword searches on grants.gov / sam.gov for "rehabilitation," "weatherization," "housing" with TX locality can surface related federal grants and local-program announcements

### Track 5: Set up a "DFW municipal rehab" Google Alert

- Google Alert for keywords like "Garland Texas housing rehabilitation" or "DFW residential rehabilitation grant" can catch news articles and program announcements ahead of formal RFB postings
