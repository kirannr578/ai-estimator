# NO-GO Memo: UT Dallas ECE Bootcamp Education Offerings (RFP UTD20260301-AN)

**Decision date:** 2026-05-30
**Opportunity close date:** 2026-06-19 17:00 CDT (open at decision time)
**Verdict:** NO-GO (industry mismatch — non-credit education services, not construction)

---

## 1. What it was

The University of Texas at Dallas **RFP UTD20260301-AN** — *Extend ECE Bootcamp Education Offerings*. Scope is "non-credit, bootcamp-style education courses (workforce development, career advancement, re-skilling, up-skilling)" delivered by a vendor partner to UTD's Erik Jonsson School of Engineering and Computer Science (ECE = Electrical and Computer Engineering, or possibly Engineering and Computer Science). Vendor portal `https://utdallas.bonfirehub.com`. VetHUB participation goal 0.0%. Question cutoff Friday May 29, 2026 (already past at decision time). Proposal due June 19, 2026 5:00 PM CDT.

## 2. Why no-go

**Primary reason: BPC is a construction firm; the scope is non-credit education services.**

The RFP solicits a vendor to **deliver education / workforce-development courses** (curriculum, instructors, course delivery, possibly LMS integration). NAICS for this scope is in the **611xxx (Educational Services)** or **541xxx (Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services — Training)** family — entirely outside BPC's construction NAICS list (236xxx / 238xxx).

BPC has:
- No curriculum-development capability
- No teaching / instructional staff
- No relevant past performance in education services
- No NAICS 611xxx / 541xxx representation in SAM

**Secondary considerations:**
- The question cutoff (May 29, 2026 5:00 PM CDT) is **already past at decision time (2026-05-30)** — even if BPC could deliver the scope, the diligence window is closed.
- Proposal portal is Bonfire (UTD's vendor system), which BPC would need to register on. Not worth registering if BPC will not bid.

## 3. Did this fit BPC?

**No (industry mismatch).**

| Criterion | Match | Reasoning |
|---|:---:|---|
| Scope match | ✗ | Education / workforce-development services, not construction or facility services |
| NAICS match | ✗ | RFP is in NAICS 611xxx / 541xxx range; BPC's NAICS list is 236xxx / 238xxx / 561790 |
| Geographic match | ✓ | UT Dallas is in Richardson, TX — in BPC's core DFW radius (irrelevant given scope mismatch) |
| Past-performance fit | ✗ | BPC has no education / training delivery past performance |
| Set-aside / VetHUB | n/a | VetHUB goal stated 0.0% — not a positioning lever |

**Conclusion: This RFP is in a different industry. No path to a credible BPC response.**

## 4. If similar opportunity arises, what to track differently

### Track 1 — UT Dallas Bonfire is still useful for BPC's actual lane

UT Dallas posts construction / facility-related RFPs on the same Bonfire portal (`https://utdallas.bonfirehub.com`). **Subscribe BPC's vendor profile to UTD Bonfire** for NIGP / commodity codes that map to NAICS 236220 (Commercial & Institutional Building Construction) and the 238 trade subsets BPC self-performs. This catches future UTD facility-renovation, lab-renovation, classroom-renovation, dorm-renovation work — that's BPC's actual lane.

### Track 2 — Recognize "RFP" is a procurement-instrument label, not a scope label

DFWMSDC digests forward all kinds of RFPs from member-organization procurement teams, not all of them construction. When triaging, **read the scope statement before treating an RFP as a construction opportunity**. Add this to the intake convention.

### Track 3 — UTD Small Business Office contact is a useful relationship

`reginald.cleveland@utdallas.edu` (UTD Small Business Office) is the diversity-outreach POC at UTD. Even though BPC won't bid this specific RFP, UTD's SBO is the point of contact for **future construction set-aside / diversity-targeted opportunities** at UTD. Worth a one-touch introduction email (separate from this no-go) so BPC is on UTD's radar for future facility work.
