## Notes for sender

- Send Monday 2026-05-25 EOD. Memorial Day (Tuesday 5/26) is a federal holiday but bonding agents typically still respond to email on holidays.
- Bonding-agent lead time for **known clients**: 3–5 BD.
- Bonding-agent lead time for **new clients**: 10–15 BD — would not clear the Wed 6/3 deadline. **If the firm is new to bonding, escalate immediately and consider Bid Bond alternatives** (Irrevocable Letter of Credit per FAR 52.228-14, certified check at $95K — though impractical).
- Reserve **$500K capacity** (mid-target + ~5% headroom).
- Provide the bondability-letter template (proposal `06-bondability-letter-template.md`) to surety.
- Pursue a parallel sub-tier outreach to confirm the firm's existing surety relationship is current and working — sometimes firms discover at this stage that their surety has changed underwriters.

## Follow-up cadence

| Day | Action |
|---|---|
| Mon 5/25 EOD | Send email; confirm receipt by phone follow-up |
| Wed 5/27 | Surety acknowledges; underwriting begins |
| Fri 5/29 | Surety provides quote on bid-bond premium + bondability letter draft |
| Mon 6/1 | Final bid amount provided to surety; SF 24 in production |
| Wed 6/3 EOD | SF 24 issued (wet-ink) + bondability letter on letterhead |
| Thu 6/4 | Scanned bid bond attached to submission email; wet-ink original retained at firm + at surety |

## If the firm is new to bonding

Refer to the proposal `07-bid-bond-form-template.md` § E for FAR 52.228-1 alternatives:
- Irrevocable Letter of Credit (FAR 52.228-14) — bank-issued; faster than new-surety underwriting
- Treasury bonds or notes — uncommon, requires Treasury coordination
- Cashier's check at $95K — practical only if firm has unrestricted cash
