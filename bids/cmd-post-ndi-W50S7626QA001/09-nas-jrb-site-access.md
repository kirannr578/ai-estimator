# NAS/JRB Fort Worth site access — distinct logistics document

> **\[FILENAME NOTE\]** The user task brief asked for `09-tx-arng-site-access.md`. The contracting + user agency is **136th Airlift Wing, Texas AIR National Guard (TXANG, NOT TXARNG)**, performing work at **Naval Air Station Joint Reserve Base Fort Worth** — a Navy-installation-command Joint Reserve Base hosting multiple service components. Site access is governed by **NAS-JRB Fort Worth installation rules**, not Camp Mabry / Texas Army Guard. Filename and content reflect that correction. See `01-overview.md` § "Owner / contracting agency" for the corrected agency identity.

This document is the operational handbook for "How does a contractor crew (and its sub crews) actually get on the jobsite?" Mistakes here delay NTP, delay submittals, delay subs; getting it right is the single most common source of small-federal-renovation schedule slip.

## B. Base-access lead time — 14 BUSINESS days

Verbatim from SOW §7:
> *"The Contractor is responsible for obtaining and maintaining all necessary base access for its personnel, including subcontractors. The Contractor shall submit all base access requests to the Government point of contact **no less than fourteen (14) business days prior to the required access date**. The Government is not responsible for delays related to processing base access requests."*

This is **14 business days, not calendar days, not "two weeks"**. With the standard NAS-JRB business calendar Mon–Fri, 14 business days ≈ 18–19 calendar days (accounting for weekends and federal holidays).

### B.1 Backwards-from-bid-close conflict

Counting backwards from 04 JUN 2026 (Wed, bid close):
- 14 business days back = approximately Tue 2026-05-13
- **Today is Sat 2026-05-23** → submitting a base-access request today gets earliest access ≈ Wed 2026-06-10 (after bid close)

**Conclusion: a STANDARD-RULE pre-bid site visit is not possible.**

### B.2 Mitigation strategies (in order of preference)

1. **Request an exception via the 136 CES Operations Managers** (MSgt Jones + MSgt Meeks per SOW §13.4–13.5). Frame it as: *"We are an ANG-experienced small business intending to submit a competitive comparative-evaluation quote for W50S7626QA001 on or before 04 JUN 2026. Our SOW §4.3 review and Drawing Sheet 2 review are largely complete from the bid documents alone, but a 30-minute escorted walk through B1672 and B1675 in the next 5 business days would lock our pricing precision for the comparative-evaluation submission. We acknowledge SOW §7 normally requires 14 business days advance; we are requesting a one-time accelerated processing of our access request given the 12-calendar-day bid window. We are happy to bring no more than 2 personnel; we have a current SAM-registered SAM Active UEI; we are flexible on Tuesday–Friday 0630–1715 per SOW §11."* Send to BOTH MSgt Jones and MSgt Meeks; copy CO (Capt Crabtree) + PE (Maj Norton) + PM (David Shamburger). See `outreach/02-email-136-aw-facility-poc.md`.
2. **Request a virtual / telephonic Q&A with the 136 CES Project Manager** (David Shamburger, 817-852-3328) covering the existing-conditions specifics of B1672 and B1675 — fire-protection system age + manufacturer, HVAC controls platform, electrical-panel capacity headroom, AFFF-decommissioning documentation existence. This may resolve 60–70% of the field-verification gap without an actual site visit.
3. **Use the existing-conditions photo PDFs** (`Alter CP and NDI_B1675 NDI Rm Photos REFERENCE ONLY.pdf` (3.5 MB) + `Altter CP and NDI_B1672 CP Rm Photos REFERENCE ONLY.pdf` (72.5 MB)) for visual context. 4. **Use Google Earth / publicly available imagery** of NAS-JRB Fort Worth to confirm building 1672 + 1675 footprints, access routes, parking areas, and proximity to active flightline.
5. **If exception denied: bid on field-verified-after-award basis** with a ~5–8% added contingency on direct cost; document in the offer that *"Field investigation per FAR 52.236-3 will be performed within 5 business days post-NTP. Any Differing Site Conditions per FAR 52.236-2 will be addressed by contract modification."*

### C.1 Information needed for each contractor / sub worker

For each person requesting access, plan to provide (verify exact list with 136 CES Operations):

- Full legal name (matching state-issued ID)
- Date of birth
- Social Security Number — last 4 (full SSN may be required for long-term pass background check)
- Driver's license / state ID number + state issuing
- Vehicle make + model + license plate (if driving onto base)
- Firm name + CAGE code + UEI
- Project number (DDPM262101) + contract number (post-award)
- Sub trade (electrical / HVAC / etc.) for visibility
- Government POC name (CO Capt Crabtree or PE Maj Norton)
- Any escort-required notation per badge type

### C.2 Reasons for access denial (SOW §7.1)

Per SOW §7.1, the installation may deny access to individuals with:

1. Active arrest warrants or felony convictions
2. Illegal drug involvement
3. Registration on a sexual offender registry
4. Convictions / charges related to disrespect for the law
5. Lack of proper documentation for non-U.S. citizens

**Pre-screen all crews before submission of access request** — denial mid-cycle disrupts schedule. Document each crew member's eligibility (firm-internal HR file). For non-U.S. citizens (e.g., legal-permanent residents on sub crews), confirm I-9 and additional documentation requirements with the VCC in advance.

## F. Pre-award vs. post-award access

| Phase | Mechanism |
|---|---|
| **Pre-award (now through 04 JUN)** | Bidder may request **escorted site visit** if SOW §4.3 site-familiarization is needed — subject to SOW §7 14-business-day rule, **exception requested**. |
| **Pre-award technical Q&A** | Email RFI to `136.AW.MSC@us.af.mil` per Section L (f). |
| **Award notice through NTP** | Post-award; PM coordinates with 136 CES Operations + VCC for crew badging using long-term passes. |
| **Performance phase** | All crews badged via long-term passes (30-day renewable). PM submits weekly badge-roster updates. Sub crews coordinated with same lead time + same SOW §7 14-business-day rule. |
| **Closeout phase** | Final acceptance walk per SOW §4.9. Government may coordinate post-final-acceptance access for as-built submission, AF 3000 closeout, etc. |

## G. Special considerations — secure-area construction

The B1672 Command Post will host classified work post-renovation (per drawing notes — Classified Work in Progress signage, mantrap, anti-access ducting bars). **During construction**, the area should be uncleared and standard contractor access applies. **Special considerations during construction:**

1. **Coordinate work in B1672 with the existing CP function in B4175** — the legacy CP must remain operational until the new CP is ready. Renovation timing must avoid any conflict. **\[VERIFY with PE Norton\]**.
2. **No classified material handling by contractor.** All classified material is presumed to have been removed prior to renovation. If anything classified is encountered (papers, drives, devices) — **STOP WORK + notify CO** immediately, do not move or dispose.
3. **Hardware delivery security** — Stanley/Best Lock-Core cores and electronic-lock kits should be received by Govt-witnessed delivery. Cores must NOT be keyed to any other facility's master keying system inadvertently — coordinate with 136 CES.
4. **Demolition documentation** — photograph existing conditions before demo (where permitted) to preserve as-built record for Government's facility historian.
5. **Drop-ceiling enclosure work** is sensitive — bars on ducting + returns must be installed AFTER ceiling reinstall to allow inspection from above. Coordinate sequence with 136 CES inspections.

## J. Common pitfalls + how to avoid them

| Pitfall | Avoidance |
|---|---|
| Showing up at VCC without prior access request submitted | NEVER do this. SOW §7 requires advance request. |
| Submitting personnel data via unencrypted email | Use NAS-JRB-approved secure-transfer method or encrypted attachment. \[VERIFY with VCC\] |
| Forgetting that base business hours are Tue–Fri (not Mon–Fri) | All scheduling reflects 4-day workweek; Saturday work needs 1-week CO advance |
| Bringing weapons, alcohol, drugs onto base | Termination + criminal liability — brief crews + sub crews |
| Photography of flightline / sensitive ops | Confirm with 136 CES what's permitted at the jobsite |
| Sub crews showing up without badging | Sub names submitted ≥ 14 business days in advance |
| Showing up in vehicle with firearm in glove box | Vehicle searched at gate; permanent denial of base access |
