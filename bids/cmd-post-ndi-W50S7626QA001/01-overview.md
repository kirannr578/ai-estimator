> ⚠️ Pricing nuance: this is a **secure-controlled-access** Command Post but **not a SCIF**. The drawing notes call out mantrap, credential window, classified-work-in-progress signal lights, and bars-on-ducting/returns to prevent secure-space penetration — which means **commercial-secure-area construction** standards (escort-only access, hardware compliance, controlled finishes), not a TEMPEST / ICD 705-accredited SCIF build. The user's original framing of "potential SCIF / TEMPEST / cleared personnel" appears not to apply to this scope per the drawings + SOW. Field-verify at site visit; if the post-award scope expands to SCIF accreditation, that is a Differing Site Conditions claim under FAR 52.236-2.

### CLIN 0002 — Building 1675 Nondestructive Inspection (NDI) Room

Verbatim from Section B:
> *"NONDESTRUCTIVE INSPECTION (NDI) ROOM: The contractor shall furnish all labor, parts, tools, plant, equipment, appliances, transportation, and materials necessary to convert a decommissioned Aqueous Film Forming Foam (AFFF) room in Building 1675 to a NDI Room as specified in the Statement of Work (SOW) and corresponding attachments."*

Per SOW §1.2: *"Establish a Nondestructive Inspection (NDI) Room: Convert a former and decommissioned Aqueous Film Forming Foam (AFFF) room in Building B1675 for use as a NDI facility."* See drawing sheet 2 of 2 in `DDPM262101_Alter CP and NDI_Plans (20260512).pdf`. Existing-conditions photos at `Alter CP and NDI_B1675 NDI Rm Photos REFERENCE ONLY.pdf` (3.5 MB).

> ⚠️ **Scope clarification — important pricing point.** This CLIN is a **space-conversion** of a former AFFF room into a space *suitable for NDI use*, not an NDI-equipment installation. The Government installs NDI equipment (X-ray, ultrasonic, eddy-current, dye-penetrant, magnetic-particle inspection equipment) post-renovation — the contractor delivers a clean, fully-finished room into which that equipment will later be installed. SOW §2.2 enumerates the trade scope as *"interior wall demolition and construction, painting, caulking, carpentry, and associated electrical, mechanical, and plumbing work"* — i.e., standard interior renovation, not specialty radiation-shielded / EMI-shielded / lead-lined construction. The user's original framing ("NDI rooms typically require lead-shielding, EMI-shielding, vibration limits, dust containment for live equipment") is **not in scope** for this contract per the SOW; the contractor is preparing the room for future NDI use, not building around live NDI equipment. **Field-verify at site visit** (drawing sheet 2 will show the actual interior partition / finishes / mech-elec scope).

> ⚠️ **AFFF residue.** The room is "decommissioned" AFFF storage. AFFF historically contained PFAS / PFOA / PFOS chemicals; demolition / remediation of fixtures or piping that contacted AFFF may require coordinated environmental handling under the SOW §9 (Disposal) clause. Confirm with the 136 Environmental Office (per SOW §9) whether any decontamination or disposal-as-PFAS-impacted-waste is required for items such as old AFFF tanks, foam-piping, sumps, or floor coatings.

## Evaluation factors (Section M.52.212-2 Tailored Deviation 2026-O0038)

Verbatim from Section M (p.49–50):

1. **Responsibility check** — debarment / suspension / proposed-for-debarment screen first (paragraph (a)(i)).
2. **Comparative evaluation, no objective standards** — paragraph (a)(ii): *"In accordance with RFO FAR 12.203(c)(2), a comparative evaluation, without objective standards, of the quotes will be conducted."*
3. **Past Performance** — paragraph (a)(iii): CO will use CPARS, SPRS, and personal knowledge. *"If unfavorable information is discovered or known of an Offeror, the Offeror may be removed from the competition and further evaluation."*
4. **Technical Review (other factors)** — paragraph (a)(iv):
   - **Subfactor 1**: List of equipment, materials, and labor disciplines (RSMeans-style output is the explicit example given by the CO) — used to evaluate "the Offeror's understanding of the technical requirement through analysis of the major work elements proposed."
   - **Subfactor 2**: Project schedule — used to evaluate "ability to identify all necessary work elements of the specific project, the Offeror's ability to schedule the activities in a logical sequence, and to ensure contract performance time required in this solicitation is met."
5. **Best-value award** — paragraph (a)(v): *"The Government intends to award a contract, without negotiating, to the quote that represents the best value to the Government; however, the Government reserves the right to negotiate if deemed in its best interest. … The Government has the discretion to accept other than the lowest priced quote, if determined to be the best value."*

**Implication.** Comparative evaluation is **less prescribed than LPTA** — the CO has wide discretion. We must hit:
- Past performance — strong CPARS history on similar small federal interior renovation work (especially DoD facility renovations);
- A **realistic, sequenced 90-day schedule** showing the contractor understands secure-area construction (mantrap, credential window, secure-door hardware, anti-access ducting bars) and AFFF-room conversion;
- A **comprehensive equipment/materials/labor list** that itemizes the trade scope (don't list only "general construction" — list all the AFI / UFC-driven trade disciplines: carpentry, GWB/finish, doors+frames+hardware, electrical including emergency egress + battery-backup, low-voltage rough-in, HVAC modification + balancing, fire-protection modification, fire-alarm modification, painting, flooring (VCT + carpet), demolition, environmental disposal, security site access management, project management, QC, safety);
- A defensible **competitive (not lowest)** price.
