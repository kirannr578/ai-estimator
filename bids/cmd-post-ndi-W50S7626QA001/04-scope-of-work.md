## A. CLIN 0001 — Building 1672 Command Post Relocation

The Command Post is being **relocated from B4175 to B1672**. The contractor's scope is the **B1672-side renovation** to receive the CP function, plus decommissioning of the existing studio space inside B1672 (Rm 143).

### A.1 Demolition

- Remove sound deadening from existing walls (clean substrate, prep for new texture/paint)
- Disassemble and dispose of existing rack system + studio lighting in current Rm 143 (photo studio)
- Remove existing photo backdrop rolls and bracket assembly; **re-install at BLDG 1669 Rm 112 south-east wall** (top-of-bracket 11 ft AFF; back-brace fabrication may be required)
- Repair penetrations to drop ceiling left after rack/lighting removal
- Selective demo of existing partitions / cabinets / counters / sink / cove base in the renovation footprint — required to make room for new mantrap configuration and new cabinet layout
- Carpet tile removal and adjustment as needed for new partition layout

### A.2 Architectural / Carpentry

- New metal-stud partitions, **full-height from floor to second-floor deck** (not just to ceiling) — sized to the layout shown on Sheet A-1 (mantrap, control room, foyer, comms room, etc.)
- 5/8" gypsum wallboard both sides, mud + tape + texture to match existing
- Above drop-ceiling enclosure where partitions abut secure space — **double-layer 5/8" GWB** to prevent ceiling-plenum access between secure rooms (no texture/paint required above ceiling line)
- Modify drop-ceiling grid + tiles to accommodate new partitions; **all flooring and ceilings to have a seamless transition with aligned tiles** (per Drawing General Note 3)
- Solid-wood doors with **steel-sheet exterior reinforcement** to prevent removal; doorframes compatible with door strength + adjacent wall construction; **all external pins, bolts, screws, and other fasteners protected from removal** (per Construct Note F)
- Mantrap doors: **electronically locked, remote-unlock from console, only one door open at a time** (anti-tailgate); fail-locked on power loss; **manual override mounted near interior mantrap door** for emergency egress; key lock on exterior side for emergency entry; empty EMT conduit + comm box near remote-unlock function and electronic locks for future card-entry system installation (per Construct Notes F, H)
- **Credential window** through partition wall: framing penetration with metal studs; impact-rated mesh-reinforced 1-way window above credential slot; trim around credential window (per Construct Note D)
- Door alarm on emergency exit door; emergency exit door **handle-less from hallway side**, opens only from inside; soundproof requirements for door + replacement seal (per Construct Note E)
- Cabinets: remove + replace all existing cabinets, overhead cabinets, counters, and sink in new dimensions and configuration; overhead cabinets above all identified lower cabinets; **cabinet selections require Government approval** (per Construct Note J)
- Hardware: Stanley/Best Lock-Core compatible (per Drawing General Note 5)

## C. General requirements (apply to both CLINs)

### C.1 Compliance hierarchy (SOW §12)

In order of priority where conflicts arise — **most stringent applies**:

1. NFPA 101 Life Safety Code
2. UFC 1-200-01 — DoD Building Code
3. UFC 3-101-01 Architecture
4. UFC 3-501-01 Electrical Engineering
5. UFC 3-600-01 Fire Protection Engineering
6. UFC 4-010-01 DoD Minimum Antiterrorism Standards for Buildings
7. AFI 32-1054 Corrosion Control
8. UPC — Uniform Plumbing Code
9. Texas 30 TAC Rule 335.262 (Paint and Paint-Related Waste)
10. UFGS series — 01 30 00 Admin, 01 33 00 Submittal, 01 33 29 Sustainability, 01 35 26 Gov Safety, 01 45 00 QC, 01 45 35 Special Inspections, 01 74 19 Construction Waste, 01 78 00 Closeout, 01 78 23 O&M Data
11. OSHA 29 CFR 1926
12. USACE EM 385-1-1 Safety
13. RCRA + EPA NPDES Stormwater
14. All applicable TCEQ environmental regs

### C.2 Quality control + safety

- Designated Safety Manager ensuring daily compliance per OSHA 1926 + EM 385-1-1 + State requirements (SOW §4.5)
- QC plan per UFGS 01 45 00; Special Inspections per UFGS 01 45 35
- Pre-final inspection at 75% completion (SOW §4.8); Final at 100% with 14-day advance written request including assurance that deficiencies have been corrected (SOW §4.9)

### C.3 Submittals (SOW §5.2, §5.3, §5.4)

- AF 3000 Material Approval Submittal for each item on the AF 66 Submittal Register; ESOH review on SDSs
- Submittal Register updated **at least twice per month**
- Bi-weekly progress meeting minutes + updated schedule + planned next-week work

### C.4 Warranties (SOW §5.5, §5.6)

- 1-year Letter of Guarantee against defects in materials and workmanship
- 1-year minimum on workmanship + manufacturer's standard warranty on products

### C.5 Site logistics

- Government-approved space for storing equipment + materials (per SOW §10) — contractor provides fencing with lockable gate
- **End-of-day cleanup**: Contractor clears all equipment and materials from the work site daily unless otherwise approved (SOW §10) — important for ANG installations where active mission spaces border the work area
- Demolition debris removed daily (SOW §4.6); waste disposed off-site at appropriately permitted facility (SOW §4.6, §9); paint-related waste collected per "UNIVERSAL WASTE - PAINT AND PAINT-RELATED WASTES" labeling (SOW §9)
- Working hours: **0630–1715 Tuesday–Friday** (4-day workweek typical for ANG units); after-hours work requires CO approval ≥ 1 week in advance (SOW §11)
- Construction-boundary fencing where applicable (SOW §4.2)
- FOD (Foreign Object Debris) protection at all times — critical at any flightline-adjacent installation (SOW §4.1)
