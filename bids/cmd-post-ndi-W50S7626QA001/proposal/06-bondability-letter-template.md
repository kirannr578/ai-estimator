# Bondability letter — T-Circular-570 surety commitment template

A bondability letter is a one-page statement from the firm's surety on the surety's letterhead, indicating willingness to issue Performance and Payment bonds for the W50S7626QA001 contract at award. **Not strictly required at bid** (FAR 52.228-15 requires the actual bonds at award) but **recommended at bid** as a positive differentiator that reduces the CO's risk perception.

## B. What to expect from the surety

| Element | Standard | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Treasury Circular 570 listing | Required (FAR 52.228-2 + 52.228-15) | Verify at `https://www.fiscal.treasury.gov/surety-bonds/circular-570.html` |
| Single-bond limit | Should comfortably exceed bid envelope (e.g., for a $475K bid, single limit $500K+) | Surety may issue at lower limit with co-surety arrangement |
| Aggregate-bond limit | Should comfortably exceed firm's total bonded backlog + this project | Cross-check with surety's underwriting team |
| Conditions | "None" preferred | If surety adds conditions, address them with bonding agent before submission |
| Letter date | Within 30 days of bid submission | Older letters may be rejected as stale |
| Authorized signatory | Surety officer with authority + Power of Attorney number | Verify against surety's POA register |

## D. Why include at bid

Section M (a)(iii) Past Performance evaluates "personal knowledge of an Offeror" by the CO. A new firm's strongest defense against an unfamiliar CO is to demonstrate financial+execution credibility through:
1. Strong CPARS history (if available)
2. **Bondability letter from a Treasury-approved corporate surety** (this is the second-strongest signal — surety underwriting validates the firm's financial + management capacity independently)
3. Reference letters from prior customers
4. Key personnel CVs

## E. Bid bond vs. Performance/Payment bond — clarity check

| Bond | Required at | Form | Penal amount |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Bid Bond** | WITH the offer (Section L (a)(2) NOTE) | SF 24 | 20% of bid OR $3M cap (FAR 52.228-1) |
| **Performance Bond** | At award + within bid-guarantee window (typically 10 cal days) | SF 25 | 100% of contract price (FAR 52.228-15 if award > $150K) |
| **Payment Bond** | Same as Performance Bond | SF 25A | 100% of contract price |

**The bondability letter** describes the surety's willingness to issue the **Performance + Payment bonds at award**. It does NOT cover the Bid Bond — the Bid Bond is issued separately and goes to the offer. See `07-bid-bond-form-template.md`.

## F. If the surety is not Treasury Circular 570 listed

Per FAR 52.228-15(d), bonds may be supported by:
1. Corporate surety on Treasury Circular 570 (preferred)
2. Individual surety with pledged assets (per FAR 52.228-11 / 52.228-17)
3. Postal money order, certified check, cashier's check
4. Irrevocable Letter of Credit (per FAR 52.228-14)
5. Eligible Treasury bonds or notes

For a small-business interior-renovation contract at this magnitude, **Treasury Circular 570 corporate surety is by far the most common and the most accepted by federal COs.** Firms using individual surety or IRC frequently encounter additional CO scrutiny and slower award processing. **Strongly prefer corporate surety.**
