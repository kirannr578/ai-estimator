# Bid Bond — SF 24 fill guide and penal-amount calculation

A bid bond is a one-page Standard Form 24 (Bid Bond) that the bonding agent issues for the offeror. **Required with the offer** per Section L paragraph (a)(2) NOTE:

> *"NOTE: If a bid bond is required (SF1442, Page 1, Block 13B), scanned copies must be submitted with submission of the quote. The bid bond must be a SF 24."*

## A. Bid bond mechanics (FAR 52.228-1)

Per FAR 52.228-1 verbatim:

> *"(a) Failure to furnish a bid guarantee in the proper form and amount, by the time set for opening of bids, may be cause for rejection of the bid.
>
> (b) The bidder shall furnish a bid guarantee in the form of a firm commitment, e.g., bid bond supported by good and sufficient surety or sureties acceptable to the Government, postal money order, certified check, cashier's check, irrevocable letter of credit, or, under Treasury Department regulations, certain bonds or notes of the United States...
>
> (c) The amount of the bid guarantee shall be 20 percent of the bid price or $3,000,000, whichever is less."*

## E. Surety alternatives (if SF 24 cannot be issued in time)

Per FAR 52.228-1(b), bid guarantees may be in any of the following firm-commitment forms:

| Alternative | Notes |
|---|---|
| **Bid bond (SF 24)** | Standard — preferred |
| Postal money order | Practical only at low penal amounts ($1K cap on USPS Money Orders) |
| Certified check | Drawn on a federally-insured bank; payable to "United States of America" |
| Cashier's check | Drawn on a federally-insured bank; payable to "United States of America" |
| Irrevocable Letter of Credit (FAR 52.228-14) | From a federally-insured bank; expiry must extend ≥ 60 days post-bid acceptance period |
| Treasury bonds or notes | Per Treasury Department regulations |

For a $475K bid, the Bid Bond penal amount of $95K can ONLY be reasonably backed by a bid bond OR an Irrevocable Letter of Credit. Cashier's / certified check at $95K is impractical for cash-flow. **Use SF 24 bid bond.**

## F. Bid-bond return

Per FAR 52.228-1(b):
- Unsuccessful bidder: Bid Bonds returned by CO as soon as practicable after bid opening.
- Successful bidder: Bid Bond returned upon execution of contract documents and Performance/Payment bonds.

## G. Bid-bond default (consequence of withdrawal or non-execution)

Per FAR 52.228-1(d) + (e):
- If the successful bidder fails to execute contractual documents within 10 days after acceptance, the CO may terminate the contract for default.
- The bidder is liable for any cost of acquiring the work that exceeds the amount of the bid, with the Bid Bond available to offset the difference.

**Lesson**: Don't withdraw a bid after it's accepted unless willing to forfeit the bid-bond penal amount.

## H. Order timing

Send the bonding-agent outreach email (`outreach/03-email-bonding-agent.md`) on **Mon 2026-05-25 EOD**. ## I. Common pitfalls

| Pitfall | Consequence | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| SF 24 issued in wrong amount (e.g., based on a draft bid that changed) | Rejected for non-conforming amount | Lock final bid by Wed 6/3 EOD; surety re-issues SF 24 if amount changes |
| SF 24 issued for solicitation number with typo | Rejected as non-conforming | Verify W50S7626QA001 (no I/O confusion) |
| Surety not Treasury Circular 570 listed | Rejected | Verify at `https://www.fiscal.treasury.gov/surety-bonds/circular-570.html` |
| SF 24 single-bond limit < penal amount | Rejected | Verify surety's single-bond limit ≥ penal amount; co-surety arrangement if needed |
| Wet-ink original delayed in mail | CO may award based on scan, but require mail | Send scan with bid; mail wet-ink immediately after submission |
| Power of Attorney expired or missing | Rejected | Surety attaches valid POA with the SF 24 |
