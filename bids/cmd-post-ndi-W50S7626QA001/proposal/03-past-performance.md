# Past Performance — template for 2–3 prior similar projects

Per Section M.52.212-2 (a)(iii):
> *"Past Performance: Information for past performance reviews may come from either an information system or personal knowledge of an Offeror. Information may be sourced from systems such as, but not limited to, the Contractor Performance Assessment Reporting System (CPARS), the Supplier Performance Risk System (SPRS), or the Contracting Officer's personal knowledge of work performed on any previous federal contract. **If unfavorable information is discovered or known of an Offeror, the Offeror may be removed from the competition and further evaluation.**"*

The CO will source past performance regardless of whether the offeror submits anything. **However**, including 2–3 strong past-performance references positively differentiates and accelerates the CO's evaluation. **Recommend: include.**

## A. Selection criteria for prior projects

Choose prior projects that demonstrate:

| Element | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| **Federal customer** (DoD preferred; civilian federal acceptable) | Section M (a)(iii) cites CPARS, SPRS, "any previous federal contract" — federal experience is the gold standard |
| **Interior renovation** scope (not new construction; not exterior; not site work) | Aligns with W50S7626QA001 SOW |
| **Comparable size** ($200K–$700K range; or larger if a single-CLIN portion was comparable) | Demonstrates scale-appropriate execution capacity |
| **Comparable timeline** (60–120 cal day POP) | Demonstrates schedule discipline |
| **Davis-Bacon administration** experience (any DBA-covered project) | Demonstrates WH-347 + DBA literacy |
| **Secure / controlled-access work** (any DoD interior work; SCIF; CP; classified spaces) | Differentiator — even if rare, lead with it |
| **Recent** (within 3 years preferred; up to 5 years acceptable) | CO can access CPARS for recent work |
| **Strong CPARS** (Satisfactory or higher in all rating elements) | Confirms positive CO review |

## E. Selection guidance

| Scenario | Recommendation |
|---|---|
| Firm has 3+ DoD interior renovation projects in last 3 years | Lead with the most recent + most secure-area-relevant; include 2 backups |
| Firm has 1 DoD project + 1 federal-civilian project + 1 state/muni | Lead with the DoD; second the federal-civilian; consider including the state/muni only if it's strongly DBA-administered |
| Firm has 0 federal projects | This is a meaningful disadvantage. Mitigate by: (a) including state/muni-government project past-performance with strong references; (b) including the prime's key personnel CVs with their **prior-firm DoD experience**; (c) including reference letters from prior customers (private + public) attesting to performance quality |
| Firm has 1 federal project + 1 partial-fed project (sub-tier on a federal prime) | Lead with the federal prime project; second the sub-tier (cite the federal prime by name + describe the sub-tier scope) |

## F. Past-performance volume — what NOT to include

- **No fabrications.** The CO will pull CPARS independently; any inconsistency between the offeror's submitted past-performance volume and the CPARS record is a serious problem.
- **No projects with unfavorable CPARS** (any "Marginal" or "Unsatisfactory" element). The CO will see them anyway; better not to draw attention.
- **No projects from sister entities** without clear ownership/control documentation. Past performance is the offeror's, not its parent's or sister's.
- **No subcontract-only projects** unless explicitly documented as sub-tier with the federal prime named — and prefer prime-contractor projects.

## G. Reference-letter mechanics

If references are included as attachments:
- 1-page letter from each reference customer
- On customer's letterhead (not firm's letterhead)
- Signed by an authorized customer representative (CO, COR, PM, or facility owner)
- Includes: project name + dates + firm's role + ratings on quality / schedule / management / regulatory compliance + a recommendation

## H. Submission

Embed the past-performance volume in the same email as the SF 1442. Use a clear filename: `W50S7626QA001_Volume_PastPerformance_[Firm].pdf` or include directly in the consolidated proposal PDF.
