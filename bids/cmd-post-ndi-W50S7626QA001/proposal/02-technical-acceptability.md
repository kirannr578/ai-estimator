# Technical Acceptability — Subfactor 1 + Subfactor 2

Section M.52.212-2 (a)(iv) defines the technical evaluation as **two subfactors only**:

> **Subfactor 1**: The list of equipment, materials, and labor disciplines shall list those items necessary to accomplish the scope of work identified in the statement of work. This list will be used to evaluate the Offeror's understanding of the technical requirement through analysis of the major work elements proposed.
>
> **Subfactor 2**: The project schedule will be evaluated relative to overall coordination of the construction phase, to include ability to identify all necessary work elements of the specific project, the Offeror's ability to schedule the activities in a logical sequence, and to ensure contract performance time required in this solicitation is met.

The submission for these two subfactors is **two PDF files** (or two sections of one PDF). **Resist the temptation to write a long technical narrative** — Section M evaluates these two specific deliverables, not a generic technical approach.

Format: 2–4-page itemized list, CSI-divided. Section L paragraph (a)(1) explicit guidance: *"An example submission would be an RSMeans output list or a similar product."*

### A.2 Discrimination strategy

Section M (a)(iv)(a) judges this list on **comprehensiveness + understanding of major work elements**. Win on:

1. **Completeness** — every trade discipline + material category from `04-scope-of-work.md` is listed
2. **Specificity** — name actual products (SW1016 Silver Screen, AZrock V-866, Shaw Clear Tile #59564 Moonstone 62500, Stanley/Best Lock-Core)
3. **DoD / UFC literacy** — cite UFC 3-501-01, UFGS section numbers, AF 3000 / AF 66 submittals — demonstrates DoD execution literacy
4. **Federal-construction literacy** — Davis-Bacon WD TX20260270 administration, WH-347 weekly certified payroll, base-access coordination
5. **Site-specific literacy** — NAS-JRB Visitor Control Center, FOD prevention, daily clearout, anti-access bars per Drawing General Note 11, classified-work-in-progress signage, mantrap mechanics

> **Don't include cost data here.** This is a list, not a budget. Pricing belongs in `01-price-proposal.md` only.

### B.2 Discrimination strategy

Section M (a)(iv)(b) judges the schedule on three things:
1. **Identifies all necessary work elements** — every trade transition + inspection + submittal cycle is shown
2. **Logical sequence** — demo → framing → MEP rough-in → drywall → doors/hardware/specialties → finishes → MEP trim → commissioning → punch list → final
3. **Fits inside 90 calendar days** — final acceptance on Day 90, with pre-final at Day 70 and 14-day advance final-inspection notice on Day 76

Win on:
1. **Tuesday–Friday calendar transparency** — explicit acknowledgment that the working calendar is Tue–Fri, not Mon–Fri (shows literacy)
2. **Long-lead acknowledgment** — Stanley/Best Lock-Core and mantrap kit lead times are baked into Day 7 submittal cycle
3. **Base-access coordination** — sub-crew badging on Day 1 + 14 BD turnaround
4. **Inspection sequencing** — AHJ rough-in (Day 48–52); pre-final at Day 70; final at Day 90 with 14-day advance per SOW §4.9
5. **Both CLINs in parallel** — demonstrates capacity to manage simultaneous renovations

## C. Optional value-add elements (Section M (a)(v): "Government may take more than the minimum")

Per Section M (a)(v): *"The Government reserves the right to consider a quote which offers more than the minimum and select that quote if it provides a benefit to the Government."*

Consider including:

1. **Key personnel CVs** (1 paragraph each) for: Site Superintendent (DoD-experienced), Safety Manager (EM 385-1-1 trained), QC Manager (UFGS 01 45 00 implementation), Project Manager
2. **Quality Control Plan** (1-page summary; full plan submittable post-award per UFGS 01 45 00)
3. **Safety Plan summary** (1-page summary; full plan submittable post-award per UFGS 01 35 26)
4. **Environmental coordination plan** for AFFF residue handling (CLIN 0002 specifically)

> **But: Don't pad.** A 30-page technical narrative will hurt, not help, in comparative evaluation. Each value-add element should be ≤ 1 page; total submitted ≤ 15 pages excluding SF 1442 + bid bond + Schedule of Prices.
