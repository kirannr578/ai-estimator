# Scope Template — Cosmetology / Barber / Esthetician / Nail-Tech Classroom Renovation

> **Source bid mined:** [`bids/mcc-cosmetology-phase4-2026-0622/`](../../bids/mcc-cosmetology-phase4-2026-0622/) — McLennan Community College CSC Module B Cosmetology Phase 4 Renovation, RBDR project 25062, Waco TX. 414-page PM + 37-sheet DWG set; HCS Inc. is one of the bidding GCs into MCC's CSP, BPC sub-bidding to HCS. Section numbers below cite the CSI MasterFormat sections present in that PM ToC (verbatim where quoted).
>
> **Use this template when:** A community college, trade school, technical institute, or career-and-technical-ed (CTE) high school is renovating an existing classroom or wing into a state-licensed **cosmetology**, **barber**, **esthetician**, or **nail-tech** instructional lab. Pattern is interior renovation (no envelope work) of an existing building, with wet-trade plumbing density and high station-electrical density layered on top of a standard classroom finish-out. Also covers public-school cosmetology / barber CTE labs that must meet state-board licensure requirements (TDLR in Texas; analogous regulators in other states).
>
> **Not a fit when:** Ground-up new construction (size up to a full classroom-building template); salon tenant build-out in a strip-mall lease (Section 11 23 Salon Equipment dominates, less classroom infrastructure); spa / wellness facility (heavier on Div 22 hydrotherapy + Div 23 humidity control than the classroom pattern).
>
> **Typical CSI divisions touched:** 01, 02, 03 (slab patching only), 06 (rough carp + plam casework), 07 (insulation + firestop + sealants), 08 (doors / frames / hardware / fire-rated glazing), 09 (drywall + ACT + resilient + **fluid-applied chemical-resistant flooring** + paint), 10 (marker boards + signage + wall protection), 12 (quartz tops + walk-off mats), 22 (domestic water + DWV + **plumbing fixtures including shampoo bowls per 224001**), 23 (HVAC fans + diffusers + roof curbs — no dedicated kitchen-hood scope but cosmetology-specific exhaust at color stations is a common add), 26 (selective demo + LV power + **wiring devices at station density** + lighting controls + LED retrofit per IECC C405.2).

**Hard schedule constraint:** community-college cosmetology programs run on academic terms. If the contract window overlaps fall or spring semester, summer-term blackout dates control sequencing (typ mid-May → mid-August working window). The state cosmetology board / TDLR inspection MUST be complete before instruction begins on opening day — build a 2-week buffer at the end for re-inspection if the first pass fails.

## 2. Standard takeoff-item list

### 2.1 Per-station (repeat per station type)

```
STYLING STATION (each)
  □ Plam casework base cabinet (LF) — typ 36" wide per station, ADA at 1 of N
  □ Quartz countertop (LF) — pairs with casework
  □ Mirror wall behind station (SF) — continuous OR per-station; verify A-elevations
  □ Dedicated 20A circuit + duplex receptacle at counter height (each — typ 1 per station)
  □ Second dedicated 20A circuit for high-amp hot tools (dryer, flat iron) if spec calls for hood/hot-tool wall (each)
  □ Task lighting at mirror (each — typ vanity strip or recessed downlight pair)
  □ Wall blocking for mirror + casework anchors (LF)
  □ ADA station (each — minimum 1 per N stations; knee clearance + 30"x48" clear floor)
  □ OFCI salon chair coordination (each — verify base anchor type, plumbing if hydraulic-pump chair)

SHAMPOO BOWL STATION (each)
  □ Shampoo bowl fixture (each) — verify mfr (typ Belvedere, Takara Belmont, Collins) + bowl style (forward-tilt, reclining)
  □ Tempering / mixing valve to ASSE 1070 (each — code-required at every shampoo bowl; max ~110°F)
  □ Hot + cold supply rough-in (each)
  □ Trap + waste rough-in to DWV (each)
  □ Vent line (each — wet vent or AAV per IPC)
  □ Hand-held sprayer / hose connection (each)
  □ Shampoo-chair anchor coordination (each — OFCI typical)
  □ GFCI receptacle within 6' (NEC Art. 210.8 — required near any plumbing fixture)
  □ Floor drain at bowl bank (each — recommended; not always spec'd)
  □ ADA shampoo station (each — minimum 1; verify TDLR's ADA fixture-count rule)

MANICURE / NAIL-TECH STATION (each)
  □ Plam table + base cabinet (LF) — typ 24"–30" wide per station
  □ Quartz OR plastic-laminate top (LF) — verify chemical-resistance class for acetone exposure
  □ Dedicated duplex receptacle at station (each — lamp + dust collector)
  □ Acrylic-vapor / dust local exhaust (each — typ source-capture arm OR downdraft table; required by many state cosmo boards)
  □ Wall blocking for table anchor (LF)
  □ ADA nail station (each — min 1 of N; knee + floor clearances)

PEDICURE STATION (each)
  □ Pedicure chair anchor coordination (each — OFCI typ; some chairs are plumbed with pump/drain, others are basin-only)
  □ Hot + cold supply rough (each, if plumbed chair)
  □ Drain + trap rough (each, if plumbed chair)
  □ Dedicated 20A circuit (each — heating element + massage)
  □ GFCI within 6' (each)
  □ Floor drain at pedi area (each — recommended)

ESTHETICIAN / FACIAL STATION (each)
  □ Casework + storage cabinet at head of bed (LF)
  □ Esthetician facial bed anchor coordination (each — OFCI typ; some hydraulic, some electric)
  □ Dedicated 20A circuit for bed motor + steamer + magnifying lamp (each)
  □ Task lighting at facial bed (each — typ surgical-grade LED OR adjustable arm)
  □ Wall blocking for shelf / steamer mount (LF)
  □ Curtained or partitioned privacy enclosure (LF + each — typ ceiling-mounted curtain track)
  □ ADA facial-room access (min 1 of N; door clear width + transfer space)
```

### 2.2 Lab-wide items

```
LAB WIDE (cosmetology lab room itself)
  Plumbing
    □ Domestic water main upsize at branch serving lab (verify pipe size per fixture-unit count under IPC Table 6.2)
    □ Booster pump or recirculating loop (if existing pressure inadequate at shampoo bowl bank — site verify)
    □ Dedicated tank-type OR tankless water heater sized for shampoo bowl peak demand (GAL or kW) — typ 80–120 gal tank OR 199 kBTU tankless for a 6–10 bowl bank
    □ Grease / hair interceptor at shampoo waste line (each — required by some AHJs to protect downstream drain)
    □ Floor drains at wet zones (each)
    □ Eyewash / drench station (each — required at any chemical-handling lab per ANSI Z358.1)
  Electrical
    □ 200A+ subpanel upgrade serving the lab (verify panel schedule; existing classroom panel typ undersized for 30+ stations × dedicated circuits + hot tools + dryers)
    □ Branch wiring + receptacles per station (each — typical density 1 dedicated 20A circuit per styling/nail station; 2 per station if hot-tool wall)
    □ GFCI receptacles in all wet zones (each — NEC Art. 210.8)
    □ Lighting controls (occupancy + daylight per IECC C405.2)
    □ Full LED retrofit if not already (EA — fixture type per EL spec; verify lumen / CRI per state board's color-matching minimum, typ CRI ≥90 for color work)
    □ Emergency / egress lighting (each)
  HVAC
    □ Roof curb + exhaust fan dedicated to lab (each) — chemical / vapor exhaust per IMC
    □ Source-capture exhaust hood at color-mixing station (each) — verify spec; not always present but increasingly required
    □ Source-capture exhaust at nail-tech station bank (each OR LF of downdraft table)
    □ Supply diffusers + return grilles per RCP (each)
    □ Makeup-air balancing — exhaust-heavy labs need conditioned makeup air to avoid negative pressure
  Finishes
    □ Fluid-applied chemical-resistant resin floor in lab area (SF) — verify chemistry: epoxy (chemical-resistant, common), polyaspartic (faster cure, UV stable), urethane mortar (heaviest-duty, food-service crossover)
    □ Integral cove base at fluid-applied floor (LF)
    □ Resilient tile (LVT / VCT) in lecture / instructor / corridor zones (SF)
    □ 4" or 6" cove base at LVT zones (LF)
    □ Walk-off mat at lab entry (SF) — recessed
    □ Wall protection (corner guards + chair rail) in high-traffic + wet zones (LF)
    □ Mirror wall (LF + height) — see per-station above
    □ Marker board / tack board at instructor zone (each)
    □ Signage — ADA tactile at every door + station-area wayfinding (each)
  Casework
    □ Reception desk (LF + plam + quartz top)
    □ Back-of-house product storage uppers + base cabinets (LF)
    □ Towel / linen storage cabinet (LF)
    □ Chemical storage cabinet (each) — ventilated; meets OSHA HazCom for hair color + relaxer + acetone storage
    □ Laundry / utility area with washer-dryer hookups (LF + each)
```

## 7. Typical inclusions / exclusions checklist

**Typical inclusions in a BPC cosmetology-classroom quote:**

- [ ] Selective demolition of existing classroom build-out per drawings
- [ ] All MEP rough-in + trim per the plumbing / electrical / mechanical sheets
- [ ] All finishes per the finish plan (fluid-applied lab floor, resilient elsewhere, paint, ceilings, casework)
- [ ] Plam casework + quartz tops for cosmetology stations, reception, and storage per millwork sections
- [ ] Mirror wall install per A-elevations
- [ ] Salon-equipment **rough-in coordination** (anchor blocking, supply/drain stub-outs, dedicated circuits)
- [ ] State cosmetology board / TDLR final-inspection coordination + punch
- [ ] ADA / TAS compliance verification at 1 of each station type
- [ ] Standard 1-year workmanship warranty + flow-through of manufacturer warranties

**Typical exclusions (flag explicitly in the quote):**

- [ ] OFCI salon equipment — chairs, dryers, beds, dispensers, retail fixtures (**verify division of responsibility before pricing; misalignment here is the #1 dispute risk**)
- [ ] Hazardous-materials survey + abatement (asbestos floor tile / mastic, lead paint) — Phase-N campus building scope; price as a separate allowance OR exclude
- [ ] Owner's IT / data cabling beyond conduit-and-box (Div 27 not in scope unless explicitly spec'd)
- [ ] Owner's security / access control beyond conduit-and-box (Div 28 not in scope)
- [ ] FF&E (waiting-room furniture, retail display fixtures, decor)
- [ ] State cosmetology board licensure fees + permit fees (owner's responsibility)
- [ ] After-hours / weekend premium time if construction window is restricted (price as an allowance OR carry in alternate)
- [ ] Re-inspection fees if state-board first-pass fails for owner-furnished equipment reasons

## 8. Common risks

| # | Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|--:|---|:--:|:--:|---|
| 1 | **State cosmetology board / TDLR inspection failure on opening day** — TX TDLR Cosmetology Rules (16 TAC Ch. 83) specify minimum square footage per station, minimum fixture count, exhaust requirements, sanitation provisions. **Critical-path dominant items:**

- **Salon-grade plumbing fixtures (shampoo bowls, pedi chairs, esthetician beds):** 8–14 weeks. Order at submittal acceptance.
- **Plam casework shop drawings + production:** 6–10 weeks from approved shops.
- **Quartz countertops:** 4–8 weeks from approved templates (template drawn after casework is set, so this stacks).
- **Source-capture exhaust hoods / arms (if spec'd):** 6–10 weeks.

## 10. Pricing notes

- **Cost-DB tags:** unit rates above use the `casework.plam_salon_*`, `plumb.shampoo_*`, `floor.fluid_applied_*`, `elec.20a_station_*` namespace. Verify against [`config/cost_database.json`](../../config/cost_database.json) and update as a separate PR when actuals come in from a shipped bid.
- **Escalation:** apply **AGC Construction Cost Index (CCI)** quarterly delta from base-year rate to bid-month rate. Cosmetology has no special escalation profile vs. general commercial interior reno; use the standard CCI factor.
- **Regional labor:** use BLS OEWS (Occupational Employment and Wage Statistics) area code for the project's MSA. For McLennan: **MSA 47380 Waco TX**. For other Texas community-college locations: DFW 19124, Houston 26420, Austin 12420, San Antonio 41700.
- **Davis-Bacon overlay:** if federal funding is implicated (Perkins V CTE funding, CARES/ARP, etc.), use the county-specific WD from [sam.gov/wage-determinations](https://sam.gov/wage-determinations) instead of OEWS; WD rates are typically 15–30% above OEWS for building-construction trades in Texas.
- **Specialty equipment vendors usually require a sub-quote.** Salon-grade equipment (Belvedere, Takara Belmont, Collins, Pibbs, Salon Ambience) is rarely stocked locally; vendors will quote against the specific cut-sheet list and lead-time. On a private-GC roll-up (e.g., the McLennan / HCS pattern), the GC may want either a packaged equipment-furnished-and-installed sub-quote OR a "rough-in only, equipment OFCI" quote. **Confirm scope of the equipment line at Gate 4** before pricing. See [`firm/playbooks/_learning-log.md`](../playbooks/_learning-log.md) for the "private-GC-sub-bid" pattern (will be authored when Worker C closes that playbook gap).
- **Mobilization premium** matters for any cosmetology job outside BPC's primary North-Texas service area. Waco at 95 mi from Frisco adds a non-trivial per-diem + travel line; price honestly as a separate line, do not absorb in margin (per the McLennan Phase-4 risk register).

## 13. Common pitfalls

1. **OFCI / CFCI matrix not nailed down before pricing.** The single biggest source of margin erosion on cosmetology jobs. RFI every line.
2. **State-board inspection treated as an afterthought.** The state cosmetology board inspector has *gate authority* over opening day; their punch list is not the architect's punch list. Coordinate the inspection at substantial completion -3 weeks.
3. **Existing electrical service inadequate.** New 30-station lab + dryer wall + hot tools commonly exceeds the existing classroom feeder. Confirm at site visit; if a service-tap is needed, that scope must be in someone's price.
4. **Hot-water peak demand miscalculated.** Six shampoo bowls running simultaneously at 8 minutes each = ~24 GPM of 105°F water — needs an actual sizing calc, not a default 50-gal tank.
5. **Fluid-applied floor chemistry mismatched to chemical exposure.** Standard epoxy stains under acetone + peroxide-based color developer; verify chemical-resistance grade per spec.
6. **Mirror wall install ahead of station-equipment install.** OFCI station chairs sometimes need anchor adjustments after install; sequence mirror install AFTER chair / station base anchoring is finalized.
7. **Cosmetology dust / hair tracking through adjacent classrooms during construction.** Standard interior-reno dust protection is insufficient — add negative-pressure containment + sticky-mat regimen during demo + plam install.
8. **CRI of lab lighting too low for color work.** Default classroom LED is CRI 80; cosmo color matching needs CRI ≥90. Verify at submittal.
9. **No source-capture exhaust at nail tech bank.** Acrylic dust + MMA vapor have OSHA exposure limits; source-capture is the engineered control. Verify against state-board rule.
10. **Cosmetology lab marketed as "fully sprinklered" without verifying.** Most older campus buildings have partial sprinkler coverage; lab modifications may trigger new heads. Verify against the LS partition schedule + the existing sprinkler plan if available.
