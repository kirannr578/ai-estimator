## 5. Common alternates / VE opportunities

- **Alt locker material:** HDPE (base) → Z-frame steel (premium, longer life) → Phenolic (cleanroom-grade — overkill for athletic use)
- **Alt locker tier:** single-tier (base, more space per athlete) → double-tier (more lockers per LF wall)
- **Alt tile vs sheet vinyl in wet rooms:** tile (base, longer life) → seamless sheet vinyl with welded coves (cheaper install, faster, but shorter life cycle)
- **Alt epoxy vs cement grout:** epoxy (base for chemical / sweat resistance) → cement (cheaper, faster, regroutable every 7–10 years)
- **VE — eliminate hand dryers, keep paper towels:** saves $500–$1,200 per room + dedicated-circuit electrical
- **VE — reuse existing toilet partitions if structurally sound + ADA-compliant**
- **VE — sheet-vinyl flooring in dressing zones (non-wet) instead of porcelain tile**
- **VE — building-supplied ventilation if existing exhaust is adequate per re-test**

## 6. Schedule duration guidance

| Project size | Substantial completion |
|---|---|
| 1 room + 1 shower / restroom | 6–8 weeks |
| 3–5 dressing rooms + shared shower / restroom | 10–12 weeks |
| 10-room suite (ASU Carr EFA archetype, ~3,000 SF) | 14–16 weeks |
| Whole-floor athletic locker complex (~10,000+ SF) | 20–28 weeks |

**Long-lead items to flag early:**

- Lockers (8–14 weeks; many mfrs are 10+ weeks on Z-frame)
- Toilet partitions (4–8 weeks)
- Tile (often stock locally but verify large-scale spec quantity)
- Solid-surface or epoxy-resin shower-curb material if specified

## 7. ADA discipline (critical on this project type)

- Tempering / mixing valves on every shower per ASSE 1016 (max 110°F)
- ADA accessible compartment in every restroom group with grab bars, accessible TP dispenser, accessible coat hook
- 60" turning radius in roll-in shower; 30"x60" minimum clear floor at ADA fixtures
- Tactile signage at every door per ICC A117.1
- Maximum 1:48 floor slope at ADA shower pan
- Verify **all fixture heights** against ICC A117.1 (lav rim, mirror bottom, soap dispenser, grab bar, robe hook, TT dispenser) — these are punch-list killers if missed at rough-in

## 8. State-CSP overlay (ASU Carr EFA archetype)

Layer the state-CSP playbook ([`firm/playbooks/texas-state-csp-hsp.md`](../playbooks/texas-state-csp-hsp.md)) considerations:

- Lab / specialty-trade HUB pool thinner than building HUB pool; allocate locker + tile + plumbing aggressively to HUB subs
- TAMU / TTUS / UT SGC insurance limits often exceed firm baseline — verify Umbrella
- LD rate published in CSP (ASU Carr = $250/day) — bake into schedule risk
- Owner-supplied items: verify exactly what is OFCI / OFOI vs CFCI (Carr EFA had a late scope-flip on lockers from OFCI to CFCI; assume CFCI by default and ask)
