## 5. Common alternates / VE opportunities

- **Alt OH door:** insulated sectional (base) → non-insulated sectional (cheaper but loses R-value) → rolling steel (much cheaper but loses thermal performance)
- **Alt OH door operator:** chain-hoist (base) → motorized (premium add ~$1,200 per door)
- **Alt windows:** aluminum-clad fixed (base) → insulated translucent panel (cheaper, brings light, no view) → no window (if security or use doesn't require)
- **Alt insulation:** R-13 batt (base) → R-19 batt (premium, longer payback) → spray-foam closed-cell (premium, much better but 3x cost)
- **Alt interior finish:** FRP panel (base for shop) → vinyl-faced gypsum (cheaper) → painted CMU / steel (cheapest, industrial look)
- **Alt floor:** sealed concrete (base) → epoxy coating (premium, longer life on shop floors) → no treatment (cheapest, dusts)
- **VE — leave existing roof + recoat only:** saves 30–60% over full re-roof
- **VE — leave existing electrical panel if amperage is adequate:** saves 10–15% on electrical
- **VE — LED retrofit kit (driver + diode array into existing HID housing):** saves 40–60% per fixture vs full replacement
- **VE — leave existing windows + reseal only:** saves window line entirely

## 7. Site-specific factors common to federal rural / NWR sites

USFWS / NPS / USACE sites are often **remote**. This affects:

- **Mobilization windows:** longer drive times for crews + subs; build into per-diem if > 50 miles from crew home base
- **Material deliveries:** lead times often longer; building dimensions may require flat-bed vs box-truck (verify OH door overall length)
- **Power availability:** verify if temp power exists or if generator is required for crew + tools
- **Water availability:** wash-down water for sealant cure + concrete patching may be from on-site well — verify with site POC at site visit
- **Wildlife / endangered-species coordination:** seasonal restrictions (USFWS San Marcos sits next to the San Marcos River with multiple federally-listed species — Texas wild rice, San Marcos salamander, fountain darter); site activities may have seasonal blackouts
- **Lockout / tagout coordination:** facility utilities often must be shut down with 5+ day notice
- **Government-installation access:** USACE / USAF / DoD bases require background-cleared workers + escort hours; civilian USFWS / NPS / FWS typically don't
- **Davis-Bacon prevailing wage:** federal project — apply WD from RFP; weekly certified payroll discipline; add 1–2% to GC line for administrative burden

## 8. Federal-LPTA pricing overlay

Layer the federal-LPTA playbook ([`firm/playbooks/federal-sba-rfq-lpta.md`](../playbooks/federal-sba-rfq-lpta.md)) considerations:

- **Magnitude floor + 5–10%** as the pricing anchor — USFWS San Marcos magnitude was $100K–$250K, so price ~$105K–$120K
- **Self-perform ≥ 15%** of direct cost on-site (GC + supervision + selective demo most defensible)
- **Buy American on iron + steel:** OH-door tracks, hollow-metal frames, gutter / downspout aluminum (Buy America Act treats aluminum as iron-and-steel for this purpose); domestic-content rep on every PO
- **Bond:** 5% bid + 100% P&P at award (over $150K); add 1.5–2.5% to direct cost
- **Insurance:** federal baseline + agency-specific additional insured language
