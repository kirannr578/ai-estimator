# Scope Template — Historic Roof Repair / Replacement

> **Source context:** PAIS (Padre Island National Seashore) Backcountry Cabin scope referenced in `firm/firm-profile.json` future-opportunities + general NPS / NRHP-listed structure precedent. NPS **Preservation Brief 4** (Roofing for Historic Buildings) is the controlling reference, alongside the Secretary of the Interior's Standards for Rehabilitation.
>
> **Hard differentiator from a standard re-roof:** material selection, attachment method, flashing detailing, and ventilation strategy must all be SHPO / NPS-approved. "In-kind" replacement (matching original material + profile) is the default; deviations require justification.
>
> **Typical CSI divisions touched:** 01, 02, 06 (carpentry — deck repair), 07 (waterproofing, sheet metal, roofing — primary), 22 (drains/scuppers if applicable), 26 (lightning protection if specified).

## 1. Scope outline (standard sequencing)

| # | Phase | Trade(s) | Typical duration (single ~1,500 SF historic structure) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Pre-construction historic-conditions assessment + roof condition survey + photo-doc | Preservation consultant + roofing contractor | 1 week |
| 2 | SHPO / Section 106 pre-construction submittal (material + attachment + flashing) | Preservation consultant + agency | 4–8 weeks |
| 3 | Salvage of original roofing elements (cresting, finials, snow guards, cupola caps) — bag + tag + retain | Roofer + preservation contractor | 2–3 days |
| 4 | Tear-off (selective — typ down to deck unless deck-replacement scope) | Roofer | 3–5 days |
| 5 | Deck repair / replacement (in-kind species + dimension) | Carpenter | 3–7 days |
| 6 | Underlayment + ice & water shield (where SHPO permits) | Roofer | 2–3 days |
| 7 | New roofing in-kind (standing-seam metal OR cedar shake OR slate OR asphalt 3-tab) | Roofer (specialty) | 1–2 weeks |
| 8 | Flashing (lead-coated copper OR terne metal OR base/counter flashing per spec) | Sheet-metal sub | 3–5 days |
| 9 | Gutters + downspouts (half-round, period-appropriate) + leaders + splash blocks | Sheet-metal sub | 2–3 days |
| 10 | Reinstall salvaged elements (cresting, finials, lightning protection ground straps) | Roofer | 2–3 days |
| 11 | Ventilation rebuild (concealed ridge vent, gable vent, soffit vent per SHPO) | Roofer + carpenter | 2–3 days |
| 12 | Touch-up paint + caulk + final inspection | GC | 1–2 days |
| 13 | SHPO post-construction sign-off + as-builts | Preservation consultant | 1–2 weeks |
| **Total** | | | **8–14 weeks (review cycle + weather windows can stretch)** |

## 2. Standard takeoff-item list

### 2.1 Tear-off + deck

```
TEAR-OFF + DECK
  □ Tear off existing roofing (SF) — note material + estimate # of layers
  □ Tear off existing underlayment (SF)
  □ Tear off existing flashing (LF perimeter + LF penetrations)
  □ Tear off existing gutters + downspouts (LF + each)
  □ Tear off existing ridge cap (LF)
  □ Salvage cresting / finials / snow guards / cupola caps / weathervanes (each — DO NOT discard without SHPO authorization)
  □ Disposal (CY) — separate stream if hazardous (older asbestos-cement shingle, lead-flashed seams)
  □ Inspect + document deck condition (SF)
  □ Deck patch / replace in-kind species (T&G 1x6 OR T&G 1x8 OR plank — match original)
  □ Deck nailing (smooth-shank nails per historic spec, NOT modern ring-shank if visible)
```

### 2.2 New roofing (in-kind options)

```
NEW ROOFING — PICK ONE per SHPO approval
  Standing-seam metal (most common for early-1900s + later)
    □ 24-ga or 26-ga galvanized OR terne-coated steel OR copper (per spec)
    □ Standing-seam profile width (typ 12" OC for historic; verify against original)
    □ Pan length (continuous OR seamed)
    □ Clip spacing (per wind uplift calc + historic profile)
    □ Cleat OR concealed-fastener clip
  Cedar shake (residential-era historic, pre-1940)
    □ Cedar shake (#1 grade) — verify thickness + exposure + treatment per spec
    □ Open OR closed valley
    □ Skip-sheath OR solid deck (per original)
  Slate
    □ Natural slate (region-appropriate quarry — Vermont, Virginia, Pennsylvania)
    □ Hooks OR copper nails per spec
    □ Slate hammer + cutter + ripper (specialty tools)
    □ Headlap + sidelap per spec
  Asphalt 3-tab (post-WWII)
    □ 3-tab OR architectural — match profile + color
    □ Algae-resistant
```

### 2.3 Underlayment + ice/water + ventilation

```
UNDERLAYMENT
  □ Synthetic underlayment (where SHPO permits) (SF) — note: traditional 30-lb felt may be required by some SHPOs
  □ Ice + water shield (eaves + valleys + penetrations) (SF) — NPS permits in most regions; verify per project
  □ Eave starter (LF)
VENTILATION (SHPO prefers least-visible)
  □ Concealed ridge vent (LF) — preferred for visibility
  □ Gable vent rebuild (each) — period-appropriate louver
  □ Soffit vent (LF) — concealed bird-screen
  □ NO turbine vents on historic structures (modern eyesore)
```

### 2.4 Flashing + sheet metal

```
FLASHING (historic-grade material)
  □ Lead-coated copper OR terne metal at:
    □ Chimney base + counter-flashing (LF + each)
    □ Wall + roof intersection (LF)
    □ Vent pipe boots (each)
    □ Skylight curb (each, if applicable)
    □ Roof-to-wall step flashing (LF)
  □ Ridge cap (LF) — material matches roofing
  □ Drip edge (LF) — color OR profile per spec
GUTTERS + DOWNSPOUTS (period-appropriate)
  □ Half-round 5" or 6" copper OR galv OR aluminum (LF)
  □ Round downspout (LF + each) — typ 3" or 4" round
  □ Leader heads (each) — decorative if period-appropriate
  □ Splash blocks OR underground tie-in (each)
  □ Snow guards (LF) — if cold-climate site; required on metal/slate
LIGHTNING PROTECTION (if existing — must be re-installed)
  □ Air terminals (each)
  □ Down conductors (LF) — copper braid
  □ Ground rods (each)
```

## 5. Common alternates / VE opportunities

| Item | Base | Alternate | Trade-off |
|---|---|---|---|
| Roofing material | In-kind original (cedar shake, slate) | "Like-kind appearance" (synthetic slate, composite shake) | Synthetics 30–50% cheaper but require SHPO acceptance (frequently rejected on high-significance NRHP) |
| Flashing material | Lead-coated copper | Terne metal OR plain copper | Plain copper patinas more aggressively; terne is 30% cheaper; lead-coated has 75-100 yr lifespan |
| Underlayment | 30-lb felt (traditional) | Synthetic underlayment | Synthetic is 30% cheaper + longer lifespan but some SHPOs reject |
| Ice + water shield | Permit at eaves + valleys + penetrations | Omit (traditional) | Omitting saves $0.30–$0.80/SF + risks ice-dam damage; SHPO usually permits in cold climate |
| Ventilation | Concealed ridge vent | Cupola-vented OR gable-only | Original config preferred; modify only if condensation problem documented |
| Gutter profile | Half-round | K-style | K-style is 20–30% cheaper but anachronistic for pre-1950 historic |
| Tear-off depth | To deck, replace bad sections | To deck, replace all deck | Full deck replacement adds 30–50% but worth it on >70 yr structures |

## 6. Schedule duration ranges

| Project size | Typical duration | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Single small historic structure (~1,500 SF roof) | 8–12 weeks | SHPO review is critical-path |
| Multi-building NPS campus (~5–10 structures) | 14–22 weeks | Phased per visitor impact + weather windows |
| Large historic structure (~5,000+ SF, slate or copper) | 12–20 weeks | Long-lead material |
| Remote NPS site (PAIS, Big Bend, Death Valley) | Add 20–40% to base | Travel + mobilization + supply chain |

**Critical-path items:**
- SHPO review cycle (4–8 weeks per submittal; plan 2 cycles minimum)
- Slate / copper material lead times (10–16 weeks for slate; 6–12 weeks for copper)
- Weather windows (cedar shake install best in dry seasons; slate avoid freeze-thaw)
- Remote-site mobilization (boat-only access at PAIS Backcountry Cabin is the extreme case — verify access logistics at pre-bid)

## 8. Common pitfalls

1. **In-kind material assumed available locally — but isn't.** Vermont slate, lead-coated copper, traditional cedar shake (#1, untreated) are specialty supply. Verify lead times BEFORE bid.
2. **SHPO review cycle assumed at 30 days.** Plan 60–90 days per submittal; major-impact undertakings (deck replacement + ventilation change) may take 90–120 days.
3. **Wind uplift calc skipped.** Coastal sites (PAIS, Galveston, Charleston) require enhanced attachment + may force higher-gauge metal OR mechanical attachment of shakes. Don't price standard attachment without verifying wind exposure.
4. **Salvage scope under-budgeted.** Salvaging + tagging + storing cresting, finials, snow guards, etc. for re-install commonly takes 2–3 days per structure — not accounted for in standard re-roof.
5. **Lead-paint hazard on flashing.** Pre-1978 painted flashing often contains lead — disposal + worker protection required. Test BEFORE tear-off.
6. **Asbestos in old roofing.** Asbestos-cement shingle on early-1900s buildings — separate disposal stream + worker protection.
7. **Modern-profile gutters spec'd.** K-style gutters are visible from grade + anachronistic for pre-1950 historic. Use half-round.
8. **Lightning-protection re-install missed.** Existing copper braid + air terminals must be re-installed; specialty sub required.
9. **Remote-site access logistics under-estimated.** PAIS Backcountry Cabin example: boat access only; verify cargo capacity for material delivery. Some NPS sites are pack-in (helicopter or mule).
10. **Weather window misjudged.** Cedar shake install needs dry weather; slate avoid freeze-thaw; metal needs minimum substrate temp. Build weather days into schedule.
