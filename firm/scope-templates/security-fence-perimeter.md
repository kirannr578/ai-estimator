# Scope Template — Perimeter Security Fencing

> **Source context:** McFaddin NWR fence NO-GO ([`bids/_NO_GO/2026-05-22-mcfaddin-nwr-fence-140FC126Q0015.md`](../../bids/_NO_GO/2026-05-22-mcfaddin-nwr-fence-140FC126Q0015.md)) + general federal-site-security baseline (FBI, USCG, USACE bases, refuge boundary fencing, water-treatment-plant fencing).
>
> **Scope range:** simple chain-link boundary fence (low-security USFWS refuge) → high-security ornamental + anti-climb perimeter with access control (federal site, water/power critical-infrastructure). Pricing structure scales 5-10x across this range.
>
> **Typical CSI divisions touched:** 01, 02 (clearing, grubbing, minor grading), 03 (concrete footings), 31 (earthwork), 32 31 13 (chain-link fence) / 32 31 19 (ornamental fence) / 32 31 26 (anti-climb mesh fence), 32 31 13.53 (gates) / 32 31 26 (specialty gates), 28 13 (electronic access control where applicable), 26 (power for gate operators + lighting).

## 2. Standard takeoff-item list

### 2.1 Site prep + earthwork

```
SITE PREP
  □ Erosion control (silt fence LF + inlet protection EA + stabilized construction entrance EA)
  □ Utility locate (LS) — 811 ticket; may need private locator on federal site
  □ Survey + stakeout (LF perimeter + corners)
  □ Tree + brush clearing (LF + AC) — typical 8-12' wide corridor for chain-link; 15-20' for security fence
  □ Stump removal (each)
  □ Minor grading (CY) — leveling for fence line; rocky / hilly sites add cost
  □ Disposal of clearing debris (CY)
```

### 2.2 Posts + footings

```
FENCE POSTS
  □ Line posts (per fence type, spaced per spec):
    □ Chain-link: 2-3/8" or 2-7/8" OD galv steel @ 10' OC
    □ Ornamental: 2-1/2" or 3" sq steel @ 6-8' OC
    □ Anti-climb mesh (8'+8'): 4" sq HSS @ 8' OC
  □ Corner / end / pull posts (heavier section — typ 1 size larger than line post):
    □ Chain-link: 4" OD or 3-1/2" OD
    □ Ornamental: 4" sq HSS
    □ Anti-climb: 6" sq HSS
  □ Gate posts (sized for gate weight + width):
    □ Single swing: same as corner
    □ Double swing: heavier (6" OD or 6" sq)
    □ Cantilever slide: very heavy (8" sq or larger, with counterweight calc)
FOOTINGS
  □ Concrete-set (typ — required in non-frost or non-cohesive soils):
    □ Line post: 12"–18" dia × 30"–48" deep
    □ Corner / pull post: 18"–24" dia × 36"–48" deep
    □ Gate post: 24"–30" dia × 48"–60" deep (sized for moment + frost depth)
  □ Driven post (alt — sandy / cohesive soils only):
    □ Line post: 24"–36" deep
    □ Verify per geotech + uplift calc
  □ Concrete (CY total) — typ 3,000 psi
```

### 2.3 Fabric / panels / mesh (per fence type)

```
CHAIN-LINK
  □ Fabric: 9-ga or 11-ga galv OR vinyl-coated (color per spec — typ green or black)
  □ Mesh size: 2" typ; 1" mini-mesh for higher-security
  □ Height: 4', 6', 8', 10' typ
  □ Top rail (LF) — 1-5/8" OD galv
  □ Bottom tension wire (LF) — 7-ga galv coiled spring
  □ Tension bands + bars + ties (per LF)
  □ Top treatment (per LF):
    □ Barbed wire (3-strand standard; 6-strand high-security)
    □ Razor ribbon (LF) — typ on top of barbed wire for max security
    □ Anti-climb mesh extension (8'+8' total height)
    □ None (boundary or aesthetic)
  □ Privacy slats (per LF) — vinyl or aluminum if specified
ORNAMENTAL STEEL
  □ Panel system: 3-rail or 4-rail; pressed-point spear OR flat-top (per spec)
  □ Panel height: 6', 7', 8' typ
  □ Picket spacing: 4" OC typ (federal/security)
  □ Manufacturer: Ameristar (Aegis Plus), Fortress Iron, Master Halco — verify per spec
  □ Color: black powder-coat typ (galv + powder-coat for coastal)
ANTI-CLIMB MESH (high-security)
  □ Welded mesh: 358 mesh (3"H × 0.5"W × 8 ga wire) typ
  □ Height: 8'-12' single OR 8'+8' double (with anti-cut sensor wire between)
  □ Galv + powder-coat
  □ Manufacturer: Betafence (Securifor), Anti-Climb Pro
```

### 2.4 Gates

```
SWING GATES
  □ Single swing (typ 4'-6' wide pedestrian; 12'-16' wide vehicular)
  □ Double swing (typ 16'-30' wide vehicular)
  □ Hardware: hinges (heavy-duty), latch (with padlock provision), drop rod (for double)
  □ Fabric / panel matches fence
CANTILEVER SLIDE GATES (typ for vehicular)
  □ Span: 20'-40' typ
  □ Wheel-less cantilever design (no track in driveway — preferred for snow / debris)
  □ Counterweight section (typ 50% of opening width)
  □ Frame: 2" or 3" sq HSS truss
  □ Operator: chain-drive OR rack-and-pinion electric (FAAC, LiftMaster, DoorKing brands)
PEDESTRIAN GATES
  □ Width: 3'-4'
  □ Self-closing hinge + auto-latch
TURNSTILES (high-security)
  □ Full-height OR waist-high
  □ Manufacturer: Smarter Security, Boon Edam, Alvarado
ACCESS CONTROL (when scope)
  □ Keypad (each gate)
  □ Card reader (each gate)
  □ Intercom + video (each gate)
  □ Loop detector(s) (free-exit, safety-reverse, presence)
  □ Photocell safety beam (each pair)
  □ Battery backup (NEMA 4 enclosure)
  □ Power supply (120V dedicated circuit — coordinate with electrician)
  □ Network connection (cat6 or fiber to head-end if integrated with site security system)
LIGHTING (when scope)
  □ Perimeter pole light (each) — typ 25'-40' mast; LED 150-400W
  □ Fence-mounted light (each) — typ 12'-15' mast; LED 30-100W
  □ Bollard light (each) — at pedestrian gates
```

## 5. Common alternates / VE opportunities

| Item | Base | Alternate | Trade-off |
|---|---|---|---|
| Fence material | Galv chain-link | Vinyl-coated chain-link | Vinyl coating adds 15–25%; better corrosion + aesthetic |
| Top treatment | Barbed wire | Razor ribbon OR anti-climb extension OR none | Razor adds $8–$15/LF; anti-climb adds $25–$50/LF |
| Footings | Concrete-set | Driven post | Driven saves 20–35% but only acceptable in cohesive soil + low uplift |
| Gate operator | Electric | Manual | Manual saves $5K–$9K per gate but slows site access |
| Gate type | Swing | Cantilever slide | Cantilever costs 30–60% more but no road-track + better snow handling |
| Access control | Keypad | Card reader OR card + biometric | Each level adds $2K–$8K per gate |
| Lighting | Pole | Fence-mounted | Fence-mount saves 30–50% but lower lumen output |
| Anti-climb height | 8' single | 8' + 8' double (with sensor) | Double-stack is 80–120% more but required for high-security (FBI/FCI/water) |
| Corner detail | Standard radius | Mitered corner panels (ornamental) | Mitered adds 5–10% but premium aesthetic |

**Critical-path items:**
- **Survey of federal land boundary** (2–6 weeks; long-lead at NWR / refuge sites)
- **Anti-climb mesh production lead** (8–14 weeks for 358 mesh from Betafence / Anti-Climb Pro)
- **Cantilever gate fabrication** (4–8 weeks for custom widths)
- **NEPA / Section 7 (ESA) review** for refuge or wildlife management areas — can add 30–90 days; coordinate with USFWS planner

## 8. Common pitfalls

1. **Boundary survey not budgeted.** Federal land boundaries are often historic + un-surveyed at the actual fence line. Budget $5K–$25K for PLS survey BEFORE fence layout.
2. **Soil class assumed.** Concrete-set vs driven post depends on geotech. Coastal sites (McFaddin) have soft soil + high water table — driven post may fail uplift; concrete-set required.
3. **NEPA / ESA review skipped.** USFWS refuge fences crossing wetlands or wildlife corridors require Section 7 consultation. Adds 30–90 days.
4. **Anti-climb mesh lead time underestimated.** 358 mesh from European suppliers (Betafence, Locinox) commonly 12-week lead; domestic (Anti-Climb Pro) 8-week.
5. **Cantilever gate counterweight calc wrong.** Cantilever gates require counterweight section sized for opening width. Field-modified counterweights routinely fail UL 325 inspection.
6. **UL 325 entrapment-protection compliance missed.** All powered gates require ≥2 means of entrapment protection (loop detector + photoeye + audible warning + reverse-on-contact). Easy to miss in spec.
7. **Frost depth ignored.** Northern projects (above the frost line) require footings below frost (typ 36"-48" north of TX/AZ). Southern coastal projects (Padre Island, Galveston, McFaddin) require uplift calc for hurricane wind.
8. **Existing utility conflicts.** 811 locate is required but DOES NOT find private / abandoned utilities. Federal sites often have un-recorded comms cable, propane lines, irrigation — visual + private locate critical.
9. **Federal wage rates (Davis-Bacon).** Federal site fence projects subject to DBA — wage rates can be 30–60% higher than market. Verify per RFP.
10. **Refuge boundary signage required.** USFWS refuge fences typically require boundary markers + signage at intervals (per refuge regulation). Easily missed in scope.
11. **Tree clearing limits at refuges.** Many refuges restrict clearing during nesting season (typ March-Aug). Verify with refuge biologist BEFORE bid.
