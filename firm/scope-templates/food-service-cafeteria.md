## 1. Scope outline (standard sequencing)

| # | Phase | Trade(s) | Typical duration (single serving line — ~600 SF) | Typical duration (full kitchen — ~3,000 SF) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Site visit + mobilization + temp protection + dust barricades | GC | 2–3 days | 5–7 days |
| 2 | Selective demolition (existing serving line equipment, finishes, MEP) | Demo sub + GC | 3–5 days | 1–2 weeks |
| 3 | MEP rough-in (gas, grease waste, 208V/3-ph, dedicated exhaust, DI water if specified) | MEP subs (parallel) | 1–2 weeks | 2–4 weeks |
| 4 | Hood + exhaust system install (Type I for grease; Type II for steam/heat) | HVAC + sheet-metal | 3–5 days | 1–2 weeks |
| 5 | Fire-suppression install (Ansul R-102 or equivalent over hood) | Fire-suppression sub (state-licensed) | 2–3 days | 3–5 days |
| 6 | Flooring (quarry tile + base OR epoxy resin) | Flooring sub | 3–5 days | 1–2 weeks |
| 7 | Wall cladding (FRP panels OR stainless OR epoxy paint) | Carpenter + paint | 2–3 days | 1 week |
| 8 | Stainless-steel serving line equipment delivery + install (hot wells, cold wells, sneeze guards, tray slides, cash-register counter) | Kitchen-equipment dealer (NSF-certified install) | 3–5 days | 1–2 weeks |
| 9 | Reheat ovens + steam tables + coolers + freezers + ice makers (specialty) | Kitchen-equipment dealer | 2–3 days (parallel) | 1 week |
| 10 | MEP trim (connections to all new equipment) + commissioning | MEP subs | 2–3 days | 3–5 days |
| 11 | Health-department + AHJ inspections (kitchen, fire-suppression, gas, electrical) | AHJ | 1–3 days | 1 week |
| 12 | Punch + final clean + sanitization | GC | 2–3 days | 3–5 days |
| 13 | Closeout (O&M manuals, as-builts, warranty start dates, staff training on new equipment) | GC + equipment dealer | 1 week | 1–2 weeks |
| **Total** | | | **6–10 weeks** | **12–18 weeks** |

**Hard constraint:** food-service renovations on K-12 + university campuses MUST complete before the next academic term starts (typical hard deadline mid-August for fall semester). Schedule the construction window to leave 2-week buffer for inspection + commissioning + staff training before opening day.

## 2. Standard takeoff-item list

### 2.1 Demolition

```
DEMO
  □ Existing stainless-steel hot wells (each) — palletize for resale OR scrap
  □ Existing cold wells / refrigerated displays (each)
  □ Existing sneeze guards + tray slides (each)
  □ Existing cash-register counter (each + LF)
  □ Existing reheat ovens, steam tables (each) — disconnect + remove
  □ Existing hood + ductwork + fire-suppression (LF + each)
  □ Existing flooring (SF) + base (LF)
  □ Existing wall finishes (SF) — FRP panels, ceramic tile, or epoxy paint
  □ Existing ceiling tile + grid (SF)
  □ Existing lighting (each)
  □ Existing exhaust fan + makeup-air unit on roof (each)
  □ Existing gas piping to equipment (LF) — cap at main
  □ Existing grease-waste piping (LF) — cap at trap
  □ Existing electrical to equipment (LF + each circuit)
  □ Haul + disposal — note resale value of stainless equipment can offset disposal cost
```

### 2.2 New equipment (NSF/ANSI 2 + 4 compliant)

```
NEW SERVING LINE EQUIPMENT (typically OFCI — district specifies brands)
  □ Hot wells (each, with specified BTU, # of pans, drop-in vs free-standing) — typ Vollrath, Hatco, Wells
  □ Cold wells (each, with specified pan capacity + refrigeration type) — typ Atlas Metal, Delfield
  □ Sneeze guards (LF — heated, cold, or unheated) — typ Custom Manufactured
  □ Tray slides (LF) — stainless OR solid surface
  □ Cash-register counter + millwork (LF + each)
  □ Reheat ovens (each) — typ Alto-Shaam, Henny Penny
  □ Steam tables (each)
  □ Walk-in cooler OR walk-in freezer (each — if scope) — typ Kolpak, Polar King
  □ Ice maker (each)
  □ Hand sinks at serving line (each — code-required per NSF/ANSI 2)
  □ Sneeze guards (each station)
  □ Specialty: salad bar, pizza station, hot beverage station, ice-cream station (each)
```

### 2.3 MEP

```
PLUMBING
  □ Gas piping from main to each gas-powered unit (LF + connection each)
  □ Grease-waste piping from each sink + dish station to grease trap (LF + connection)
  □ Cold water + hot water rough-in for hand sinks + dish sinks (each)
  □ DI water rough-in if specified (LF)
  □ Floor drains (each) — required at serving line; verify slope per IPC
  □ Grease trap servicing access (each)
ELECTRICAL
  □ 208V/3-phase circuits to each high-voltage unit (each)
  □ Dedicated 120V circuits for refrigeration (each)
  □ GFI receptacles at all wet stations (each)
  □ Emergency shut-off (E-stop) at serving line entry (each)
  □ LED high-bay over serving line (each)
HVAC
  □ Type I hood over grease appliances (LF) — typ Captive-Aire, Greenheck
  □ Type II hood over steam/heat appliances (LF)
  □ Hood exhaust ductwork (LF) — welded stainless per NFPA 96
  □ Roof-curb-mounted exhaust fan (each)
  □ Makeup-air unit (each — typ tempered for cold-climate)
  □ Hood interlock with fire-suppression + makeup-air + gas shutoff
FIRE SUPPRESSION
  □ Wet-chemical (Ansul R-102 or equivalent) over each hood (LF coverage)
  □ Pull station at hood entry (each)
  □ Gas-valve interlock (each — auto-shutoff on hood discharge)
  □ State-licensed fire-suppression contractor required (Texas: TX DSHS license)
```

### 2.4 Finishes

```
FLOORING
  □ Quarry tile + epoxy grout (SF + LF base) — typ Daltile, American Olean; slip-resistant per ANSI A137.1
  □ OR Epoxy resin floor (SF) — typ Stonhard, Dur-A-Flex; integral cove base
  □ Floor sealer at perimeter + equipment legs (LF)
WALLS
  □ FRP panels (SF) — typ Marlite, Crane Composites; chemical + moisture resistant
  □ OR Stainless-steel wainscot (SF) — typ at splash zones
  □ Epoxy paint above wainscot/FRP (SF) — semi-gloss for sanitation
CEILINGS
  □ Washable / cleanable acoustic tile (SF) — typ Armstrong Ceramaguard
  □ OR painted GWB hard-lid (SF) — for grease/steam zones
```

## 5. Common alternates / VE opportunities

| Item | Base | Alternate | Trade-off |
|---|---|---|---|
| Floor type | Quarry tile + epoxy grout | Epoxy resin | Epoxy is 30–40% cheaper but easier to repair; tile is more durable + premium aesthetic |
| Hood type | Type I (grease) | Type II (steam/heat) if all-electric serving line | Type II is 25–40% cheaper but requires all-electric equipment (no gas) |
| Hood capture | Wall-mounted | Center-island | Island hoods are 50–80% more expensive but allow open-kitchen design |
| Serving line config | Linear (long row) | Scramble (offset stations) | Scramble eats more SF but moves more students faster — district decision |
| Reheat oven brand | Alto-Shaam | Henny Penny / Vulcan | Brand spec is often locked by district preference — confirm pre-bid |
| Walk-in cooler/freezer brand | Kolpak | Polar King / Norlake | Same; spec lock common |
| Sneeze guard | Heated (warming light) | Unheated | Unheated is 40–60% cheaper if reheat capacity is sufficient |

**Critical-path dominant item:** kitchen-equipment lead times. NSF-spec hot wells, walk-ins, hoods, and Ansul systems often have **10–18 week lead times**. On a 6–10 week construction window, equipment must be ordered 4–8 weeks before NTP. Coordinate procurement with the kitchen-equipment dealer **at proposal time**, not at NTP.

## 7. Code references

- **NSF/ANSI 2** (Food Equipment) — applies to most serving line equipment
- **NSF/ANSI 4** (Commercial Cooking, Rethermalization, Powered Hot Food Holding) — applies to hot wells + reheat ovens
- **NFPA 96** (Standard for Ventilation Control + Fire Protection of Commercial Cooking Operations) — hood + duct + fire-suppression
- **IMC** (International Mechanical Code) — hood + makeup-air + exhaust
- **IPC** (International Plumbing Code) — grease waste + floor drains + grease trap
- **NEC Article 422** (Appliances) — dedicated circuits + GFI
- **State + local health department** (TX: TX Department of State Health Services + county health) — pre-construction plan review + final inspection
- **K-12 districts:** TEA-related TASB FF series facility design standards may apply for ISD projects

## 8. Common pitfalls

1. **Equipment lead time underestimated.** Walk-in coolers + Ansul systems + custom hoods commonly run 12+ weeks. Order at proposal-assembly time, not at NTP.
2. **Hood-to-makeup-air ratio wrong.** Mechanical code requires makeup-air ≥ 80% of hood exhaust. Missing makeup-air = negative-pressure kitchen + door slams + odor migration. Catch in submittal review.
3. **Fire-suppression contractor not state-licensed.** TX DSHS license required for Ansul install. Sub default falls back to "we're a fire-protection sub" — verify the specific Ansul cert.
4. **Floor sealer + cove base wrong system.** Epoxy resin floors require manufacturer-specified cove + sealer system; mixing brands voids warranty.
5. **Gas line + electrical conflict.** 208V/3-ph circuits and gas piping commonly cross paths in serving lines — coordinate at MEP rough-in, not at trim.
6. **Health-department pre-construction plan review missed.** Most TX counties require plan-review approval BEFORE construction; missing it can void the certificate of occupancy at final.
7. **Resale value of demo equipment ignored.** Used stainless-steel hot wells + walk-ins have resale value ($500–$3,000 each). Negotiate with owner pre-bid on who keeps the resale proceeds; can offset $5K-$15K of demo cost.
8. **Sneeze guard codes confused.** Local health departments vary on sneeze guard requirement (some have moved away post-COVID); verify before pricing.
9. **Staff training not scoped.** New equipment requires 2–4 hours of food-service staff training on operation, cleaning, daily startup/shutdown. Equipment dealer typically includes this — confirm in proposal.
10. **Critical-path overlap with school year start.** K-12 cafeteria projects on a "back-to-school" deadline have ZERO float at the end. Build in 2-week buffer for inspection + commissioning + training.
