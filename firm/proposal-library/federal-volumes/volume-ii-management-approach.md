## 5. Change-management approach

### 5.1 Change identification

Changes are identified through:

- **Constructive change discovery** by BPC during execution (FAR 52.243-4 differing site conditions, defective specifications, owner-directed scope adjustment).
- **`{{AGENCY}}`-directed change** via Contracting Officer change order, modification, or constructive direction.
- **Sub-discovered change** captured by BPC PM and routed to `{{AGENCY}}` per the formal RFI / change protocol.

### 5.2 Change-management protocol

1. **Identify + notify.** Within 5 working days of discovery, BPC PM issues a written notice to the Contracting Officer (FAR 52.243-4 differing site conditions notice within statutory window if applicable).
2. **Document.** Field observations photographed, dated, geo-tagged where useful; impacted drawings / specs / RFIs cross-referenced.
3. **Quantify.** Cost impact built bottom-up using BPC's standard estimating buildup (labor at WD-applicable rates, material at quoted rates with sub-quotes attached, equipment at FEMA / Blue Book or vendor rates, OH + profit + bond + insurance per `{{AGENCY}}` allowable percentage).
4. **Schedule impact.** Time-impact analysis using fragnet inserted into the CPM; criticality assessed against the baseline.
5. **Propose.** Change-order proposal submitted within 10 working days of the change directive (or such other timeline as Section L specifies) per FAR 52.243-4.
6. **Negotiate + execute.** BPC PM negotiates; PIC concurs; CO issues SF 30 modification.
7. **Implement.** Work begins after SF 30 modification execution, except where the CO directs immediate execution under FAR 52.243-4(c) ("Order of the Contracting Officer to make a change") with the price + time impact reserved for post-execution negotiation.

### 5.3 Cost + schedule discipline through change

BPC's policy is that **no work outside the original SOW or executed modifications is performed at risk** — the PM and PIC enforce this with subs as well as with self-perform crew. This protects both BPC and `{{AGENCY}}` from after-the-fact disputes and unallowable cost recovery.

## 7. Contingency planning

### 7.1 Personnel contingency

- **PIC unavailability** (illness, travel): PM has standing delegation to make decisions up to `{{PM_DELEGATION_$}}` change-order value without PIC concurrence; above threshold, decisions hold pending PIC return or written delegation to a designated backup.
- **PM unavailability:** designated backup PM `[USER TO FILL — backup PM name]` mobilizes within 1 working day; all open RFIs / submittals / change orders re-assigned with PM-handover memo to `{{AGENCY}}` COR.
- **Superintendent unavailability:** designated backup Super `[USER TO FILL — backup Super name]` mobilizes the same day; field crew briefed at next 7:00 AM tailgate.
- **SSL unavailability:** no work in progress without SSL coverage; backup SSL `[USER TO FILL]` mobilizes the same day.

### 7.2 Subcontractor contingency

- **Back-up sub identified for each critical-trade scope.** If primary sub is on probation and fails the 5-working-day cure window, BPC executes back-up sub agreement per pre-bid commitment.
- **Sub crew sickness / absenteeism:** sub commits in writing to a crew-replacement clause; failure to maintain crew is grounds for probation.

### 7.3 Material contingency

- **Long-lead item delay:** alternate vendors identified pre-bid for each long-lead item; expedite-freight contingency in the GR budget; re-sequence around the delayed item where the CPM permits without critical-path impact.
- **Buy American compliance trigger** (federal): all iron + steel + construction materials sourced per FAR 52.225-9; documentation collected at procurement; non-compliance triggers immediate vendor change.

### 7.4 Site contingency

- **Adverse weather:** buffer days in the CPM per FAR 52.249-14 (excusable delays); covered staging for outdoor work; re-sequencing per phase plan.
- **Unforeseen site conditions:** FAR 52.236-2 differing site conditions notice within statutory window; cease work in the affected area; immediate RFI to A/E; differing-site-conditions cost recovery via change order if the condition is materially different from the contract documents.
- **`{{AGENCY}}` mission-emergency interruption:** BPC accommodates `{{AGENCY}}`-directed work stoppage per FAR 52.249-14; equitable adjustment per FAR 52.243-4 if duration is material.

## 8. Evaluator's perspective (delete before submission — internal-use callout)

> **What scorers weight heavily on Volume II (best-value tradeoff construction RFP):**
> 1. **Named, credentialed key personnel.** SSEB calls references; checks credentials; verifies SAM exclusion. Skeleton names or "TBD" entries score zero or trigger a non-responsive determination.
> 2. **Allocation realism.** A PM or Super claimed at 100% on three concurrent projects is a red flag; allocation table per role per concurrent project is what the evaluator wants.
> 3. **Substitution discipline.** Explicit FAR 52.215-21 substitution language signals the offeror knows the rule and will follow it.
> 4. **Communications plan with response-time commitments.** Generic "we communicate well" scores low; quantified response-time SLAs score high.
> 5. **Change-management protocol.** Numbered, FAR-cited steps demonstrate the offeror has done this before — and protect both parties from after-the-fact disputes.
> 6. **Escalation matrix.** Clear authority chain prevents field disputes from blowing up at the COR level.
> 7. **Contingency planning.** Personnel + sub + material + site contingencies show the offeror has thought through what can go wrong; particularly important on tradeoff awards where past performance gaps can be partially offset by demonstrated risk discipline.
