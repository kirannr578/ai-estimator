## 2. Three-phase QC inspection process (per scope item)

### Phase 1 — Preparatory inspection (before scope item begins)

Held with the crew foreman, BPC Super, and BPC QC Lead **before any work begins on a given scope item**. Covers:

- All approved submittals + shop drawings on site
- All materials on site, inspected for damage / wrong color / wrong dimension
- All AHAs reviewed with crew
- Workmanship standards reviewed; reference standards (ASTM, ANSI, mfr install instructions) on site
- Mock-up location agreed (if mock-up required)
- Inspection points scheduled (in-wall, in-ceiling, AHJ)
- Owner / AHJ inspection sequence confirmed

Documented on a **Preparatory Phase Inspection (PPI) form**, signed by foreman + Super + QC Lead. Stored in QC log book on site.

### Phase 2 — Initial inspection (after first ~10% of scope item complete)

Held after the **first room, first opening, first run, or first 10% of the scope item** is in place. Covers:

- Verification that installed work matches submittal + drawings
- Verification that workmanship meets standard
- Definition of any deficiency + corrective action
- Acceptance of approach for the balance of the scope item

Documented on an **Initial Phase Inspection (IPI) form**, signed by foreman + Super + QC Lead.

### Phase 3 — Follow-up inspection (daily, during scope item)

QC Lead conducts daily walk-through of in-progress work and documents inspection on the **Daily QC Report**. Any **non-conformance** is logged as an **NCR (Non-Conformance Report)**, tracked in the QC log, and closed with documented corrective action before next workday.

### Phase 4 — Completion inspection

At substantial completion of the scope item, QC Lead conducts a **walk-through inspection** with the Super + foreman. Any open items become **punch list** entries with assigned owner + due date. Punch list closure tracked on the QC log.

**No installation begins without an approved submittal in the QC binder.**

## 5. Materials inspection

All materials inspected at delivery for:

- Compliance with approved submittal (mfr, model, color, dimension, quantity)
- Damage from transit
- Compliance with Buy American (federal projects)
- Compliance with environmental / sustainability spec (LEED, GREENGUARD, low-VOC) if specified

Non-conforming material is **tagged**, segregated, and returned. Installation of non-conforming material is grounds for back-charge and rework at sub cost.

## 7. Subcontractor QC

Every subcontractor agreement includes:

- Reference to this QC Plan + Plan compliance is a condition of payment
- Sub's responsibility to participate in Preparatory + Initial inspections for their scope
- Sub's responsibility for their own QC procedure (warranties, factory-trained installers, ASTM compliance)
- BPC's right to back-charge for rework caused by sub non-conformance
