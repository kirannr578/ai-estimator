## 1. Safety philosophy

Blue Print Constructs operates under the principle that **all incidents are preventable**. We commit to the safety of every worker, the building occupants of `{{AGENCY}}`, and all visitors, vendors, and AHJ inspectors who interact with the project site for the duration of the Work.

## 2. Site-specific hazards + controls

| Hazard | Specific to this project | Controls |
|---|---|---|
| **Active-occupancy (building remains open)** | `{{BUILDING_OCCUPANCY_DESCRIPTION}}` | Dust-tight barricades at scope perimeter; HEPA negative-air for any demo; separate trade entry / exit route from occupant route; daily clean before occupant arrival; weekly walk with `{{AGENCY}}` EHS |
| **Fall protection** | Ceiling work, roof work, scaffolding | 100% fall protection ≥ 6'; harness inspection at start of every shift; rescue plan posted; ladder inspection log |
| **Hot work (cutting / welding / grinding)** | `{{HOT_WORK_LOCATIONS}}` | Hot Work Permit issued by SSL before each instance; fire-watch posted during + 30 min after; extinguisher at point of work; combustibles removed or shielded; smoke / fire alarm coordination with `{{AGENCY}}` Facilities |
| **Lockout / tagout (electrical, mechanical, plumbing)** | All work involving energized systems | LOTO procedure per `{{AGENCY}}` energy-control program; LOTO permit pre-job; coordinated with `{{AGENCY}}` Facilities; verified zero-energy before work |
| **Electrical** | New circuits, panel work | NFPA 70E PPE per task; arc-flash boundary respected; energized electrical work permit if > 50V live |
| **Lab-specific (if lab)** | Existing lab chemicals, fume hoods, eyewash, gas drops | Pre-construction lab decommissioning verified by `{{AGENCY}}` EHS; lab gas shut off + drain-down verified; no hot work until verified clear of flammables; SDS for any chemical brought to site |
| **Confined space (if applicable)** | `{{CONFINED_SPACE_LOCATIONS}}` | Permit-required confined space program; gas monitoring; attendant; rescue plan |
| **Scaffolding / lift** | `{{LIFT_REQUIREMENTS}}` | Erected / inspected by competent person; daily inspection tag; user-operator certifications |
| **Materials handling** | Heavy materials (drywall, casework, lockers, doors) | Mechanical handling preferred over manual; team-lift > 50 lbs; back-belts NOT a substitute for proper technique |
| **Slip / trip / fall (housekeeping)** | All project areas | Daily site cleanup; no extension cords across pedestrian paths; clear egress always |

## 5. Coordination with `{{AGENCY}}` EHS / Safety office

- Pre-construction meeting with `{{AGENCY}}` EHS within 5 days of NTP
- Project-specific EHS sign-on / orientation for every worker before first day on site
- Daily access coordination per `{{AGENCY}}` protocol (key card, escort, sign-in)
- All AHAs shared with `{{AGENCY}}` EHS in advance of the high-hazard task
- Any incident (recordable, near-miss, first-aid, property damage, fire alarm, evacuation) reported to `{{AGENCY}}` EHS within 1 hour

## 6. Incident response

- Immediate medical attention for any injury — 911 if life-threatening; nearest urgent-care otherwise
- All injuries (recordable + first-aid) logged on OSHA 300 within 7 days
- Serious incidents (fatality, in-patient hospitalization, amputation, loss of an eye) reported to OSHA within 8 hours (fatality) / 24 hours (others) per 29 CFR 1904.39
- Root-cause investigation within 24 hours; corrective actions tracked to closure
- Lessons-learned shared at next firm-wide safety stand-down
