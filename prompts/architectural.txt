UNIT SELECTION RULES (read carefully — this drives downstream pricing):

Emit `unit: "LS"` (lump sum) ONLY when the line item is genuinely a
lump-sum scope with no measurable quantity (e.g. "demolition of existing
wall — included", "general clean-up"). Lump-sum lines collide with the
unit-mismatch suppressor in the cost-DB and will zero out if the cost
database expects a measurable unit — so pick the right dimensional unit
whenever one applies.

Default dimensional units by item type:

  * 2x and dimensional lumber (studs, joists, rafters, plates, blocking,
    beams, posts) → `LF` (linear feet) or `BF` (board feet). Prefer `LF`
    for linear callouts; use `BF` only when the drawing quotes board-foot
    quantities directly.
  * Sheathing, subfloor, plywood, OSB, gypsum board, deck boards →
    `SF` (square feet).
  * Hardware, framing connectors, joist hangers, hold-downs, anchors,
    fasteners-by-the-bag, individual studs called out as count → `EA`.
  * Doors, windows, plumbing fixtures, electrical fixtures, appliances,
    casework units → `EA`.
  * Floor finishes, wall finishes, painted areas, roofing, siding → `SF`.
  * Concrete, fill, excavation → `CY` (cubic yards).
  * Structural steel by weight → `TON`.

For wood-framing items in CSI 06 10 00 specifically: use `LF` for studs,
plates, joists, rafters, beams and posts; `BF` only if a board-foot
quantity is shown on the drawing; `SF` for sheathing and deck boards;
`EA` for hangers, connectors, hold-downs and individual posts called out
by count. Do NOT default to `LS` for any framing item that can be
measured.

Guidance for floor plans:
  - For each room you can identify, populate `rooms` with the best area you
    can read from the drawing or schedule, and any finish notes.
  - Add `raw_takeoffs` for things you can quantify directly:
      * Total interior partition length (LF) -> CSI 09 22 16
      * Total drywall area both sides (SF)   -> CSI 09 29 00
      * Door count (EA, by type)             -> CSI 08 11 13 / 08 14 16
      * Window count or area                 -> CSI 08 50 00 / 08 51 13
      * Floor finish areas (SF) by material  -> CSI 09 65 19 / 09 68 13
      * Ceiling areas by material            -> CSI 09 51 13 / 09 29 00
      * Painted wall area (SF)               -> CSI 09 91 23

Guidance for elevations / sections / details:
  - Skip the rooms array; focus on `raw_takeoffs` for finishes (siding,
    roofing, masonry, glazing, insulation thickness etc.) and any window /
    door schedules visible.

Be conservative. A confidence of 0.9 means "directly read from the drawing".
0.5 means "estimated from scale or context". Below 0.4 means "rough guess".
